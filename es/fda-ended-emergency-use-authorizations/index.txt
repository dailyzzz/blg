<h2 id="the-ancient-pattern">The Ancient Pattern</h2>
<blockquote>
<p>"And the light of a candle shall shine no more at all in thee; and the voice of the bridegroom and of the bride shall be heard no more at all in thee: for thy merchants were the great men of the earth; for by thy sorceries were all nations deceived." — Revelation 18:23</p>
</blockquote>
<p>The Greek word translated as "sorceries" in Revelation 18:23 is <em>pharmakeia</em> — the root of our modern word "pharmaceutical." Scripture prophesied that in the last days, the nations of the earth would be deceived by pharmaceutical sorcery. Not by crude potions mixed in cauldrons, but by the polished instruments of an industry cloaked in the language of science and the authority of government approval.</p>
<p>As the Confliction track <a href="https://confliction.zzzdaily.com/tracks/es/07-The_Serpents_Curse.mp4">The Serpent's Curse</a> reveals, the curse that began in Eden has threaded through every generation, manifesting in systems that promise healing while delivering bondage. The Emergency Use Authorization was one such instrument — a legal mechanism that bypassed every safety standard designed to protect human life, all under the banner of urgency and necessity.</p>
<p>Now the FDA has ended these authorizations. But the question that demands an answer is not whether they ended — it is why they existed in the first place, what they enabled, and whether the damage they unleashed can ever be undone.</p>

<h2 id="the-emergency-use-authorization-a-legal-weapon">The Emergency Use Authorization: A Legal Weapon</h2>
<p>The Emergency Use Authorization (EUA) is codified under Section 564 of the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act. Under normal circumstances, any pharmaceutical product must undergo years of rigorous clinical trials — Phase I, Phase II, Phase III — before the FDA will consider granting approval. These trials test for safety, efficacy, dosing, side effects, long-term consequences, and interactions with other drugs. The process typically takes 10 to 15 years and exists for a single reason: to protect human beings from being used as unwitting test subjects.</p>
<p>The EUA obliterates this process. When the Secretary of Health and Human Services declares a public health emergency, the FDA Commissioner gains the authority to authorize unapproved medical products for emergency use. The legal threshold is breathtakingly low: the product must "may be effective" — not proven effective, not demonstrated to be safe, merely plausibly useful based on whatever data the manufacturer provides.</p>
<p>During the COVID-19 pandemic, this mechanism was invoked to authorize the Pfizer-BioNTech, Moderna, and Johnson &#x26; Johnson COVID-19 injections. These products had not completed standard Phase III clinical trials. Pfizer's own clinical trial was designed to last until 2023, yet the EUA was granted in December 2020 — three years before the trial would conclude. The control group was eliminated within months when unblinded participants in the placebo arm were offered the injection, destroying the possibility of long-term comparative safety data.</p>
<p>Karen Kingston, biotech analyst and former Pfizer consultant, has documented extensively in The Kingston Report (karenkingston.substack.com) that the EUA framework was not merely a shortcut — it was a legal shield. Under the PREP Act (Public Readiness and Emergency Preparedness Act), products authorized under EUA are exempt from liability. Manufacturers cannot be sued for injuries or deaths caused by their products. Healthcare providers who administer the products are similarly shielded. The only legal remedy available to injured individuals is the Countermeasures Injury Compensation Program (CICP), which has historically denied the vast majority of claims and offers no right to legal representation, no discovery process, and no judicial review.</p>
<p>Kingston has stated plainly: "The EUA was never about saving lives. It was about deploying a product without accountability. The entire legal architecture was designed to ensure that no one — not the manufacturer, not the government, not the administering physician — could ever be held responsible for what happened."</p>
<h3 id="the-scale-of-deployment">The Scale of Deployment</h3>
<p>Under the EUA framework, more than 670 million doses of COVID-19 injections were administered in the United States alone. Globally, the number exceeds 13 billion. These injections were administered to children as young as six months old. They were mandated by employers, universities, and branches of the U.S. military. Service members who refused were court-martialed. Healthcare workers who declined were terminated. Students who resisted were expelled. The unvaccinated were barred from restaurants, gyms, concerts, and houses of worship.</p>
<p>All of this was made possible by a single legal instrument: the Emergency Use Authorization.</p>
<h3 id="the-products-themselves">The Products Themselves</h3>
<p>Karen Kingston's research has further documented that the COVID-19 mRNA injections were not traditional vaccines by any prior definition. They did not contain an attenuated or inactivated virus. They did not stimulate the immune system in the manner of conventional vaccines. Instead, they delivered synthetic messenger RNA wrapped in lipid nanoparticles — a technology that had never before been authorized for use in humans at scale.</p>
<p>The lipid nanoparticles were designed to evade the body's immune defenses and deliver their payload into human cells, instructing those cells to manufacture the SARS-CoV-2 spike protein. The body's immune system would then mount a response against its own cells — a mechanism that critics, including Kingston, have described as functionally autoimmune.</p>
<p>Pfizer's own biodistribution study, obtained through a Japanese regulatory FOIA request, revealed that the lipid nanoparticles did not remain at the injection site as publicly claimed. They distributed throughout the body, concentrating in the liver, spleen, adrenal glands, ovaries, and bone marrow. This data was available to regulators before the EUA was granted. It was not disclosed to the public.</p>

<h2 id="the-announcement-eua-termination">The Announcement: EUA Termination</h2>
<p>The FDA's announcement terminating all COVID-19 Emergency Use Authorizations represents an official acknowledgment that the declared public health emergency no longer exists and that the legal basis for bypassing standard approval processes has expired. The implications are far-reaching.</p>
<p>First, any COVID-19 injection product that relied solely on EUA status — and did not receive full FDA approval (Biologics License Application, or BLA) — can no longer be legally distributed in the United States. This distinction matters because the fully approved product (Comirnaty, in the case of Pfizer) was never widely distributed in the U.S. market. The vast majority of doses administered were the EUA-authorized version, which is legally distinct from the BLA-approved product.</p>
<p>Second, the termination of the EUA removes the PREP Act liability shield for any future administration of these products under emergency authorization. While existing liability protections for doses already administered remain a subject of legal debate, the elimination of the EUA framework going forward means that any future COVID-19 injection program would need to proceed under standard FDA approval — with full liability exposure for manufacturers.</p>
<p>Third, the EUA termination undercuts the legal foundation for any future COVID-19 vaccine mandates. Mandates were legally justified on the basis of the emergency authorization. Without the EUA, there is no emergency authorization, and without emergency authorization, the legal basis for compulsory vaccination dissolves.</p>
<h3 id="what-the-fda-did-not-say">What the FDA Did Not Say</h3>
<p>The FDA's announcement was careful in its language. It did not acknowledge harm. It did not reference the hundreds of thousands of adverse event reports filed with VAERS (the Vaccine Adverse Event Reporting System). It did not address the myocarditis signal in young males. It did not reference the excess mortality data that has confounded actuaries and insurance executives since 2021. It did not acknowledge the fertility concerns, the neurological injuries, the cardiac events, or the cancer signals that have been documented by researchers worldwide.</p>
<p>The FDA ended the EUA the way it began it — silently, bureaucratically, without accountability.</p>

<h2 id="the-prep-act-the-shield-that-remains">The PREP Act: The Shield That Remains</h2>
<p>Even with the EUA terminated, the PREP Act liability shield remains the most significant legal obstacle to justice for those injured by the COVID-19 injections. The PREP Act, signed into law in 2005 and invoked by HHS Secretary Alex Azar on February 4, 2020 — weeks before any COVID-19 case was confirmed in the United States — provides blanket immunity to manufacturers, distributors, program planners, and qualified persons involved in the administration of covered countermeasures.</p>
<p>Karen Kingston has identified the PREP Act as the linchpin of the entire operation. In multiple reports published on The Kingston Report, she has detailed how the PREP Act was specifically designed to create a legal environment in which experimental products could be deployed to the entire population without any possibility of legal recourse. The Act was not a response to COVID-19 — it was written 15 years before COVID-19 and was sitting on the shelf, ready to be activated the moment a public health emergency was declared.</p>
<p>Kingston has noted: "The PREP Act is not a shield — it is a weapon. It was designed to ensure that pharmaceutical companies could inject experimental products into billions of people without facing a single lawsuit. That is not public health. That is military-grade legal warfare against civilians."</p>
<p>The effort by over 100,000 doctors and medical professionals to vacate PREP Act immunity — documented in our companion article — represents the most significant legal challenge to this framework since its inception.</p>
