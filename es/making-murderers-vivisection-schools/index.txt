<h2 id="the-history-of-school-dissection">The History of School Dissection</h2>
<h3 id="how-it-began">How It Began</h3>
<p>Animal dissection entered American schools in the early 20th century as part of the broader restructuring of public education along industrial lines. The same era that produced the Flexner Report (which restructured medical education) also produced the transformation of public schooling from classical education to industrial training.</p>
<p>The General Education Board — founded by John D. The Board funded the restructuring of American education, replacing classical learning with vocational training, critical thinking with rote obedience, and moral education with "scientific" materialism.</p>
<p>Animal dissection was introduced as part of this materialist agenda. By teaching children to view living creatures as mechanisms — assemblies of organs, tissues, and systems to be examined and catalogued — the school system replaced the biblical understanding of creation (that animals are living beings placed under human stewardship) with the Darwinian-materialist view (that animals are biological machines produced by random mutation and natural selection).</p>
