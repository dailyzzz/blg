15:T4aa8,<h1 id="is-it-a-crime-federal-government-assisted-pfizer-in-data-fraud">Is It a Crime? Federal Government Assisted Pfizer in Data Fraud</h1>
<h2 id="the-ancient-pattern">The Ancient Pattern</h2>
<blockquote>
<p>"A false balance is abomination to the LORD: but a just weight is his delight." — Proverbs 11:1</p>
</blockquote>
<p>In ancient Israel, merchants were commanded to use honest weights and measures. A false balance — a scale deliberately calibrated to deceive — was not merely fraud. It was an abomination, a word reserved in Scripture for acts that violate the fundamental moral order God established for human society. The merchant who sold a pound of grain using a rigged scale did not merely cheat his customer — he corrupted the system of trust upon which all commerce depends.</p>
<p>As the Confliction track <a href="https://confliction.zzzdaily.com/tracks/es/59-The_Deceivers_Lies.mp4">The Deceivers' Lies</a> reveals, the most devastating deceptions are not those told by strangers in the night but by authorities in the light of day — institutions clothed in the language of science, credentialed by government, and trusted by a population that has been trained since childhood to defer to expert consensus.</p>
<p>Pfizer's clinical trial data was the scale upon which the health of billions was weighed. The federal government held that scale. And the evidence now emerging suggests that both the company and the government knew the scale was false.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="the-pfizer-clinical-trial-what-was-promised">The Pfizer Clinical Trial: What Was Promised</h2>
<p>Pfizer's COVID-19 vaccine clinical trial — formally designated C4591001 — was presented to the American people as the gold standard of pharmaceutical science. The trial enrolled approximately 44,000 participants, randomized into vaccine and placebo groups, and was designed to measure the vaccine's efficacy and safety over a two-year period extending to 2023.</p>
<p>The results announced in November 2020 claimed 95% efficacy in preventing symptomatic COVID-19 infection. These numbers were broadcast across every media platform on Earth. They formed the basis for the FDA's Emergency Use Authorization granted on December 11, 2020. They were cited by employers, universities, and government agencies as justification for mandating the injection. They were the foundation upon which the entire global vaccination campaign was built.</p>
<p>But the trial was not what it appeared to be.</p>
<h3 id="the-ventavia-whistleblower">The Ventavia Whistleblower</h3>
<p>In September 2021, Brook Jackson, a regional director at Ventavia Research Group — one of Pfizer's clinical trial subcontractors — filed a complaint with the FDA alleging systemic data integrity problems at three Ventavia trial sites in Texas. Jackson's allegations included:</p>
<ul>
<li>Falsification of data on case report forms</li>
<li>Failure to follow up on adverse events reported by trial participants</li>
<li>Unblinding of trial participants (revealing who received the vaccine vs. placebo)</li>
<li>Inadequate training of vaccine administrators</li>
<li>Failure to maintain proper cold chain storage for the vaccine</li>
<li>Retaliation against employees who raised concerns about data quality</li>
</ul>
<p>Jackson was fired from Ventavia within hours of raising these concerns internally. She subsequently filed a False Claims Act lawsuit against Pfizer in January 2021.</p>
<p>The FDA's response to Jackson's complaint was documented through FOIA requests: the agency did not inspect the Ventavia trial sites for months after receiving her report, and when an inspection was eventually conducted, it was limited in scope and resulted in no enforcement action. The FDA concluded that the data integrity problems at Ventavia did not affect the overall trial results — a conclusion reached without conducting the comprehensive audit that would be necessary to make such a determination.</p>
<p>Karen Kingston has analyzed the Ventavia situation extensively in The Kingston Report (karenkingston.substack.com), noting that the FDA's failure to act on Jackson's complaint was not merely negligent — it was structurally necessary. "If the FDA had conducted a proper investigation and found that Ventavia's data was compromised, it would have been forced to revoke the EUA," Kingston has written. "The entire global vaccination campaign would have been called into question. The political, economic, and institutional consequences would have been catastrophic. The FDA chose to preserve the narrative rather than protect the public."</p>
<h3 id="the-elimination-of-the-control-group">The Elimination of the Control Group</h3>
<p>Perhaps the most consequential decision in the Pfizer trial was the elimination of the placebo control group. Within months of the EUA being granted, Pfizer unblinded the trial and offered the vaccine to participants who had received the placebo. Approximately 93% of placebo recipients crossed over to the vaccine arm.</p>
<p>This decision destroyed the possibility of long-term comparative safety data. Without an intact control group, it became impossible to determine whether health outcomes in vaccinated participants — including deaths, serious adverse events, and chronic conditions — occurred at rates higher than would be expected in an unvaccinated population.</p>
<p>The elimination of the control group was not a scientific decision. It was a political one. Pfizer argued that it would be "unethical" to withhold the vaccine from placebo participants once the EUA was granted. But the trial was designed to run until 2023 precisely because long-term safety data requires long-term observation. By eliminating the control group less than six months into a two-year trial, Pfizer ensured that the long-term safety questions could never be answered using trial data.</p>
<p>Kingston has noted: "The destruction of the control group was deliberate. It was the single most important decision Pfizer made after the EUA, because it guaranteed that no one could ever use the trial data to prove the vaccines caused harm. Without a control group, there is no comparison. Without comparison, there is no proof. Without proof, there is no liability."</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="the-federal-governments-role">The Federal Government's Role</h2>
<p>The federal government did not merely fail to prevent Pfizer's data irregularities — evidence suggests it actively facilitated them.</p>
<h3 id="operation-warp-speed">Operation Warp Speed</h3>
<p>The COVID-19 vaccines were developed under Operation Warp Speed (OWS), a public-private partnership between the U.S. Department of Defense, HHS, and pharmaceutical companies. Under OWS, the federal government committed over $18 billion in taxpayer money to vaccine development, manufacturing, and distribution before any safety data existed.</p>
<p>The contracts governing this relationship were executed under Other Transaction Authority (OTA) — military procurement agreements that exempt the products from standard pharmaceutical regulations, including the Federal Acquisition Regulation and its quality assurance requirements. Kingston has documented that under OTA contracts, the COVID-19 injections were classified as "prototype" countermeasures, not pharmaceutical products — a legal distinction that removed them from the FDA's standard regulatory oversight.</p>
<p>"The federal government did not purchase vaccines from Pfizer," Kingston has stated. "It contracted for the development and delivery of military countermeasures under defense procurement authority. The FDA's role was ceremonial — the products were never subject to standard pharmaceutical regulation because they were never legally classified as pharmaceutical products."</p>
<h3 id="the-75-year-foia-delay">The 75-Year FOIA Delay</h3>
<p>When a group of scientists and medical professionals (Public Health and Medical Professionals for Transparency) filed a FOIA request for the complete Pfizer clinical trial data that the FDA used to authorize the vaccine, the FDA asked a federal court for permission to release the documents over a 75-year period — approximately 500 pages per month.</p>
<p>The request was extraordinary. The FDA was asking to keep the data upon which the most consequential public health decision in modern history was based hidden from the public until the year 2096. Every person who received the vaccine, every person who administered it, every person who mandated it — all would be dead before the full data was available.</p>
<p>A federal judge rejected the FDA's request and ordered the agency to produce the documents on an accelerated timeline. The documents that have been released since then have revealed previously undisclosed adverse events, discrepancies between the data Pfizer presented publicly and the data it submitted to regulators, and evidence that both Pfizer and the FDA were aware of safety signals that were not communicated to the public.</p>
<h3 id="the-suppression-of-early-treatment">The Suppression of Early Treatment</h3>
<p>The federal government's assistance to Pfizer extended beyond data management to market protection. Under FDA regulations, an Emergency Use Authorization can only be granted when "there are no adequate, approved, and available alternatives." If an approved treatment for COVID-19 existed, the EUA for the vaccines could not have been issued.</p>
<p>This created a direct institutional incentive to suppress evidence that early treatments — including ivermectin, hydroxychloroquine, and other repurposed drugs — were effective against COVID-19. The FDA issued public warnings against ivermectin use, characterizing it as a veterinary drug despite decades of human use and a Nobel Prize in Medicine for its developers. The agency posted a now-infamous social media message: "You are not a horse. You are not a cow. Seriously, y'all. Stop it."</p>
<p>Physicians who prescribed early treatments were investigated by state medical boards, stripped of their licenses, and subjected to professional sanctions. Pharmacies refused to fill prescriptions. Hospitals prohibited the administration of treatments that attending physicians had ordered.</p>
<p>Kingston has documented that this suppression was not incidental — it was essential to the legal framework. "The EUA required that no adequate alternative existed. The government ensured that no adequate alternative existed — not by proving these treatments didn't work, but by preventing doctors from using them and preventing researchers from studying them. The absence of approved alternatives was manufactured, not discovered."</p>

<h2 id="the-criminal-question">The Criminal Question</h2>
<p>The title of this article asks whether the government's assistance constitutes a crime. The answer depends on the legal framework applied.</p>
<p>Under the False Claims Act (31 U.S.C. Section 3729-3733), any person who knowingly submits false claims to the federal government — or causes false claims to be submitted — is liable for treble damages and penalties. Brook Jackson's lawsuit alleges that Pfizer submitted false clinical trial data to the FDA in connection with contracts funded by taxpayer money, constituting a false claim.</p>
<p>Under federal fraud statutes (18 U.S.C. Section 1001), making materially false statements to the federal government is a criminal offense carrying up to five years in prison. If Pfizer's clinical trial data contained knowing falsehoods, and if those falsehoods were material to the FDA's authorization decision, the submission of that data constituted a federal crime.</p>
<p>But the more difficult legal question involves the government's own conduct. If federal officials knew about data irregularities and chose not to act — or actively suppressed investigation of those irregularities — their conduct potentially constitutes conspiracy to commit fraud (18 U.S.C. Section 371), obstruction of justice (18 U.S.C. Section 1505), and misprision of felony (18 U.S.C. Section 4).</p>
