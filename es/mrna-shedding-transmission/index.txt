15:T4648,<h1 id="mrna-shedding-and-transmission-the-evidence-they-tried-to-hide">mRNA Shedding and Transmission: The Evidence They Tried to Hide</h1>
<h2 id="the-ancient-pattern">The Ancient Pattern</h2>
<blockquote>
<p>"A little leaven leaveneth the whole lump." — Galatians 5:9</p>
</blockquote>
<p>The apostle Paul used leaven as a metaphor for corruption that spreads invisibly through a body — whether a body of believers or a body of flesh. A small amount of leaven, worked into dough, transforms the entire mass. It does not remain contained. It does not stay where it was placed. It permeates, it spreads, it changes everything it contacts.</p>
<p>The mRNA vaccines were presented to the public as a localized intervention — an injection into the deltoid muscle that would remain at the injection site, produce spike protein locally, stimulate an immune response, and degrade. This was the promise. The reality, documented in Pfizer's own trial protocols and biodistribution data, tells a very different story. The lipid nanoparticles did not stay at the injection site. The spike protein was not confined to the deltoid muscle. And the possibility that vaccinated individuals could transmit vaccine components to unvaccinated people was not a conspiracy theory — it was a scenario that Pfizer's own clinical trial protocol explicitly anticipated and planned for.</p>
<p>As the Confliction album track <a href="https://confliction.zzzdaily.com/tracks/es/07-The_Serpent&#x27;s_Curse.mp4">The Serpent's Curse</a> reminds us, the curse that entered through the serpent did not remain with the serpent. It spread to the ground, to the woman, to the man, to all creation. When something corrupting is introduced, it does not stay contained. This is the ancient pattern — and it is the modern reality of spike protein shedding.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="pfizer-protocol-c4591001-the-smoking-gun">Pfizer Protocol C4591001: The Smoking Gun</h2>
<p>The most significant piece of evidence regarding mRNA shedding is not a study conducted by independent researchers. It is Pfizer's own clinical trial protocol — document C4591001 — which governed the Phase I/II/III clinical trials for the BNT162b2 vaccine (Comirnaty).</p>
<p>Section 8.3.5 of the protocol, titled "Exposure During Pregnancy or Breastfeeding, and Occupational Exposure," contains extraordinary provisions for what Pfizer termed "Environmental Exposure to the Study Intervention." The protocol defined this environmental exposure as occurring "by inhalation or skin contact" with a study participant.</p>
<p>The protocol specified that if a trial investigator became aware that a female partner of a male trial participant had become exposed to the study intervention "by inhalation or skin contact," this exposure event was to be reported to Pfizer Safety within 24 hours. The same reporting requirement applied if a female trial participant exposed a male partner, who then exposed another female partner. The protocol created a chain-of-transmission reporting framework that extended multiple links from the original vaccinated individual.</p>
<p>This is remarkable. Pfizer's own protocol anticipated that the vaccine could be transmitted from vaccinated individuals to unvaccinated individuals through inhalation and skin contact. The protocol did not speculate about what biological mechanism might produce such transmission — but it explicitly created a safety reporting framework to track it. Companies do not create adverse event reporting systems for phenomena they consider impossible.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="biodistribution-data-where-the-lipid-nanoparticles-actually-went">Biodistribution Data: Where the Lipid Nanoparticles Actually Went</h2>
<p>The assertion that the mRNA vaccine remained at the injection site was contradicted by Pfizer's own biodistribution study, conducted in Japan using the Wistar Han rat model. This study, obtained through Freedom of Information requests by Japanese researchers, tracked the distribution of the lipid nanoparticles (LNPs) used to deliver the mRNA payload throughout the body over 48 hours following intramuscular injection.</p>
<p>The results were unambiguous: the lipid nanoparticles did not remain at the injection site. They were detected in the liver, spleen, adrenal glands, ovaries, bone marrow, and other organs within hours of injection. The concentration in the ovaries was particularly notable — it continued to increase throughout the 48-hour monitoring period, suggesting ongoing accumulation rather than transient passage.</p>
<p>This biodistribution data directly contradicted the public assurances given by health authorities, who repeatedly stated that the vaccine remained at the injection site and that the spike protein produced by the mRNA was confined to the local tissue surrounding the injection. The data showed that the delivery vehicle — the lipid nanoparticle — traveled throughout the body, carrying its mRNA payload to organs far from the injection site. This means that spike protein could be produced in the liver, the spleen, the ovaries, and other organs — not just in the deltoid muscle.</p>
<p>If the lipid nanoparticles and their mRNA cargo can travel throughout the body of the vaccinated individual, the question of whether those components — or the spike protein they produce — can be transmitted to other individuals through bodily fluids, exhaled breath, or skin contact becomes not a matter of speculation but a matter of urgent investigation.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="spike-protein-in-breast-milk-documented-transmission">Spike Protein in Breast Milk: Documented Transmission</h2>
<p>In September 2022, a study published in <em>JAMA Pediatrics</em> by Hanna et al. reported the detection of COVID-19 vaccine mRNA in the breast milk of lactating women who had received mRNA vaccines. The study detected mRNA from the BNT162b2 and mRNA-1273 vaccines in breast milk samples collected within 48 hours of vaccination.</p>
<p>This finding confirmed what many nursing mothers had reported anecdotally: that the vaccine components did not remain localized and could be transmitted to infants through breastfeeding. The CDC had previously assured nursing mothers that vaccination was safe during breastfeeding and that vaccine components would not pass into breast milk. The JAMA Pediatrics study demonstrated that this assurance was incorrect.</p>
<p>The detection of vaccine mRNA in breast milk raised immediate questions about the potential effects on nursing infants, whose immune systems and developing organs are fundamentally different from those of adults. No studies had been conducted on the effects of mRNA vaccine components on neonates prior to the recommendation of vaccination for breastfeeding mothers. The precautionary principle — the foundational ethical principle that uncertainty about safety should lead to caution rather than assumption of safety — was abandoned in favor of blanket assurances.</p>

<h2 id="reports-from-the-unvaccinated-the-anecdotal-evidence">Reports from the Unvaccinated: The Anecdotal Evidence</h2>
<p>While the scientific literature on shedding has been slow to develop — in part because the research infrastructure has been actively hostile to investigating the phenomenon — the anecdotal evidence has been voluminous and remarkably consistent.</p>
<p>Thousands of unvaccinated individuals, particularly women, have reported experiencing unusual symptoms after close physical contact with recently vaccinated individuals. The most commonly reported symptoms include: menstrual irregularities (abnormal bleeding, unusually heavy periods, unexpected resumption of menstruation in post-menopausal women), unusual bruising, headaches, fatigue, and nosebleeds.</p>
<p>The menstrual symptoms are particularly significant because they suggest a biological mechanism. The spike protein produced by the mRNA vaccines has been shown to bind to ACE2 receptors, which are expressed in the uterine lining and ovarian tissue. If spike protein is being transmitted from vaccinated to unvaccinated individuals — through exhaled aerosols, skin contact, or other mechanisms — and that spike protein reaches the reproductive tissues of the unvaccinated recipient, menstrual disruption would be an expected physiological response.</p>
<p>A formal investigation into these reports was not conducted by the CDC, FDA, or any other regulatory agency. The reports were dismissed as anecdotal, psychosomatic, or coincidental. No large-scale epidemiological study was funded to investigate whether proximity to vaccinated individuals was associated with menstrual disruption in unvaccinated women. The absence of evidence was treated as evidence of absence — a fundamental scientific error that served institutional interests rather than public health.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="exosomes-and-extracellular-vesicles-the-biological-mechanism">Exosomes and Extracellular Vesicles: The Biological Mechanism</h2>
<p>The biological mechanism by which vaccinated individuals could transmit spike protein to unvaccinated individuals is not mysterious. It is well-established biology. The human body routinely sheds proteins, nucleic acids, and other biological molecules through several pathways, including exosomes and extracellular vesicles.</p>
<p>Exosomes are small vesicles (30-150 nanometers in diameter) secreted by virtually every cell type in the body. They carry proteins, lipids, mRNA, and microRNA, and they play critical roles in cell-to-cell communication. Exosomes are found in all bodily fluids, including blood, saliva, breast milk, urine, sweat, and exhaled breath.</p>
<p>When a cell is producing spike protein — whether due to natural SARS-CoV-2 infection or mRNA vaccination — that spike protein can be packaged into exosomes and released into the surrounding tissue and bodily fluids. These exosomes can then be transmitted to other individuals through normal human contact: breathing shared air, skin contact, kissing, sexual contact, and breastfeeding.</p>
<p>A 2022 study by Bansal et al. published in <em>The Journal of Immunology</em> detected spike protein on the surface of exosomes in the blood of vaccinated individuals for up to four months after vaccination. This is a critical finding: if spike protein is present on circulating exosomes for months after vaccination, then the window during which transmission to unvaccinated individuals could occur is not limited to the first few days after injection but extends for months.</p>

<h2 id="self-amplifying-mrna-the-shedding-concern-amplified">Self-Amplifying mRNA: The Shedding Concern Amplified</h2>
<p>The next generation of mRNA vaccines — self-amplifying mRNA (sa-mRNA) — raises the shedding concern to an entirely new level. Unlike conventional mRNA vaccines, which deliver a fixed quantity of mRNA that degrades over time, self-amplifying mRNA vaccines include a replicase gene that enables the mRNA to copy itself within the host cell, continuously producing more mRNA and more spike protein.</p>
<p>Japan approved the world's first self-amplifying mRNA vaccine, ARCT-154 (Kostaive), manufactured by CSL/Arcturus Therapeutics, in November 2023. The approval generated significant controversy, with Japanese physicians and scientists raising concerns about the potential for prolonged and enhanced shedding from individuals vaccinated with self-amplifying products.</p>
<p>If conventional mRNA vaccines can produce spike protein that is shed through exosomes for months after vaccination, a self-amplifying mRNA vaccine — which continuously replicates its own mRNA — could theoretically produce spike protein indefinitely. The shedding potential from such a product would be not a brief event but a chronic condition, transforming the vaccinated individual into a continuous source of spike protein exposure for everyone in their environment.</p>
<p>As detailed in our article on <a href="/self-amplifying-mrna-replicon/?lang=es">Self-Amplifying mRNA Replicon</a>, the implications of self-amplifying mRNA technology extend far beyond individual vaccination into the realm of environmental exposure — a technological capability that could affect entire populations regardless of individual consent.</p>

If shedding is real, then the "personal choice" framing used to justify the vaccines collapses, because the choice to be vaccinated would impose biological consequences on others who did not make that choice.</p>
