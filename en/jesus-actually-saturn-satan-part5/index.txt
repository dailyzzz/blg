<h2 id="the-counterfeit-christ">The Counterfeit Christ</h2>
<h3 id="what-the-churches-teach-vs-what-scripture-says">What the Churches Teach vs. What Scripture Says</h3>
<p>The institutional church presents a Jesus who is comfortable, safe, and affirming. This Jesus wants you to attend services, tithe your income, celebrate the holidays, and trust your pastor. This Jesus looks like the culture that worships him — European in European churches, African in African churches, Asian in Asian churches. This Jesus is marketed on merchandise, reduced to bumper stickers, and invoked at football games.</p>
<p>The Jesus of scripture is none of these things. The Jesus of scripture overturned tables in the temple. He called the religious leaders "whitewashed tombs" and "children of your father the devil" (John 8:44). He said the path to life is narrow and "few there be that find it" (Matthew 7:14). He demanded the abandonment of family, wealth, and self. He promised persecution, suffering, and death to those who followed him.</p>
<p>These two figures cannot be the same person. The church's Jesus and scripture's Jesus are incompatible. One of them is real, and the other is a construction — a Saturn figure wearing a Jesus mask.</p>
<h3 id="the-sun-god-in-the-sanctuary">The Sun God in the Sanctuary</h3>
<p>The Catholic Church's imagery betrays its origins. The monstrance — the golden vessel used to display the consecrated host — is shaped like a sunburst. The host itself is a circular disc — the sun. The mass is celebrated on Sunday — the day of the sun. The Vatican's St. Peter's Square features an Egyptian obelisk (a solar-phallic symbol) at its center, surrounded by an ellipse designed by Bernini to represent the sun's path.</p>
<p>These are not Christian symbols. They are solar symbols, and the solar cult is inseparable from the Saturn cult. Saturn and Sol are complementary — the father and the son in the pagan pantheon, the dark and the light aspects of the same cosmic power. The Catholic Church, under Jesuit administration, worships both simultaneously: Saturn through its cube symbolism, calendar, and hierarchy, and Sol through its imagery, architecture, and liturgical timing.</p>
<p>Agent131711's Substack (agent131711.substack.com) documents this dual worship in extensive detail, showing how the institutional church functions as a two-faced system — Christian in vocabulary, pagan in practice. The Jesuit strategy, as revealed in <em>Catholic Confidential</em>, is to infiltrate "cloaked with cunning," appearing as what they are not. The church itself is the greatest example of this infiltration: an institution that appears Christian but operates as a Saturn-Sol temple.</p>
<h3 id="the-halo-saturns-ring-on-christs-head">The Halo: Saturn's Ring on Christ's Head</h3>
<p>Every depiction of Jesus in Catholic and Orthodox art features a halo — a golden ring around the head. This is not a biblical symbol. No scripture describes Jesus with a ring of light around his head. The halo derives from pagan art, where it represented the solar disc. It appears in depictions of Helios, Apollo, Mithras, and other sun gods long before it was applied to Christian figures.</p>
<p>By placing Saturn's ring — the ring of binding — on the head of Christ, the artists of the church (many of whom were financed by Jesuit patrons) performed a visual incantation: they bound the image of Christ to the image of Saturn-Sol. Every church that displays these images, every Bible with these illustrations, every Christmas card with a haloed Jesus — all propagate the Saturn counterfeit.</p>
<h2 id="the-name-manipulation">The Name Manipulation</h2>
<h3 id="jesus-vs-yeshua">"Jesus" vs. Yeshua</h3>
<p>The name "Jesus" is a transliteration through multiple languages: from Hebrew Yeshua to Greek Iesous to Latin Iesus to English Jesus. The name itself is not the issue — transliteration is a normal linguistic process. The issue is what has been attached to the name.</p>
<p>In the ancient world, names carried power. To know a being's true name was to have authority over it. The Saturn cult understood this. By attaching the name "Jesus" to a figure that combines solar imagery, Saturn symbolism, pagan ritual timing, and institutional hierarchy, the cult effectively hijacked the name — using it as a vessel for a different spiritual entity.</p>
<p>When Christians pray to "Jesus" while visualizing the haloed, European-featured figure of institutional art, while kneeling in a building designed according to pagan architectural principles, while celebrating festivals timed to the pagan calendar — are they actually reaching the Yeshua of Nazareth? Or are they connecting with the Saturn-Sol composite that the Jesuits have substituted in his place?</p>
<p>Howard Pittman's testimony suggests the latter. The 97.5 percent rejection rate implies that the vast majority of Christians are praying to the wrong being, worshipping the wrong figure, following the wrong path. They believe they know Jesus, but as Pittman was told, they "never made Me Lord." The "Me" they missed is not the church's Jesus. It is the real one.</p>
<h3 id="the-cross-instrument-of-death-as-object-of-worship">The Cross: Instrument of Death as Object of Worship</h3>
<p>The cross is the central symbol of Christianity. But consider what it represents: an instrument of torture and execution. Roman crucifixion was designed to be the most painful, humiliating death possible. The cross was not a religious symbol — it was a gallows, an electric chair, a lethal injection table.</p>
<p>In occult symbolism, the cross has pre-Christian meanings. The tau cross (T-shape) was associated with Tammuz, the dying and rising god of Babylon. The ankh of Egypt — a cross with a loop — represented life through death, the cycle of sacrifice and renewal that Saturn governs. By placing the cross at the center of Christian worship, the church ensured that Christians would venerate death, sacrifice, and the Saturn cycle — all while believing they were honoring their Savior.</p>
<p>Jesus said to take up your cross and follow him (Matthew 16:24). This was a call to self-denial and spiritual death to the world. The church transformed this metaphor into a literal object of worship, a golden ornament worn around the neck, a jeweled decoration on the altar — reversing the meaning from spiritual discipline to material idolatry. This is Saturn's trick: to take the spiritual and make it material, to take the living truth and fossilize it into dead ritual.</p>
<h2 id="the-jesuit-construction-project">The Jesuit Construction Project</h2>
<h3 id="building-the-false-church">Building the False Church</h3>

<p>Step one: Infiltrate the Catholic Church. The Jesuits were founded in 1540 and within decades had become the most powerful order in Catholicism. <p>Step two: Standardize worship. The Council of Trent (1545-1563), heavily influenced by the Jesuits, standardized Catholic liturgy, theology, and practice. It codified the Mass, the calendar, the structure of worship. It ensured that every Catholic church worldwide would perform the same rituals, speak the same words, follow the same calendar — the Jesuit-designed calendar that is built on pagan timing.</p>
<p>Step three: Suppress alternatives. The Inquisition, which the Jesuits administered with particular zeal, eliminated anyone who questioned the standardized version of Christianity. Gnostics, Cathars, Waldensians, Hussites, and eventually Protestants were targeted. The Jesuits' willingness to practice "every species of vice," as <em>Catholic Confidential</em> reveals, extended to torture, assassination, and the engineering of wars.</p>
<p>Step four: Infiltrate Protestantism. When the Reformation threatened to break the monopoly, the Jesuits deployed their most sophisticated strategy: they infiltrated Protestant movements. The Jesuit oath, documented in multiple sources, explicitly commands members to appear as Protestants, to join Protestant churches, to rise to positions of leadership within them. Over centuries, this infiltration has brought Protestant worship closer and closer to Catholic practice — the same holidays, the same imagery, the same institutional structure.</p>

<p>The result of five centuries of Jesuit work is a global Christianity that, across almost all denominations, worships on the solar calendar, celebrates pagan holidays, uses Saturn-Sol imagery, operates through hierarchical institutions, and teaches a version of Jesus that is functionally indistinguishable from a pagan deity.</p>
<p>This is the Jesus of the churches: a Saturn-Sol figure wearing Christian names, preached from pulpits designed as pagan temples, celebrated on dates stolen from pagan festivals, depicted with pagan symbols, and worshipped through rituals that trace their lineage to Babylon, Rome, and the mystery schools.</p>

<p>Howard Pittman's 97.5 percent statistic makes sense in this light. <h2 id="finding-the-real-jesus">Finding the Real Jesus</h2>
<h3 id="outside-the-institution">Outside the Institution</h3>
<p>If the institutional Jesus is a counterfeit, where is the real one? The answer, paradoxically, is in the scripture that the institution claims to follow but systematically contradicts. The real Jesus is found in the words he spoke — words that Matthew 12:36-37 tells us carry eternal weight. Not in buildings, not in rituals, not in holidays, not in institutional authority, but in the text itself.</p>
<p>The real Jesus said: <em>"God is a Spirit: and they that worship him must worship him in spirit and in truth"</em> (John 4:24). Spirit and truth. Not brick and mortar. Not ritual and calendar. Not hierarchy and obedience to men. Spirit — the invisible, immaterial reality that Saturn worship is designed to make you forget. And truth — the uncomfortable, tradition-shattering, institution-toppling truth that Pittman brought back from the other side.</p>
<p>The 5 percent who truly serve God, according to Pittman's account, are those who have found the real Jesus outside the Saturn system. They may attend churches or they may not, but they do not confuse the institution with the Savior. They test everything against scripture. They reject traditions that contradict the text. They worship in spirit and truth, not in cubes and circles and rings.</p>

It is comfortable. It is popular. It is decorated with beautiful music, impressive architecture, and the warm glow of community. And it leads to the 97.5 percent rejection rate that Pittman witnessed.</p>
<p>The narrow path requires leaving all of that behind. It requires questioning everything you were taught.
