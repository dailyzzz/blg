15:T3f90,<h1 id="over-100000-doctors-pave-way-to-vacate-prep-act-immunity">Over 100,000 Doctors Pave Way to Vacate PREP Act Immunity</h1>

<hr>
<h2 id="the-prep-act-the-fortress-of-immunity">The PREP Act: The Fortress of Immunity</h2>
<p>The Public Readiness and Emergency Preparedness Act (PREP Act), signed into law in 2005, provides blanket immunity from liability for manufacturers, distributors, program planners, and qualified persons involved in the administration of "covered countermeasures" during a declared public health emergency.</p>
<p>On February 4, 2020 — before any COVID-19 case had been confirmed in the United States through community spread — HHS Secretary Alex Azar issued a PREP Act declaration covering COVID-19 countermeasures. This declaration was subsequently amended multiple times to expand its scope, extending immunity to vaccine manufacturers, pharmacies, healthcare providers, and government officials involved in the vaccination program.</p>
<p>The PREP Act's immunity is nearly absolute. It can only be overcome in cases of "willful misconduct" — a legal standard so high that it has never been successfully met in any PREP Act case. The definition of willful misconduct under the Act requires proof that the covered person acted "intentionally to achieve a wrongful purpose" with "knowledge that the act is unlawful" and in "disregard of a known or obvious risk that is so great as to make it highly probable that the harm will outweigh the benefit."</p>
<p>The only compensation mechanism available to those injured by covered countermeasures is the Countermeasures Injury Compensation Program (CICP), administered by HHS. The CICP has no judicial review, no discovery process, no right to legal representation, no right to appeal, and a one-year filing deadline. As of 2024, the CICP had adjudicated thousands of COVID-19 vaccine injury claims — and compensated fewer than a dozen.</p>
<p>Karen Kingston has documented in The Kingston Report (karenkingston.substack.com) that the PREP Act was the essential legal precondition for the entire COVID-19 injection program. "Without the PREP Act, no manufacturer would have agreed to produce the vaccines, no pharmacy would have agreed to administer them, and no employer would have agreed to mandate them," Kingston has written. "The PREP Act is not merely a liability shield — it is the foundation upon which the entire compulsory injection apparatus was built. Remove the PREP Act, and the apparatus collapses."</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="the-physicians-challenge">The Physicians' Challenge</h2>
<p>Over 100,000 physicians have now signed onto a legal and legislative effort to vacate the PREP Act's immunity provisions as applied to COVID-19 countermeasures. The effort is proceeding on multiple fronts:</p>
<h3 id="constitutional-challenge">Constitutional Challenge</h3>
<p>The physicians argue that the PREP Act, as applied to COVID-19 vaccines, violates the Seventh Amendment right to a civil jury trial. The Seventh Amendment guarantees: "In Suits at common law, where the value in controversy shall exceed twenty dollars, the right of trial by jury shall be preserved." The PREP Act eliminates this right entirely for individuals injured by covered countermeasures, replacing the jury trial with an administrative compensation program that provides no judicial review.</p>
<p>The constitutional challenge also invokes the Due Process Clause of the Fifth Amendment, arguing that the PREP Act deprives injured individuals of their liberty and property (including their right to sue for compensation) without due process of law.</p>
<h3 id="statutory-challenge">Statutory Challenge</h3>
<p>The physicians argue that the PREP Act's immunity should not apply to COVID-19 mRNA injections because the products were materially misrepresented to the public. The PREP Act protects "covered countermeasures" — but the physicians contend that the products administered were not what they were represented to be.</p>
<p>Specifically, the physicians allege that the mRNA injections were represented as "vaccines" — products that prevent infection and transmission — when in fact they did not prevent infection or transmission. The products were represented as "safe and effective" when clinical trial data was incomplete, the control group was eliminated, and safety signals were suppressed. The products were represented as remaining at the injection site when biodistribution data showed they spread throughout the body.</p>
<p>If the products were materially misrepresented, the argument goes, the PREP Act's immunity does not apply because the covered countermeasure actually administered was not the covered countermeasure described in the authorization.</p>
<h3 id="legislative-effort">Legislative Effort</h3>
<p>In parallel with the legal challenges, physicians are pressing for congressional action to amend or repeal the PREP Act. The legislative effort focuses on:</p>
<ul>
<li>Eliminating or substantially lowering the "willful misconduct" standard</li>
<li>Providing injured individuals with a right to a civil jury trial</li>
<li>Establishing an independent compensation tribunal with judicial review</li>
<li>Requiring manufacturers to maintain liability insurance as a condition of EUA authorization</li>
<li>Implementing a sunset provision that limits PREP Act immunity to the duration of the declared emergency</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="the-significance-of-100000-physicians">The Significance of 100,000 Physicians</h2>
<p>The number is significant not merely for its scale but for what it represents. These are licensed physicians — individuals who have completed medical school, residency training, and board certification. They hold the credentials that the medical establishment uses to gatekeep authority. They cannot be dismissed as "anti-vaxxers" or "conspiracy theorists" without undermining the credibility of the entire credentialing system.</p>
<p>The 100,000 physicians represent approximately 10% of the practicing physician workforce in the United States. They include family medicine doctors, internists, cardiologists, neurologists, oncologists, pediatricians, emergency medicine physicians, and surgeons. They practice in every state, in every healthcare system, in every clinical specialty.</p>
<p>Their unified challenge to the PREP Act represents the most significant dissent within the medical profession since the COVID-19 pandemic began. It demonstrates that the narrative of unanimous medical consensus supporting the vaccination program was always false — maintained not by genuine agreement but by the threat of professional consequences for dissent.</p>
<p>Kingston has observed: "The 100,000 physicians who are challenging the PREP Act represent the conscience of American medicine. They are the doctors who saw the injuries, reported the adverse events, and were told to be silent. They are the physicians who asked questions and were disciplined. They are the medical professionals who placed their oath above their careers — and they are no longer willing to accept a legal framework that prevents their patients from seeking justice."</p>

<h2 id="the-injuries-behind-the-numbers">The Injuries Behind the Numbers</h2>
<p>The PREP Act does not exist in a vacuum. It protects manufacturers and administrators from liability for real injuries sustained by real human beings.</p>
<p>VAERS — the Vaccine Adverse Event Reporting System, co-managed by the CDC and FDA — has received over 1.6 million adverse event reports related to COVID-19 vaccines, including:</p>
<ul>
<li>Over 37,000 reports of death</li>
<li>Over 68,000 reports of permanent disability</li>
<li>Over 213,000 reports of hospitalization</li>
<li>Over 172,000 reports of emergency room visits</li>
<li>Over 21,000 reports of myocarditis/pericarditis</li>
<li>Over 16,000 reports of heart attack</li>
<li>Over 56,000 reports of severe allergic reaction</li>
</ul>
<p>These numbers are widely acknowledged — even by the CDC — to represent significant underreporting. A Harvard Pilgrim Healthcare study commissioned by HHS found that fewer than 1% of vaccine adverse events are reported to VAERS, suggesting that the actual number of adverse events is orders of magnitude higher than the reported numbers.</p>
<p>The PREP Act ensures that none of these individuals — or their families, in the case of deaths — can sue the manufacturers of the products that harmed them. The 100,000 physicians challenging the PREP Act are fighting to restore the legal right of these injured Americans to seek justice through the court system.</p>
