15:T3598,<h1 id="sunscreen-the-toxic-protection-myth">Sunscreen: The Toxic Protection Myth</h1>
<h2 id="the-ancient-pattern">The Ancient Pattern</h2>
<blockquote>
<p>"The Lord God made the sun to rule the day." — Genesis 1:16 (paraphrased)</p>
</blockquote>
<p>God created the sun. He made it to give light, to sustain life, to govern the rhythms of day and night, and to provide the energy that powers virtually every biological process on Earth. The sun produces vitamin D in the human skin, regulates circadian rhythm, stimulates serotonin production, strengthens the immune system, and provides the light that enables sight. The modern demonization of the sun — and the promotion of chemical sunscreen as daily protection against it — is the inversion of a divine design: declaring God's creation dangerous and selling a chemical shield against it that is more toxic than the exposure it claims to prevent.</p>
<p>As the Confliction album track <a href="https://confliction.zzzdaily.com/tracks/en/33-The_Fire_in_the_Cave.mp4">The Sun and the Moon</a> captures, the celestial bodies God placed in the heavens testify to His creative order. The campaign to make people fear the sun is a campaign to sever humanity from the most visible and constant expression of divine provision.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="the-sunscreen-industry">The Sunscreen Industry</h2>
<p>The global sunscreen market is valued at approximately $14 billion and projected to reach $22 billion by 2030. The dermatology establishment, the cosmetics industry, and public health agencies have spent decades promoting a single, unified message: the sun causes skin cancer, and sunscreen is essential daily protection.</p>
<p>This message has been remarkably effective. Sunscreen use has become one of the most universally accepted health behaviors in developed countries. Dermatologists recommend applying sunscreen to all exposed skin every day, regardless of UV index, season, or duration of sun exposure. Children are coated in sunscreen before recess. Office workers who spend their days indoors apply sunscreen to walk to their cars.</p>
<p>Yet the evidence that daily sunscreen use prevents skin cancer is far less clear than the messaging suggests, and the evidence that sunscreen chemicals themselves pose health risks is far more alarming than the dermatology establishment acknowledges.</p>
<h2 id="what-is-in-your-sunscreen">What Is in Your Sunscreen?</h2>
<p>Chemical sunscreens — the most commonly used type — work by absorbing UV radiation through organic chemical compounds that undergo photochemical reactions on the skin. The most common active ingredients include:</p>
<p><strong>Oxybenzone (benzophenone-3).</strong> The most widely used sunscreen chemical in America. A 2020 FDA study found that oxybenzone is absorbed into the bloodstream after a single application at levels that exceed the FDA's safety threshold by approximately 180-fold. Oxybenzone is a documented endocrine disruptor that mimics estrogen, blocks androgen, and disrupts thyroid hormones. It has been linked to endometriosis in women, low testosterone in men, and early puberty in children. A 2008 study found oxybenzone in the urine of 97% of Americans tested. Hawaii and Palau have banned oxybenzone-containing sunscreens due to their devastating effects on coral reefs — the chemical triggers coral bleaching at concentrations as low as 62 parts per trillion.</p>
<p><strong>Octinoxate (ethylhexyl methoxycinnamate).</strong> Another widely used chemical filter that is absorbed into the bloodstream and acts as an endocrine disruptor. Studies have found that octinoxate affects estrogen receptor activity and thyroid function. It is also toxic to coral reefs and has been banned alongside oxybenzone in Hawaii.</p>
<p><strong>Homosalate.</strong> Absorbed into the bloodstream at levels exceeding FDA safety thresholds. The EU Scientific Committee on Consumer Safety has expressed concern about homosalate's endocrine-disrupting potential and recommends limiting its concentration in cosmetic products.</p>
<p><strong>Octocrylene.</strong> A UV filter that degrades into benzophenone — a suspected carcinogen — over time. A 2021 study published in Chemical Research in Toxicology found that benzophenone concentration in sunscreen products increased significantly with age, meaning that older sunscreen becomes more carcinogenic over time.</p>
<p><strong>Avobenzone.</strong> While generally considered more stable than other chemical filters, avobenzone breaks down under UV exposure (the very condition it is designed to protect against), producing free radicals that can damage DNA and skin cells.</p>
<p>The FDA proposed updated sunscreen regulations in 2019 that classified only two sunscreen ingredients — zinc oxide and titanium dioxide (mineral filters) — as "generally recognized as safe and effective" (GRASE). Twelve chemical sunscreen ingredients, including oxybenzone and octinoxate, were classified as requiring additional safety data — data that the industry has not provided years later.</p>
<h2 id="the-vitamin-d-paradox">The Vitamin D Paradox</h2>
<p>The most profound consequence of universal sunscreen promotion is the mass suppression of vitamin D synthesis — one of the sun's most critical biological functions.</p>
<p>When UVB radiation strikes the skin, it converts 7-dehydrocholesterol in the skin into vitamin D3 (cholecalciferol). This is the body's primary source of vitamin D — a hormone-like substance essential for:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Bone health</strong> — vitamin D regulates calcium absorption</li>
<li><strong>Immune function</strong> — vitamin D activates T cells and other immune mechanisms</li>
<li><strong>Cancer prevention</strong> — studies consistently show that higher vitamin D levels are associated with lower rates of multiple cancers</li>
<li><strong>Cardiovascular health</strong> — vitamin D deficiency is associated with increased cardiovascular risk</li>
<li><strong>Mental health</strong> — vitamin D deficiency is linked to depression, anxiety, and seasonal affective disorder</li>
<li><strong>Autoimmune regulation</strong> — vitamin D modulates the immune system to prevent it from attacking the body's own tissues</li>
</ul>
<p>Sunscreen with SPF 30 reduces vitamin D synthesis by approximately 95-99%. Daily sunscreen use, as recommended by dermatologists, effectively eliminates the skin's ability to produce vitamin D from sunlight.</p>
<p>The result: an estimated 42% of American adults are vitamin D deficient — a figure that rises to 82% among Black Americans, whose higher melanin levels already reduce UV-induced vitamin D synthesis. Vitamin D deficiency is associated with every major chronic disease, including cancer, the very disease sunscreen is supposed to prevent.</p>
<p>The dermatology establishment's response to the vitamin D crisis it created is to recommend synthetic vitamin D supplements — manufactured by pharmaceutical companies and taken in a form that, as documented in our article on <a href="/synthetic-vitamins-natural-fraud/?lang=en">Synthetic Vitamins: The Natural Health Fraud</a>, is not identical to the vitamin D the body produces naturally from sunlight.</p>
<h2 id="the-skin-cancer-narrative">The Skin Cancer Narrative</h2>
<p>The central claim of the sunscreen industry is that UV exposure causes skin cancer and that sunscreen prevents it. The reality is more nuanced than the industry acknowledges:</p>
<p><strong>Melanoma rates have increased despite increased sunscreen use.</strong> Since the 1970s, when sunscreen use became widespread, melanoma incidence has increased 320% in the United States. If sunscreen prevents melanoma, the most deadly form of skin cancer, rates should have declined as sunscreen use increased. They did the opposite.</p>
<p><strong>Indoor workers have higher melanoma rates than outdoor workers.</strong> Multiple studies have found that people who work indoors — receiving less total sun exposure — have higher rates of melanoma than people who work outdoors. This finding contradicts the simplistic narrative that more sun equals more cancer.</p>
<p><strong>Intermittent, burning exposure — not total sun exposure — is linked to melanoma.</strong> The risk factor for melanoma is not total sun exposure but intermittent, intense exposure that causes sunburn — particularly in childhood. Regular, moderate sun exposure (the kind outdoor workers receive) appears to be protective, not harmful.</p>
<p><strong>Sunscreen may contribute to melanoma.</strong> Chemical sunscreens filter UV radiation selectively, often blocking UVB (which causes burning and vitamin D synthesis) more effectively than UVA (which penetrates deeper into the skin and is more strongly linked to melanoma). This means sunscreen allows people to spend extended time in the sun without burning — while still receiving the deeper-penetrating UVA radiation that contributes to melanoma. Sunscreen may increase melanoma risk by enabling prolonged UVA exposure without the sunburn warning signal.</p>
<p>As documented in <a href="/rockefeller-medicine-captured-healthcare/?lang=en">Rockefeller Medicine: How Healthcare Was Captured</a>, the pattern of creating a health problem and then selling the solution is the foundational business model of the pharmaceutical and cosmetics industries. Fear the sun. Buy the chemical shield. Get vitamin D deficient. Buy the supplement. Get cancer anyway. Buy the treatment.</p>
<p>As documented in <a href="/two-hundred-years-insanity-medicine/?lang=en">Two Hundred Years of Insanity in Medicine</a>, the medical establishment has a long history of promoting practices that cause more harm than the conditions they claim to address.</p>
<h2 id="the-environmental-destruction">The Environmental Destruction</h2>
<p>The chemicals in sunscreen do not stay on your skin. When you swim, shower, or sweat, sunscreen chemicals enter the water supply. An estimated 14,000 tons of sunscreen enter the world's oceans every year.</p>
<p>The effects on marine ecosystems are devastating:</p>
<ul>
<li>Oxybenzone triggers coral bleaching at concentrations of 62 parts per trillion</li>
<li>Sunscreen chemicals have been detected in fish, dolphins, and marine mammals</li>
<li>UV-filter chemicals disrupt the reproductive systems of fish and other marine organisms</li>
<li>Sunscreen contamination has been detected in remote marine environments far from beaches</li>
</ul>
<p>The industry's response has been to market "reef-safe" formulations — many of which still contain chemicals that are harmful to marine life, and for which "reef-safe" is an unregulated marketing term with no legal definition.</p>
