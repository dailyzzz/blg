Sick of watching truth-tellers silenced, deplatformed, demonetized, and destroyed while liars are amplified, promoted, and rewarded. Sick of a system that calls censorship "community guidelines" and surveillance "safety." Sick of living in a world where speaking truth is punished and spreading lies is profitable.</p>
<p>Howard Pittman was sent back from the other side with a message. Three heavens. Demonic hierarchy. 97.5 percent rejection. 95 percent self-service. Every word judged (Matthew 12:36-37). The censorship system exists because truth is the most dangerous weapon against the principalities and powers of Ephesians 6:12. If truth were harmless, they would not need to suppress it. The very existence of censorship proves that truth has power — power that the system fears.</p>

Their Inquisition punished those who possessed, distributed, or discussed forbidden ideas. Loyola's military organization, Napoleon's "absolute power," Tamburini's global governance — all depended on the suppression of information. <em>Catholic Confidential</em> reveals infiltration "cloaked with cunning," blind obedience, "every species of vice." Modern censorship is the digital Index — and Big Tech companies are the new Inquisition.</p>

<h2 id="the-censorship-machine">The Censorship Machine</h2>
<h3 id="how-it-works">How It Works</h3>
<p>Modern censorship operates through a collaboration between government agencies, technology companies, media organizations, and academic institutions. This is not a theory. It is documented through court cases, congressional hearings, leaked communications, and whistleblower testimony.</p>
<p>The government identifies narratives it wants to suppress. It communicates these priorities to technology companies through formal and informal channels. The technology companies implement the suppression through algorithmic demotion, shadow banning, content removal, account suspension, and deplatforming. Academic institutions provide the intellectual framework that justifies censorship as "combating misinformation." Media organizations amplify the approved narratives and discredit the suppressed ones.</p>
<p>This four-part censorship machine — government, tech, academia, media — operates as a unified system despite being composed of nominally independent entities. Agent131711 documents on Substack (agent131711.substack.com) how this system functions in practice, tracing the flow of censorship directives from government agencies through technology companies to the suppression of specific content creators and specific ideas.</p>
<h3 id="what-gets-censored">What Gets Censored</h3>
<p>The censored topics are revealing. They are not random — they follow a pattern:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Health information</strong> that contradicts pharmaceutical interests</li>
<li><strong>Financial information</strong> that challenges banking and taxation narratives</li>
<li><strong>Historical information</strong> that questions institutional narratives</li>
<li><strong>Religious information</strong> that challenges institutional Christianity</li>
<li><strong>Political information</strong> that challenges the two-party system</li>
<li><strong>Scientific information</strong> that challenges established paradigms</li>
</ul>
<p>The common thread is institutional power. Content is censored when it threatens the authority, revenue, or legitimacy of powerful institutions. Content that supports these institutions is amplified. This is not "combating misinformation." It is protecting institutional power from public scrutiny.</p>
<p>The Jesuits' Index of Forbidden Books followed the same logic. Books were forbidden not because they were false but because they were dangerous — dangerous to the Church's authority, dangerous to the Jesuits' influence, dangerous to the institutional order that the Society of Jesus maintained. The modern censorship machine operates on identical principles, targeting identical threats, serving identical masters.</p>
<h2 id="fighting-back">Fighting Back</h2>
<h3 id="the-weapons-of-resistance">The Weapons of Resistance</h3>
<p>Censorship is powerful but not omnipotent. It has vulnerabilities that can be exploited by anyone willing to fight:</p>
<p><strong>1. Decentralized Platforms</strong>: Platforms that cannot be controlled by a single company — decentralized social media, peer-to-peer networks, blockchain-based publishing — resist censorship by design. When content exists on thousands of independent nodes rather than a single corporate server, no authority can remove it.</p>
<p><strong>2. Direct Communication</strong>: Email lists, newsletters, personal websites, and offline networks bypass platform censorship entirely. Building direct relationships with your audience — rather than renting access through a platform — is the single most important step any truth-teller can take.</p>
<p><strong>3. Physical Media</strong>: Books, pamphlets, DVDs, and USB drives cannot be algorithmically suppressed. Physical media distributes information through channels the digital censorship machine cannot reach.</p>
<p><strong>4. Encryption</strong>: End-to-end encrypted communications prevent surveillance of private conversations. Signal, ProtonMail, and similar services protect the content of communications from government and corporate monitoring.</p>
<p><strong>5. Archiving</strong>: Content that is censored from one platform can be archived on others. The Internet Archive, distributed storage systems, and personal backup strategies ensure that deleted content survives.</p>
<p><strong>6. Community</strong>: The most powerful weapon against censorship is community — people who share information, support truth-tellers, and refuse to be silenced. A community of truth-seekers is harder to suppress than any individual.</p>
<h3 id="what-you-can-do-today">What You Can Do Today</h3>
<ol>
<li><strong>Subscribe directly</strong> to independent creators through email, not through platforms.</li>
<li><strong>Save and share</strong> important content before it is censored.</li>
<li><strong>Use alternative platforms</strong> that do not censor based on content.</li>
<li><strong>Encrypt your communications</strong> with anyone discussing sensitive topics.</li>
<li><strong>Support independent media</strong> financially — they depend on audience funding, not corporate advertising.</li>
<li><strong>Speak up</strong> — every voice raised against censorship makes the next voice easier.</li>
</ol>
<h3 id="the-spiritual-warfare-dimension">The Spiritual Warfare Dimension</h3>
<p>Censorship is spiritual warfare. Ephesians 6:12 identifies the enemy as "principalities, powers, the rulers of the darkness of this world." Darkness requires the suppression of light. Censorship is the suppression of light — the deliberate prevention of truth from reaching the people who need it.</p>
<p>Howard Pittman was told that 97.5 percent of Christians were rejected because they "never made Me Lord." Censorship contributes directly to this failure by suppressing the information that would help people see through institutional deception and find genuine truth. The censorship machine is a soul-stealing machine — preventing truth from reaching the ears of those who would hear it, and ensuring that the institutional lies that lead to the 97.5 percent rejection remain unchallenged.</p>
<p>The Jesuits understood this. Their Index existed not to protect people from falsehood but to prevent them from encountering truth — truth that would undermine institutional authority and individual dependence. The modern censorship machine serves the same purpose. It does not protect you from misinformation. It prevents you from encountering information that would free you from the system.</p>
<p>Every word shared in defiance of censorship is a spiritual act. Every truth spoken despite the risk of deplatforming is an act of warfare against the principalities. Every person who refuses to self-censor — who speaks what they know to be true regardless of the consequences — is fighting the battle that Ephesians describes. This is not metaphor. This is reality.</p>
<h2 id="the-path-forward">The Path Forward</h2>
<h3 id="the-cost-and-the-reward">The Cost and the Reward</h3>
<p>Fighting censorship has costs. Deplatforming means lost audiences, lost revenue, lost social standing. Speaking truth in a lying world means isolation, ridicule, and professional consequences. The system is designed to make the cost of truth higher than the cost of silence.</p>
<p>But the reward is eternal. Matthew 12:36-37 warns that every word will be judged — including the words not spoken, the truths not shared, the information not passed along because the cost seemed too high. The narrow path runs through the risk of speaking truth. The wide path runs through the comfort of silence.</p>
<p>I am sick of the wide path. I am sick of watching truth buried under algorithms and "community guidelines." I am sick of a system that rewards lies and punishes truth. And I am not going to be silent about it.</p>
<p>Neither should you.</p>
