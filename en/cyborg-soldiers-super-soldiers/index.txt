15:T7527,<h1 id="cyborg-soldiers-the-militarys-superhuman-program">Cyborg Soldiers: The Military's Superhuman Program</h1>
<h2 id="the-ancient-pattern">The Ancient Pattern</h2>
<blockquote>
<p>"There were giants in the earth in those days; and also after that, when the sons of God came in unto the daughters of men, and they bare children to them, the same became mighty men which were of old, men of renown." — Genesis 6:4</p>
</blockquote>
<p>The Nephilim of Genesis 6 were not ordinary warriors. They were superhuman beings — the offspring of fallen angels who abandoned their proper domain and took human women as wives. The resulting hybrids were "mighty men of old, men of renown" — entities of extraordinary physical power, heightened cognitive ability, and terrifying combat prowess. They were, by every definition we have today, super soldiers. They dominated the pre-Flood world, and their violence and corruption were so extreme that God destroyed the entire earth with a deluge to end their reign.</p>
<p>That was the ancient method: forbidden union between angelic and human genetics to produce warriors beyond the capacity of ordinary men.</p>
<p>Today, the method has changed, but the objective has not. DARPA — the Pentagon's most advanced research arm — is spending billions of dollars to create a new generation of Nephilim. Not through supernatural union, but through technology: exoskeletons that multiply human strength, neural implants that accelerate cognition, drugs that eliminate sleep and fear, and genetic modifications that rewrite the biological code of living soldiers. The hybrid warrior is being rebuilt in laboratories and defense contractor facilities across the world.</p>
<p>As the Confliction album track <a href="https://confliction.zzzdaily.com/tracks/en/73-The_Fallen_Angels.mp4">The Fallen Angels</a> recounts, the Watchers descended upon Mount Hermon and swore an oath to corrupt humanity. They taught men the arts of war — the forging of weapons, the crafting of shields, the making of swords. They gave humanity the technology of violence. What DARPA does today is the continuation of what the Watchers began. The tools have evolved from bronze to bioelectronics, but the agenda is identical: create warriors that exceed the boundaries God set for the human body.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="darpas-super-soldier-programs">DARPA's Super Soldier Programs</h2>
<p>The Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency has been the engine of American military innovation since 1958. It created the internet, GPS, stealth aircraft, and mRNA platform technology. But its most ambitious current portfolio is the systematic augmentation of the human warfighter — programs designed to push soldiers beyond the limits of natural human performance.</p>
<h3 id="warrior-web">Warrior Web</h3>
<p>Launched in 2011, DARPA's Warrior Web program aimed to develop a soft, lightweight undersuit that would reduce the physical burden on soldiers. The modern infantry soldier carries 90 to 140 pounds of equipment — body armor, weapons, ammunition, communications gear, batteries, water, and food. This load causes musculoskeletal injuries at epidemic rates: over 800,000 musculoskeletal injury cases are treated in the military health system annually.</p>
<p>Warrior Web was not an exoskeleton in the traditional science fiction sense. It was a body-conforming suit embedded with sensors, micro-motors, and biomechanical actuators that would detect a soldier's movements and provide precisely targeted physical assistance. The goal was a suit that would make a loaded soldier feel as if they were carrying no load at all — reducing injury, extending operational endurance, and allowing soldiers to move faster and fight longer than any unaugmented human.</p>
<p>The program transitioned into classified follow-on efforts that remain largely undisclosed to the public.</p>
<h3 id="talos--the-iron-man-suit">TALOS — The Iron Man Suit</h3>
<p>In 2013, the U.S. Special Operations Command (SOCOM) launched the Tactical Assault Light Operator Suit, known as TALOS — and publicly nicknamed "the Iron Man suit" by military officials who wanted to generate excitement and funding.</p>
<p>TALOS was designed to be a full-body powered exoskeleton providing:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Ballistic protection</strong> — liquid armor that would solidify on impact, stopping bullets and shrapnel</li>
<li><strong>Enhanced strength and speed</strong> — powered joints amplifying the operator's physical output</li>
<li><strong>Integrated sensors</strong> — real-time physiological monitoring, night vision, and heads-up tactical displays</li>
<li><strong>Climate control</strong> — internal heating and cooling systems for operating in any environment</li>
<li><strong>Wound monitoring and treatment</strong> — embedded medical sensors that could detect injuries and automatically apply wound-sealing compounds</li>
</ul>
<p>SOCOM commander Admiral William McRaven described the suit's ambition in stark terms: "I'd like that to be a reality someday. That would be awesome." The program brought together 56 corporations, 16 government agencies, 13 universities, and 10 national laboratories — a mobilization of resources that reveals how seriously the Pentagon takes the super soldier agenda.</p>
<p>TALOS was officially shelved in 2019, with the military stating that the technology wasn't ready for integration. But the component technologies — powered exoskeletons, liquid armor, physiological monitoring, and neural interfaces — all continued in separate classified programs. TALOS was not cancelled. It was scattered into compartmented programs harder for the public to track.</p>
<h3 id="inner-armor">Inner Armor</h3>
<p>DARPA's Inner Armor concept takes augmentation beneath the skin. Rather than external suits, Inner Armor explores biological modifications that would make the soldier's own body more resilient:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Enhanced immune response</strong> — engineering immune systems that can resist biological and chemical weapons</li>
<li><strong>Accelerated healing</strong> — stimulating the body's natural repair mechanisms to heal battlefield wounds in hours rather than weeks</li>
<li><strong>Pain suppression</strong> — modifying pain pathways so soldiers can continue fighting through injuries that would incapacitate normal humans</li>
<li><strong>Thermal tolerance</strong> — expanding the body's ability to withstand extreme heat and cold</li>
</ul>
<p>This is not science fiction. DARPA's Biological Technologies Office has an annual budget exceeding $500 million and funds dozens of programs exploring exactly these capabilities. When the Pentagon says "Inner Armor," it means turning the human body itself into a weapons platform — a living system redesigned for war.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="the-exoskeleton-arms-race">The Exoskeleton Arms Race</h2>
<p>While DARPA develops the classified frontier, private defense contractors are building and deploying military exoskeletons today.</p>
<h3 id="sarcos-robotics--the-guardian-xo">Sarcos Robotics — The Guardian XO</h3>
<p>Sarcos Robotics, now a Palantir Technologies partner, has developed the Guardian XO — a full-body robotic exoskeleton that allows a single operator to lift up to 200 pounds repeatedly without strain. The system is battery-powered, untethered, and designed for military logistics, construction, and combat support. The U.S. military has been evaluating the Guardian XO since 2020 for use in supply chain operations, ammunition handling, and battlefield repair.</p>
<p>The Guardian XO is the public-facing version. What exists in classified programs — and what Sarcos' military contracts actually specify — extends into direct combat applications that are not disclosed to the civilian public.</p>
<h3 id="lockheed-martin-hulc">Lockheed Martin HULC</h3>
<p>Lockheed Martin's Human Universal Load Carrier (HULC) is a lower-body exoskeleton that allows soldiers to carry 200-pound loads at speeds up to 10 miles per hour over rough terrain, with dramatically reduced metabolic cost. The system uses hydraulic actuators powered by lithium polymer batteries and is designed to be worn under standard-issue body armor and equipment.</p>
<p>HULC was developed in partnership with the U.S. Army Natick Soldier Research Center and has undergone extensive field testing. While Lockheed Martin has publicly positioned HULC as a logistics tool, the system's design is explicitly combat-oriented: it augments the legs and lower body, exactly the systems needed for soldiers to move faster, carry heavier weapons, and maintain operational tempo over longer distances than any unaugmented human can sustain.</p>
<p>The convergence of exoskeleton technology with AI-driven movement prediction, ballistic armor integration, and embedded weapons systems is creating something that looks less like a tool and more like the armored, unstoppable warriors described in ancient texts.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="neural-enhancement-rewiring-the-warriors-brain">Neural Enhancement: Rewiring the Warrior's Brain</h2>
<p>Physical augmentation is only half the program. DARPA is equally invested in augmenting the soldier's brain — making warriors who think faster, learn quicker, fear less, and process information at superhuman speeds.</p>
<h3 id="transcranial-direct-current-stimulation-tdcs">Transcranial Direct Current Stimulation (tDCS)</h3>
<p>The U.S. Air Force and DARPA have conducted extensive research on transcranial direct current stimulation — applying low-level electrical current to specific brain regions to enhance cognitive performance. Studies conducted at the Air Force Research Laboratory demonstrated that tDCS could:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Double the speed of learning</strong> complex tasks like drone piloting and threat identification</li>
<li><strong>Enhance focus and vigilance</strong> during extended operations</li>
<li><strong>Reduce reaction time</strong> in combat decision-making</li>
<li><strong>Improve pattern recognition</strong> — the ability to identify threats in complex visual environments</li>
</ul>
<p>In one widely reported study, snipers who received tDCS during training improved their target acquisition speed by a factor of 2.3 compared to the control group. This is not marginal improvement. This is a fundamental shift in human cognitive performance, achieved by running electricity through the brain.</p>
<h3 id="darpas-targeted-neuroplasticity-training-tnt">DARPA's Targeted Neuroplasticity Training (TNT)</h3>
<p>The Targeted Neuroplasticity Training program, launched in 2017, takes neural enhancement further. TNT uses peripheral nerve stimulation — activating specific nerves in the body — to trigger the release of neurochemicals that enhance the brain's ability to form new neural connections. The goal is to create a "window of enhanced learning" during which soldiers can acquire new skills — foreign languages, code-breaking, marksmanship, combat tactics — at dramatically accelerated rates.</p>
<p>TNT is explicitly about creating warriors who can learn in days what normally takes months or years. The program has been tested at multiple military installations and the results have been impressive enough to generate follow-on funding for classified applications.</p>
<h3 id="brain-computer-interfaces-for-combat">Brain-Computer Interfaces for Combat</h3>
<p>As detailed in our article on <a href="/neuralink-merge-man-machine/?lang=en">Neuralink and the Race to Merge Man with Machine</a>, DARPA's Neural Engineering System Design (NESD) program and its Next-Generation Nonsurgical Neurotechnology (N3) program are developing brain-computer interfaces that would allow soldiers to control weapons systems, drones, and communications equipment directly with their thoughts.</p>
<p>A soldier with a neural interface could:</p>
<ul>
<li>Control a swarm of drones with thought commands alone</li>
<li>Receive tactical intelligence directly into their visual cortex — seeing enemy positions overlaid on their natural vision</li>
<li>Communicate silently with team members through thought-to-text or thought-to-thought transmission</li>
<li>Process threat data at speeds impossible for the unaugmented brain</li>
</ul>
<p>This is not a future program. DARPA has been funding these capabilities since the early 2000s, and the convergence of neural interface technology with battlefield AI is creating a warfighter that is functionally a hybrid of human and machine — a cyborg in every meaningful sense of the word.</p>

<h2 id="pharmacological-enhancement-the-chemical-super-soldier">Pharmacological Enhancement: The Chemical Super Soldier</h2>
<p>The military's use of drugs to enhance combat performance is not new — it is one of the oldest augmentation programs in existence.</p>
<h3 id="the-historical-record">The Historical Record</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>World War II</strong> — Both Allied and Axis forces used amphetamines extensively. The Wehrmacht distributed Pervitin (methamphetamine) to millions of German soldiers. American and British forces issued Benzedrine tablets. These drugs eliminated fatigue, suppressed fear, and increased aggression.</li>
<li><strong>Vietnam War</strong> — The U.S. military issued over 225 million amphetamine tablets to American forces between 1966 and 1969.</li>
<li><strong>Gulf War and beyond</strong> — Modafinil (Provigil) replaced amphetamines as the military's preferred wakefulness agent. Originally developed for narcolepsy, modafinil allows soldiers to remain alert and cognitively functional for 40+ hours without sleep, with far fewer side effects than traditional stimulants.</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="current-pharmacological-programs">Current Pharmacological Programs</h3>
<p>Today's military pharmacology goes far beyond simple stimulants:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Fear suppression</strong> — Research into propranolol and experimental compounds that can block the formation of fear memories, creating soldiers who literally cannot experience the debilitating effects of combat stress</li>
<li><strong>Pain elimination</strong> — Development of analgesics that suppress pain perception without dulling cognitive function, allowing wounded soldiers to continue fighting at full capacity</li>
<li><strong>Metabolic enhancement</strong> — Drugs that force the body to burn fat more efficiently, reducing the need for food resupply in extended operations</li>
<li><strong>Memory enhancement</strong> — Compounds that strengthen memory consolidation, allowing soldiers to recall training, maps, and tactical data with photographic precision</li>
<li><strong>Aggression modulation</strong> — Pharmaceutical tools that can increase or decrease aggression on command, creating soldiers who switch between hyper-aggressive assault mode and calm, precise marksmanship at a chemical signal</li>
</ul>
<p>DARPA's Preventing Emerging Pathogenic Threats (PREEMPT) program and its broader biological technology portfolio include pharmacological agent development that blurs the line between medicine and weapons development. As documented in our article on <a href="/pharmakeia-sorcery-pharmaceuticals/?lang=en">Pharmakeia: Sorcery in the Modern Age</a>, the Greek word translated as "sorcery" in Revelation 18:23 is <em>pharmakeia</em> — the root of our word "pharmacy." The pharmaceutical transformation of warriors into something beyond human is, in the most literal biblical sense, sorcery applied to warfare.</p>

<h2 id="genetic-modification-rewriting-the-soldiers-dna">Genetic Modification: Rewriting the Soldier's DNA</h2>
<p>The most radical frontier of the super soldier program is genetic modification — using technologies like CRISPR-Cas9 to permanently alter the DNA of living soldiers.</p>
<h3 id="crispr-for-combat">CRISPR for Combat</h3>
<p>CRISPR (Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats) allows scientists to edit genes with unprecedented precision. In a military context, CRISPR could theoretically be used to:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Enhance muscle density and strength</strong> — modifying the myostatin gene, which regulates muscle growth, to create soldiers with naturally superior physical power</li>
<li><strong>Increase oxygen-carrying capacity</strong> — editing genes related to red blood cell production (similar to the natural mutation found in elite endurance athletes) to create soldiers who can operate at high altitudes without degradation</li>
<li><strong>Accelerate healing</strong> — activating regenerative gene pathways that are dormant in adult humans but active in embryonic development</li>
<li><strong>Enhance night vision</strong> — modifying genes related to rhodopsin production in the retina</li>
<li><strong>Eliminate PTSD susceptibility</strong> — editing genes linked to stress response and trauma processing</li>
<li><strong>Extend operational endurance</strong> — modifying metabolic genes to allow soldiers to function effectively on minimal food and sleep</li>
</ul>
<p>DARPA's Safe Genes program, launched in 2017, was officially designed to develop tools for controlling and reversing genetic modifications. But the existence of a program to reverse gene editing in military personnel presupposes the existence of programs that perform gene editing on military personnel. You don't build a cure unless the disease already exists — or is being deliberately created.</p>
<p>As explored in our article on <a href="/gene-editing-crispr-humanity/?lang=en">Gene Editing: CRISPR and the Rewriting of Humanity</a>, the modification of human DNA crosses a line that Scripture draws clearly. Genesis 1:27 states that God created man in His own image. To rewrite that image at the genetic level — to alter the fundamental code that makes a human a human — is to repeat the transgression of the Watchers who corrupted human genetics before the Flood.</p>
<h3 id="chinas-gene-edited-soldier-program">China's Gene-Edited Soldier Program</h3>
<p>In 2019, John Ratcliffe, then Director of National Intelligence, stated publicly that China was conducting "human testing" on members of the People's Liberation Army in pursuit of developing "biologically enhanced soldiers." Western intelligence officials confirmed that Chinese military scientists had been exploring the use of CRISPR to enhance combat performance in active-duty soldiers.</p>
<p>Reports from U.S. intelligence indicate that China's program includes:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Gene editing for enhanced physical endurance</strong> — modifying metabolic pathways to create soldiers who can march longer, carry more, and recover faster</li>
<li><strong>Cognitive enhancement</strong> — editing genes related to memory, processing speed, and decision-making under stress</li>
<li><strong>Pain tolerance modification</strong> — altering pain receptor genes to create soldiers less affected by battlefield injuries</li>
<li><strong>Biological resilience</strong> — gene modifications that enhance resistance to chemical and biological weapons</li>
</ul>
<p>China's willingness to conduct genetic experiments on its own military personnel — unconstrained by the ethical frameworks that nominally govern Western research — creates a gene-editing arms race. If China deploys genetically enhanced soldiers, the Pentagon will argue that the U.S. must do the same or face strategic inferiority. This is exactly how every arms race in history has escalated: the other side is doing it, so we must do it too.</p>
<p>The result is a world where both superpowers are actively rewriting the DNA of their warriors — creating human beings who are, at the genetic level, no longer entirely natural. The Nephilim were hybrids of angelic and human genetics. The gene-edited soldier is a hybrid of natural and engineered genetics. The method has changed. The category of abomination has not.</p>
