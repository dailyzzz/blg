15:T375b,<h1 id="the-knights-templar-conspiracy-that-changed-the-world-part-1">The Knights Templar CONSPIRACY That Changed the World Part 1</h1>
<h2 id="the-ancient-pattern">The Ancient Pattern</h2>
<blockquote>
<p><em>"For such are false apostles, deceitful workers, transforming themselves into the apostles of Christ."</em> — 2 Corinthians 11:13</p>
</blockquote>
<p>The Apostle Paul warned of false apostles — men who wore the garments of Christ while serving the adversary. The Knights Templar, the Poor Fellow-Soldiers of Christ and of the Temple of Solomon, are history's most famous example of this principle. Founded in the aftermath of the First Crusade as protectors of Christian pilgrims, the Templars became within decades the wealthiest, most powerful, and most secretive organization in the medieval world — an organization whose true purpose had nothing to do with protecting pilgrims and everything to do with the accumulation of forbidden knowledge, occult power, and global financial control.</p>
<p>The Jesuit order, which would be founded two centuries after the Templar suppression, was the Templars' spiritual successor. Ignatius Loyola studied the Templar model and perfected it: where the Templars were overt, the Jesuits were covert; where the Templars were destroyed by a king, the Jesuits made themselves indispensable to kings. John Adams wrote: <em>"If ever there were a body of men who merited eternal damnation on earth and in hell, it is this society of Loyolas."</em> The same could be said of the Templars — but the Templars' damnation was temporal. The Jesuits learned to make theirs eternal.</p>
<p>As <a href="https://confliction.zzzdaily.com/tracks/en/73-The_Fallen_Angels.mp4">The Fallen Angels</a> recounts, the conspiracy began with the fall of angels who brought forbidden knowledge to Earth. The Templars recovered that knowledge and used it to build an empire. <a href="https://confliction.zzzdaily.com/tracks/en/09-Satans_Disguise.mp4">Satan's Disguise</a> reveals that the greatest deception is the one that dresses evil in the clothing of holiness — exactly what the Templars achieved under their white mantles emblazoned with the red cross.</p>

<h2 id="the-founding-myth">The Founding Myth</h2>
<h3 id="nine-knights-and-a-purpose">Nine Knights and a Purpose</h3>
<p>The official history tells us that in 1119 AD, a French knight named Hugues de Payens and eight companions presented themselves to King Baldwin II of Jerusalem and offered to protect Christian pilgrims on the roads between the port of Jaffa and the holy city. Baldwin granted them quarters in the Al-Aqsa Mosque on the Temple Mount — directly above the ruins of Solomon's Temple.</p>
<p>This story, repeated in every history textbook, does not survive scrutiny:</p>
<p><strong>Nine knights could not protect a road.</strong> The route from Jaffa to Jerusalem was approximately 40 miles through hostile territory. Nine men could not have patrolled this road effectively. The Kingdom of Jerusalem had a standing army for this purpose; nine additional men were meaningless from a military standpoint.</p>
<p><strong>The Templars did not recruit for nine years.</strong> If their mission was road protection, they would have needed to expand immediately. Instead, they spent nine years as a closed group of nine men, quartered on the Temple Mount, doing — what? The only logical explanation is that their true mission was not protection but excavation.</p>
<p><strong>Bernard of Clairvaux's patronage.</strong> The Templars' chief patron was Bernard of Clairvaux, the most powerful churchman in Europe and the founder of the Cistercian order. Bernard's support transformed the Templars from obscurity to prominence virtually overnight. His 1129 tract <em>In Praise of the New Knighthood</em> provided the theological justification for the order and secured papal endorsement. Bernard's connection to the Templars suggests high-level ecclesiastical backing for whatever the knights were doing on the Temple Mount.</p>
<h3 id="the-council-of-troyes">The Council of Troyes</h3>
<p>In 1129, the Templars received official recognition at the Council of Troyes, presided over by a papal legate and attended by Bernard of Clairvaux. The council gave the Templars a formal Rule (based on the Cistercian Rule) and authorized them to receive donations and recruit members.</p>
<p>The timing is significant: 1129 was ten years after the Templars' founding — precisely the period they spent on the Temple Mount without recruiting or conducting military operations. Whatever they found during those ten years was sufficiently valuable to earn them the endorsement of the most powerful churchman in Europe and the official recognition of the papacy.</p>
<h2 id="the-rise-to-power">The Rise to Power</h2>
<h3 id="papal-privileges">Papal Privileges</h3>
<p>In 1139, Pope Innocent II issued the papal bull <em>Omne Datum Optimum</em>, which granted the Templars extraordinary privileges:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Exemption from all authority except the Pope's</strong> — The Templars answered to no king, no bishop, no local authority. They were a law unto themselves.</li>
<li><strong>Tax exemption</strong> — The Templars paid no taxes to any secular or ecclesiastical authority.</li>
<li><strong>Freedom to collect tithes</strong> — The Templars could collect their own tithes and donations without interference.</li>
<li><strong>Freedom to build their own churches</strong> — The Templars could worship in their own facilities, independent of the local diocese.</li>
</ul>
<p>These privileges made the Templars the most independent organization in medieval Christendom. They were, in effect, a sovereign entity operating within (but not subject to) the kingdoms of Europe. No organization before or since has enjoyed such comprehensive legal immunity — not until the Jesuit order received similar privileges from Pope Paul III in 1540.</p>
<h3 id="the-financial-empire">The Financial Empire</h3>
<p>With their legal privileges secured, the Templars built the most sophisticated financial network the world had ever seen. By the mid-13th century, the Templars:</p>
<ul>
<li>Held over 870 castles, commanderies, and estates across Europe and the Holy Land</li>
<li>Managed the financial affairs of the French Crown and several other monarchies</li>
<li>Operated an international money transfer system that spanned the known world</li>
<li>Employed thousands of soldiers, administrators, and workers</li>
<li>Maintained the largest private fleet in the Mediterranean</li>
</ul>
<p>The Templar financial system was the prototype for modern international banking. Their letters of credit, their lending operations, and their trust management services created the infrastructure that later banking dynasties — the Medicis, the Fuggers, the Rothschilds — would build upon. The modern banking system is, in a very real sense, a Templar invention.</p>
<h2 id="the-esoteric-dimension">The Esoteric Dimension</h2>
<h3 id="solomons-knowledge">Solomon's Knowledge</h3>
<p>The Templars' name — the Poor Fellow-Soldiers of Christ and of the Temple of Solomon — points to their esoteric purpose. Solomon, in Jewish, Christian, and Islamic tradition, was not merely a wise king; he was a magician who commanded demons, possessed supernatural knowledge, and built the Temple using techniques that transcended ordinary human capability.</p>
<p>The Templar excavations beneath the Temple Mount were a search for Solomon's knowledge — the technology and esoteric wisdom that Solomon had used to build the Temple, to command spirits, and to accumulate the greatest wealth in the ancient world. The books attributed to Solomon — the <em>Key of Solomon</em>, the <em>Lesser Key of Solomon</em>, the <em>Testament of Solomon</em> — describe a system of ceremonial magic that was well known in medieval esoteric circles. The Templars sought the original sources of this knowledge.</p>
<h3 id="sacred-geometry-in-templar-architecture">Sacred Geometry in Templar Architecture</h3>
<p>The Templars' architectural legacy reveals the esoteric knowledge they possessed. Templar churches and commanderies incorporate sacred geometric principles that are consistent with both Islamic geometric tradition and the ancient mysteries:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Octagonal plans</strong> — The octagon, which represents the transition between the square (Earth) and the circle (Heaven), is a central motif in Templar architecture.</li>
<li><strong>Golden ratio proportions</strong> — Templar buildings incorporate the golden ratio (1:1.618) in their proportions, a mathematical relationship that was known in the ancient world but largely forgotten in medieval Europe.</li>
<li><strong>Acoustic properties</strong> — Templar churches are designed to amplify and shape sound in specific ways, suggesting knowledge of acoustics that exceeded the medieval European norm.</li>
</ul>
<p>These architectural features suggest that the Templars possessed technical knowledge from a tradition older and more sophisticated than anything available in 12th-century Europe — knowledge that was almost certainly acquired during their excavations in the Holy Land.</p>
<h2 id="the-friday-the-13th-arrest">The Friday the 13th Arrest</h2>
<h3 id="the-fall-of-the-templars">The Fall of the Templars</h3>
<p>On Friday, October 13, 1307, the Templars were arrested across France in a coordinated operation that gave us the superstition about Friday the 13th. King Philip IV ordered the arrests, charging the Templars with heresy, blasphemy, and sodomy.</p>
<p>The charges, while extracted under torture, are remarkably consistent across hundreds of confessions taken in different locations by different inquisitors:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Denial of Christ</strong> — Initiates were required to spit on the cross, deny Christ, and trample on a crucifix during their induction ceremony.</li>
<li><strong>Worship of Baphomet</strong> — The Templars allegedly worshipped a head called Baphomet — a term whose etymology has never been satisfactorily explained but which has been linked to the Islamic prophet Muhammad (Mahomet), the Atbash cipher for Sophia (wisdom), and various demonic entities.</li>
<li><strong>Ritualistic practices</strong> — The initiatory rituals reportedly included indecent kissing (on the mouth, navel, and base of the spine) and encouragement of homosexual relations.</li>
</ul>
<p>Whether these charges reflected actual Templar practices or were fabricated by Philip's inquisitors remains debated. But the consistency of the confessions, and the Templars' documented connections to Islamic and esoteric traditions, suggest that at least some of the charges had a basis in reality.</p>
<p>The Templar conspiracy changed the world by creating the template that every subsequent secret society would follow: accumulate forbidden knowledge, build a financial empire, infiltrate political power, and serve a hidden master while presenting a public face of piety and service. The Jesuits perfected this template, and they operate it today through the Catholic Church, the Vatican Bank, and the global network of secret societies that answers to the Black Pope.</p>

The conspiracy did not end on Friday the 13th, 1307 — it simply changed its name and continued its mission under the banner of the Society of Jesus.
