<h3 id="the-rfid-revolution">The RFID Revolution</h3>
<p>Radio-Frequency Identification (RFID) technology emerged in the 1970s and was rapidly adapted for tracking and identification:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>1970s-1980s</strong>: RFID used for animal identification and inventory management</li>
<li><strong>1990s</strong>: Miniaturized RFID chips developed for implantation in pets</li>
<li><strong>1998</strong>: Kevin Warwick becomes the first human to voluntarily receive an RFID implant</li>
<li><strong>2004</strong>: The FDA approves VeriChip, the first RFID chip designed for human implantation</li>
<li><strong>2006</strong>: VeriChip is marketed for medical record access, patient identification, and secure facility entry</li>
<li><strong>2010s-present</strong>: Thousands of people worldwide have received voluntary RFID implants, primarily in Sweden, and the technology continues to miniaturize</li>
</ul>
