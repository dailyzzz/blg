<h2 id="the-catalogue-of-horrors">The Catalogue of Horrors</h2>
<h3 id="the-tuskegee-syphilis-experiment-1932-1972">The Tuskegee Syphilis Experiment (1932-1972)</h3>
<p>For 40 years, the United States Public Health Service conducted an experiment on 600 Black men in Macon County, Alabama. The men were told they were receiving free healthcare for "bad blood." In reality:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>399 men with syphilis</strong> were deliberately left untreated so researchers could study the disease's progression</li>
<li>They were <strong>never told</strong> they had syphilis</li>
<li>They were given <strong>placebos instead of treatment</strong>, even after penicillin became the standard cure in the 1940s</li>
<li>They were actively <strong>prevented from receiving treatment</strong> from other sources</li>
<li>At least <strong>128 men died</strong> of syphilis or its complications</li>
<li><strong>40 wives</strong> were infected with syphilis</li>
<li><strong>19 children</strong> were born with congenital syphilis</li>
</ul>
<p>The experiment was not conducted by rogue scientists. It was conducted by the United States government, overseen by the CDC, and published in peer-reviewed medical journals. The medical establishment knew about it and did nothing for four decades.</p>
<h3 id="guatemala-syphilis-experiments-1946-1948">Guatemala Syphilis Experiments (1946-1948)</h3>
<p>In a parallel atrocity, US government researchers deliberately infected hundreds of Guatemalans — prisoners, mental patients, soldiers, and sex workers — with syphilis and gonorrhea without their consent. The experiments were funded by the NIH and conducted by the same Dr. John Cutler who was involved in the Tuskegee experiment.</p>
<p>Methods included:</p>
<ul>
<li>Direct inoculation with syphilis bacteria</li>
<li>Hiring infected sex workers to have intercourse with prisoners</li>
<li>Applying infected material to subjects' eyes, mouths, and genitals</li>
<li>Scraping the skin and applying syphilis-laden fluid</li>
</ul>
<p>At least 83 subjects died. The experiments were concealed for over 60 years until historian Susan Reverby discovered them in archival records in 2010.</p>
