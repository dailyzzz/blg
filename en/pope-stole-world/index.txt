<h2 id="the-papal-bulls-legal-theft-of-the-world">The Papal Bulls: Legal Theft of the World</h2>
<h3 id="romanus-pontifex-1455">Romanus Pontifex (1455)</h3>
<p>On January 8, 1455, Pope Nicholas V issued the papal bull <em>Romanus Pontifex</em>, which granted the King of Portugal the right to conquer, colonize, and exploit all lands inhabited by non-Christians in West Africa. This bull established the legal framework for the Atlantic slave trade and the colonization of Africa.</p>
<p>The language of the bull is explicit: the Pope granted Portugal the right to "invade, search out, capture, vanquish, and subdue all Saracens and pagans whatsoever, and other enemies of Christ wheresoever placed, and the kingdoms, dukedoms, principalities, dominions, possessions, and all movable and immovable goods whatsoever held and possessed by them and to reduce their persons to perpetual slavery."</p>
<p>This is the Pope — the supposed representative of Christ on Earth — authorizing the permanent enslavement of entire peoples. This is not a medieval aberration; it is the foundational legal document for European colonialism, and it has never been formally rescinded.</p>

Everything west of the line belonged to Spain; everything east belonged to Portugal. With a stroke of his pen, the Pope gave away two continents that did not belong to him — continents that were already inhabited by millions of people.</p>
<p>Alexander VI was one of the most corrupt Popes in history. He fathered at least seven illegitimate children, was widely rumored to have committed incest with his daughter Lucrezia, and was accused by contemporaries of murder, bribery, and Satanic ritual.
