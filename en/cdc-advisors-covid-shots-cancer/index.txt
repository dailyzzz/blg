<h2 id="the-disclosures">The Disclosures</h2>
<h3 id="what-cdc-advisors-have-acknowledged">What CDC Advisors Have Acknowledged</h3>
<p>In a development that would have been unthinkable even two years ago, members of the CDC's Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices (ACIP) and affiliated scientific advisors have disclosed data and concerns linking COVID-19 mRNA vaccinations to elevated cancer risk. These disclosures include:</p>
<p><strong>Acknowledgment of Immune Suppression Patterns:</strong> Advisors have acknowledged data showing that mRNA vaccination is associated with transient but significant immune suppression — a phenomenon characterized by reduced T-cell function, altered natural killer (NK) cell activity, and shifts in the immune profile that parallel patterns seen in immunocompromised cancer patients.</p>
<p><strong>Recognition of DNA Contamination:</strong> Scientific advisors have acknowledged the discovery of residual plasmid DNA in mRNA vaccine vials — contamination from the manufacturing process. This DNA includes the SV40 promoter sequence, a genetic element derived from Simian Virus 40 that is known to drive gene expression and has been linked to cancer in laboratory studies. The presence of SV40 promoter sequences in a product injected into billions of people is, by any measure, a cancer-relevant finding.</p>
<p><strong>Review of VAERS Signal Data:</strong> Advisors have reviewed Vaccine Adverse Event Reporting System (VAERS) data showing statistically significant signals for various cancers following COVID-19 vaccination, including lymphoma, leukemia, breast cancer, and cancers of the brain and central nervous system. While VAERS data alone does not establish causation, the pattern and magnitude of the signal exceeds what would be expected from background cancer rates.</p>
<p><strong>Acknowledgment of Turbo Cancer Reports:</strong> The medical community's informal designation of "turbo cancers" — unusually aggressive, fast-growing, treatment-resistant cancers in previously healthy and often young individuals — has been acknowledged by advisors as a phenomenon requiring investigation. The temporal association between vaccination campaigns and the emergence of these cancers has been noted, though advisors are careful to frame this as "correlation requiring further study" rather than established causation.</p>

<p>Karen Kingston's analysis through <a href="https://karenkingston.substack.com">The Kingston Report</a> provides the documentary framework that predicted these disclosures years before the CDC acknowledged them.</p>
<h3 id="the-sv40-promoter-discovery">The SV40 Promoter Discovery</h3>
<p>Kingston was among the first analysts to highlight the significance of Dr. Kevin McKernan's discovery of SV40 promoter sequences in Pfizer's mRNA vaccine vials. McKernan, a genomics expert who developed sequencing methods used in the Human Genome Project, found that Pfizer's vials contained DNA contamination at levels far exceeding regulatory limits — and that this contamination included the SV40 promoter/enhancer sequence.</p>
<p>The SV40 promoter is not a random piece of DNA. It is a specific genetic element that:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Drives gene expression in mammalian cells</strong> — it acts as a powerful "on switch" for whatever gene it is placed near</li>
<li><strong>Is derived from a cancer-causing virus</strong> — Simian Virus 40, which is known to cause tumors in laboratory animals and has been linked to human cancers including mesothelioma, brain tumors, and lymphoma</li>
<li><strong>Can facilitate genomic integration</strong> — when the SV40 promoter is present near other DNA sequences, it can facilitate the integration of those sequences into the host cell genome, potentially creating permanent genetic modifications</li>
</ul>
<p>Kingston documented that the presence of SV40 promoter sequences in mRNA vaccine vials means that recipients potentially received not only synthetic mRNA but also DNA fragments capable of integrating into their genome and driving the expression of oncogenes (cancer-causing genes).</p>
<h3 id="the-immune-suppression-documentation">The Immune Suppression Documentation</h3>
<p>Kingston has also documented the mechanism of mRNA vaccine-induced immune suppression. The modified nucleoside (N1-methylpseudouridine) used in both Pfizer and Moderna mRNA products was incorporated to prevent the immune system from immediately destroying the foreign mRNA. But this modification does not selectively suppress only the anti-mRNA response — it broadly suppresses innate immune signaling, including:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Toll-like receptor (TLR) activation</strong> — the immune system's first-response pathogen detection system</li>
<li><strong>Type I interferon production</strong> — the immune system's primary antiviral and anticancer signaling pathway</li>
<li><strong>Natural killer (NK) cell function</strong> — the immune cells specifically responsible for identifying and destroying cancerous cells</li>
</ul>
<p>By suppressing these fundamental immune functions, mRNA vaccination creates a window of vulnerability during which pre-cancerous cells that would normally be identified and destroyed are allowed to survive and proliferate. In individuals with existing undetected cancers, this immune suppression can accelerate tumor growth — explaining the turbo cancer phenomenon.</p>

<h2 id="the-cancer-mechanisms-a-comprehensive-picture">The Cancer Mechanisms: A Comprehensive Picture</h2>
<p>The CDC advisors' disclosures align with multiple documented mechanisms by which COVID-19 mRNA shots can promote cancer:</p>
<h3 id="mechanism-1-p53-tumor-suppressor-suppression">Mechanism 1: p53 Tumor Suppressor Suppression</h3>
<p>As documented in our article on <a href="/spike-protein-cancer-tumor-suppressing/?lang=en">Spike Protein and Cancer Tumor-Suppressing Genes</a>, the spike protein produced by mRNA vaccines suppresses the p53 tumor suppressor — the guardian of the genome. With p53 disabled, cells accumulate DNA damage without triggering the repair or self-destruction programs that prevent cancer.</p>
<h3 id="mechanism-2-dna-contamination-and-genomic-integration">Mechanism 2: DNA Contamination and Genomic Integration</h3>
<p>The SV40 promoter-containing DNA fragments discovered in mRNA vaccine vials can potentially integrate into the host genome. If integration occurs near an oncogene, the SV40 promoter can activate that oncogene, driving cancer development. If integration disrupts a tumor suppressor gene, that gene's protective function is lost.</p>
<h3 id="mechanism-3-immune-suppression">Mechanism 3: Immune Suppression</h3>
<p>The N1-methylpseudouridine modification suppresses innate immune function, reducing the body's ability to detect and destroy pre-cancerous cells. This creates an immunological permissive environment for tumor growth.</p>
<h3 id="mechanism-4-chronic-inflammation">Mechanism 4: Chronic Inflammation</h3>
<p>Spike protein persistence — documented for months or longer after vaccination — drives chronic inflammation. Chronic inflammation is a well-established driver of cancer development, creating an environment of oxidative stress, DNA damage, and cellular proliferation that favors tumor formation.</p>
<h3 id="mechanism-5-toll-like-receptor-interference">Mechanism 5: Toll-like Receptor Interference</h3>
<p>The mRNA construct and its lipid nanoparticle delivery system interfere with Toll-like receptor signaling — the innate immune system's pattern recognition receptors that detect abnormal cells. This interference reduces immune surveillance and allows cancerous cells to evade detection.</p>
<h3 id="mechanism-6-igg4-class-switching">Mechanism 6: IgG4 Class Switching</h3>
<p>Repeated mRNA vaccination has been documented to cause immunoglobulin class switching from IgG1/IgG3 (inflammatory, cancer-fighting antibodies) to IgG4 (tolerogenic antibodies that suppress immune responses). IgG4 class switching tells the immune system to tolerate the antigen rather than fight it — and this tolerance can extend to cancer cells expressing spike protein or spike-modified surface markers.</p>
