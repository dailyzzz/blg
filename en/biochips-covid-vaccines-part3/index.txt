<h2 id="the-patent-evidence">The Patent Evidence</h2>
<h3 id="united-states-patent-us11107588b2">United States Patent US11107588B2</h3>
<p>Among the most significant patents connected to injectable biosensor technology is US11107588B2, assigned to Microsoft Technology Licensing. Titled "Cryptocurrency system using body activity data," this patent describes a system in which:</p>
<ul>
<li>A sensor device monitors body activity (including brain waves, body heat, blood flow, and organ activity)</li>
<li>The body activity data is transmitted to a server</li>
<li>The server evaluates whether the body activity satisfies conditions set by a cryptocurrency system</li>
<li>If conditions are met, cryptocurrency is awarded to the user</li>
</ul>
<p>This patent explicitly describes a system in which human biological activity -- monitored by sensors within or on the body -- is the basis for economic participation. The sensors described in the patent are consistent with the biochip structures documented in vaccine vials and human blood. The cryptocurrency system described is consistent with a digital economic system that could replace physical currency. And the "conditions" that must be satisfied before economic activity is permitted bear unmistakable resemblance to the mark described in Revelation 13 -- a requirement that must be met before anyone can buy or sell.</p>
<p>The patent number itself -- WO2020060606 (the international application number) -- has drawn attention for containing "060606," though this may be coincidental. What is not coincidental is the patent's explicit description of a system that monitors human biology through sensors and makes economic participation contingent upon body activity data.</p>
<h3 id="darpa-and-the-injectable-biosensor-programs">DARPA and the Injectable Biosensor Programs</h3>
<p>The Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA) has funded multiple programs developing injectable biosensor technology:</p>
<p><strong>Profusa and the Lumee Oxygen Platform</strong></p>
<p>DARPA funded Profusa, Inc. to develop an injectable biosensor called the Lumee Oxygen Platform. This system consists of:</p>
<ul>
<li>A hydrogel scaffold injected under the skin</li>
<li>Engineered fluorescent molecules embedded in the scaffold that change their optical properties in response to chemical signals in body tissue</li>
<li>An external reader device that detects the fluorescent signals through the skin</li>
</ul>
<p>Profusa describes this technology as providing "real-time monitoring of body chemistry." The hydrogel scaffold is designed to integrate with surrounding tissue, becoming part of the body. The system provides continuous monitoring of oxygen levels, glucose, lactate, and other biomarkers.</p>
<p>This is not a future technology. DARPA began funding Profusa's injectable biosensor in 2011. By 2021, the technology was being described as a tool for "pandemic preparedness" -- a sensor that could detect infection before symptoms appear.</p>
<p><strong>The N3 Program: Next-Generation Nonsurgical Neurotechnology</strong></p>
<p>DARPA's N3 program funds the development of noninvasive and minimally invasive neural interfaces -- technology that can read from and write to the brain without requiring surgical implantation. The program explicitly describes:</p>
<ul>
<li>Injectable nanotransducers that convert electromagnetic signals into neural signals and vice versa</li>
<li>External transceivers that communicate with the injected nanotransducers</li>
<li>Two-way communication between external systems and the human brain</li>
</ul>
<p>The N3 program does not merely monitor biological activity. It writes to the brain -- transmitting signals that influence neural activity. This is the technological foundation for mind control through injectable nanotechnology, a capability that moves the biochip system from surveillance into active control.</p>

<ul>

<li>Electromagnetic communication between implanted devices and external systems</li>
<li>Energy harvesting from biological processes to power implanted devices</li>
<li>Hydrogel scaffolds for persistent in-body device integration</li>
<li>Neural interfaces using injectable nanotechnology</li>
<li>Body-activity-based economic participation systems</li>
</ul>
<p>These patents do not describe speculative or theoretical technology. They describe engineered systems with specific designs, materials, manufacturing processes, and operational parameters. And the structures found in COVID-19 vaccine vials match the descriptions in these patents.</p>
