<h2 id="the-franklin-coverup-where-it-all-became-visible">The Franklin Coverup: Where It All Became Visible</h2>
<h3 id="the-credit-union-and-the-network">The Credit Union and the Network</h3>
<p>In 1988, the Franklin Community Federal Credit Union in Omaha, Nebraska was raided by federal authorities for financial irregularities. What investigators discovered went far beyond financial fraud. The credit union's manager, Lawrence King Jr., was not merely embezzling funds — he was operating a child trafficking network that supplied minors to some of the most powerful political figures in the United States.</p>
<p>King was a rising star in Republican politics, singing the national anthem at the 1984 Republican National Convention. His connections reached into the White House, the Congress, and the highest levels of law enforcement. The children he procured were flown to Washington D.C. for parties attended by congressmen, senators, lobbyists, and media figures. The abuse was systematic, organized, and protected at every level.</p>
<h3 id="the-witnesses-who-came-forward">The Witnesses Who Came Forward</h3>
<p>Several victims came forward to testify about their experiences. Paul Bonacci, Troy Boner, and Alisha Owen provided detailed accounts of being trafficked to parties in Washington D.C. where they were sexually abused by prominent political figures. Their testimonies were consistent, detailed, and corroborated by physical evidence and independent investigation.</p>
<p>The response of the system was not to protect these witnesses — it was to destroy them. Alisha Owen was convicted of perjury and sentenced to nine to twenty-seven years in prison for daring to name her abusers. Troy Boner recanted his testimony under pressure, later stating he had been threatened, and was eventually found dead under suspicious circumstances. The message was clear: anyone who spoke about the network would be silenced.</p>
<p>A special committee of the Nebraska legislature, led by State Senator Loran Schmit, attempted to investigate. Their lead investigator, Gary Caradori, gathered extensive evidence including photographs. On July 11, 1990, Caradori's plane broke apart in mid-air during a flight from Chicago, killing him and his eight-year-old son. The briefcase containing his evidence was never recovered. The investigation was effectively terminated.</p>
<h3 id="the-grand-jury-inversion">The Grand Jury Inversion</h3>
<p>In a stunning inversion of justice, the grand jury convened to investigate the allegations instead indicted the victims. Rather than pursuing the powerful individuals named in testimony, the legal system turned its machinery against the children who had been abused. This pattern — the protection of perpetrators through the prosecution of victims — would repeat itself in virtually every subsequent exposure of government-connected pedophile networks.</p>
<p>John DeCamp, a former Nebraska state senator and Vietnam War hero, documented the entire Franklin affair in his book "The Franklin Cover-Up." DeCamp named names, presented evidence, and connected the network to broader intelligence operations. His work survived because of his political connections and military background — he was harder to dismiss than the child victims whose testimonies had been destroyed.</p>
