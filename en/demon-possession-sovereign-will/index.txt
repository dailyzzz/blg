15:T642a,<h1 id="the-one-thing-demons-cannot-violate-your-sovereign-will">The One Thing Demons Cannot Violate: Your Sovereign Will</h1>
<h2 id="the-ancient-pattern">The Ancient Pattern</h2>
<blockquote>
<p><em>"And when they were come to the multitude, there came to him a certain man, kneeling down to him, and saying, Lord, have mercy on my son: for he is lunatick, and sore vexed: for ofttimes he falleth into the fire, and oft into the water. And I brought him to thy disciples, and they could not cure him... And Jesus rebuked the devil; and he departed out of him: and the child was cured from that very hour."</em> — Matthew 17:14-18</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p><em>"And he asked him, What is thy name? And he answered, saying, My name is Legion: for we are many."</em> — Mark 5:9</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p><em>"And when he had called unto him his twelve disciples, he gave them power against unclean spirits, to cast them out, and to heal all manner of sickness and all manner of disease."</em> — Matthew 10:1</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Jesus cast out demons with a word. He gave His disciples authority to do the same. The gospels are saturated with accounts of demonic possession and deliverance — a father's lunatic son, a man in the tombs hosting a legion, a woman bound for eighteen years, a mute man, a blind and mute man, a Syrophoenician woman's daughter. Possession was real. Deliverance was real. Authority was real.</p>
<p>But beneath all of these accounts lies a principle that Howard Pittman was shown in the spirit realm — a principle that governs all interaction between the spiritual and human realms. The sovereign will. The one thing that demons cannot violate. The one thing that angels cannot override. The one thing that God Himself will not transgress.</p>
<p>Your will is sovereign. And everything — heaven, hell, and everything between — turns on what you do with it.</p>

<h2 id="made-in-his-image">Made in His Image</h2>
<h3 id="spirit-invisible-immortal">Spirit, Invisible, Immortal</h3>
<p>To understand the sovereign will, you must first understand what a human being is. Not what modern science says. Not what secular philosophy teaches. What the spirit realm reveals.</p>
<p>Howard Pittman learned — through direct experience, not through theological study — that human beings are made in the image of God. This phrase, "made in His image," is repeated in churches around the world without most people understanding what it actually means.</p>
<p>It does not mean that God has two arms and two legs and looks like a human being. God is Spirit. He is invisible. He is immortal. And human beings are made in THAT image — the image of spirit, invisible, immortal.</p>
<p>You are a spirit being. You are not a body that has a spirit. You are a spirit that inhabits a body. The body is temporary — a vehicle, a tent, a house of clay. The spirit is permanent. The spirit is the real you. And the spirit is made in the image of God.</p>
<p>This distinction is fundamental. Animals are not made in God's image. Animals are physical beings — their consciousness, their awareness, their "self" is tied to their physical form. When the body dies, the animal is gone. But humans are different. When the body dies, the spirit continues. The soul — the personality, the consciousness, the identity — goes with the spirit, not with the body.</p>
<p>This is what Pittman experienced when he died on the operating table. His body failed. His monitors flatlined. But HE — Howard Pittman — continued. He was still aware. Still thinking. Still himself. Because he was never his body. He was always his spirit.</p>
<h3 id="the-sovereignty-of-will">The Sovereignty of Will</h3>
<p>And central to that spirit — central to being made in God's image — is the sovereign will. God has a will. It is sovereign. No one can override it. No one can compel God to act against His own will. He is the ultimate free agent in existence — the One whose will is absolutely, unconditionally, eternally free.</p>
<p>Human beings, made in His image, share this attribute. Not to the same degree — we are creatures, not the Creator. But the principle is the same. The human will is sovereign. Within the domain of the individual, the will is the supreme authority. It is the throne room of the human being.</p>
<p>This is not a philosophical position. This is a law of the spirit realm. Pittman discovered that the sovereignty of the human will is a fundamental operating principle in both heaven and hell. It governs how God interacts with humans. It governs how angels interact with humans. And it governs how demons interact with humans.</p>
<p>No one — NO ONE — can violate the sovereign will of a human being.</p>
<h2 id="the-inviolable-law">The Inviolable Law</h2>
<h3 id="demons-cannot">Demons Cannot</h3>
<p>Demons cannot work against a person's sovereign will. This is absolute. No exceptions. No loopholes. No special circumstances that allow a demon to override a human will.</p>
<p>A demon can tempt. It can suggest. It can deceive. It can pressure. It can create circumstances. It can whisper. It can scream. It can present itself as desirable. It can make sin look attractive. It can manipulate emotions. It can exploit weaknesses. It can amplify desires. It can surround a person with temptation from every angle.</p>
<p>But it cannot make the person say yes.</p>
<p>The decision — the act of will that opens the door — must come from the human being. The demon is standing outside the door of the will, knocking. It can knock loudly. It can knock persistently. It can knock in rhythm with the person's desires. But it cannot open the door from the outside. Only the person on the inside can open it.</p>
<p>This is why scripture places responsibility on the human being. "Resist the devil, and he will flee from you" (James 4:7). The command is to resist — which means the power to resist exists. If demons could override the will, resistance would be meaningless. God would not command the impossible. The fact that He commands resistance proves that resistance is possible — because the will is sovereign.</p>
<h3 id="angels-cannot">Angels Cannot</h3>
<p>Angels are similarly bound. They cannot violate a person's sovereign will any more than demons can. An angel cannot force a person to obey God. An angel cannot compel a person to make the right choice. An angel can protect, guide, influence, and communicate — but it cannot override the will.</p>
<p>This is why angelic intervention does not always produce salvation. Angels can present the truth. Angels can create opportunities. Angels can remove obstacles. But the person must still choose. The will must still act. And the angel cannot act on its behalf.</p>
<h3 id="god-will-not">God Will Not</h3>
<p>This is the most staggering revelation of all. God Himself — the Creator of the universe, the Almighty, the One who spoke everything into existence — WILL NOT violate the sovereign will of a human being.</p>
<p>He could. Nothing limits God's power. If He chose to override a human will, no force in existence could stop Him. But He will not. Because the sovereign will is His own design. He created it. He placed it in every human being. And He respects it — utterly, completely, and without exception.</p>
<p>This is why God does not force salvation. This is why He does not compel obedience. This is why He permits rebellion. This is why He allows sin. This is why evil exists in a world created by an omnipotent, loving God.</p>
<p>God gave you a will. A sovereign will. A will that He Himself will not override. And the entire drama of human existence — salvation, damnation, heaven, hell, everything — revolves around what you do with it.</p>
<p>This is love. Not sentimental love. Not the greeting-card version. Real love. The kind that gives the beloved genuine freedom — including the freedom to reject the Lover. The kind that refuses to force, refuses to coerce, refuses to manipulate. The kind that offers everything and demands nothing except a willing response.</p>
<p>The sovereign will is God's greatest gift to humanity. And it is also its greatest danger. Because the same will that can open the door to God can open the door to demons.</p>
<h2 id="how-demon-possession-works">How Demon Possession Works</h2>
<h3 id="what-pittman-witnessed">What Pittman Witnessed</h3>
<p>Howard Pittman did not merely learn about demon possession theoretically. He witnessed it. He saw it happen, in real time, in the spirit realm. And what he saw dismantled every Hollywood portrayal, every horror-movie depiction, and every sensationalized account of possession that the modern world has produced.</p>
<p>Here is what he saw.</p>
<p>A demon approached a human being. But the demon did not attack. It did not force itself upon the person. It did not break through any barrier. Instead, the demon made itself "desirable and attractive" to the human.</p>
<p>This is the key. The demon did not come as a monster. It did not come as a threat. It came as something the person WANTED. Something that appealed to the person's desires, the person's flesh, the person's will. The demon presented itself as a source of pleasure, or power, or relief, or excitement, or whatever the person's particular weakness craved.</p>
<p>And the person agreed.</p>
<p>Through the exercise of the sovereign will, the human being opened the door. He said yes — not verbally, not consciously in most cases, but through the will. Through the repeated choice to engage with the sin, to pursue the desire, to open the door wider and wider until the demon could enter.</p>
<h3 id="the-appearance-of-the-demon">The Appearance of the Demon</h3>
<p>What the demon actually looked like — in the spirit realm, stripped of its disguise — was horrifying. Pittman described it as "an overgrown, slimy, green frog, all out of shape." Not attractive. Not desirable. Not the alluring image that the human had been presented with. The demon's true form was repulsive — a grotesque, misshapen, slimy creature that bore no resemblance to the attractive facade it had projected.</p>
<p>This is the nature of demonic deception. What sin presents as beautiful, the spirit realm reveals as hideous. What the flesh perceives as desirable, the spirit realm shows as slimy and deformed. The demon's true appearance is the true appearance of the sin it represents — disgusting, repulsive, and degrading.</p>
<p>But the human never sees this. The human sees only the disguise. The attractive facade. The beautiful lie. And based on that lie, the human exercises the will and opens the door.</p>
<h3 id="the-moment-of-entry">The Moment of Entry</h3>
<p>Pittman watched the demon enter the person. The demon moved slowly toward the man's face — not rushing, not forcing, moving deliberately, almost gently. And then it entered. Not through the mouth. Not through the eyes. Not through any specific opening.</p>
<p>Pittman described it as "a puff of smoke" that entered through the pores of the skin. The demon dissolved into something like vapor and seeped through the skin itself — through the pores, through the surface, into the body. Like smoke being absorbed. Like mist settling into cloth. Gradual. Subtle. Almost imperceptible.</p>
<p>This is why possession is rarely a sudden, dramatic event. It is not like the movies — no head-spinning, no levitation, no projectile vomiting. It is gradual. Incremental. A slow seeping of demonic influence through a door that the person opened voluntarily and keeps opening wider over time.</p>
<p>Each time the person engages with the sin — each time the will agrees, each time the desire is pursued, each time the door is opened a little wider — the demon moves a little closer. A little deeper. A little more entrenched. Until the puff of smoke has become a permanent resident. Until the frog is inside. And then the disguise comes off.</p>
<h2 id="the-age-of-accountability">The Age of Accountability</h2>
<h3 id="over-90-percent-restriction">Over 90 Percent Restriction</h3>
<p>Pittman learned that over ninety percent of demon activity is restricted to those who have reached or passed the "age of accountability." This is the spiritual threshold at which a human being becomes fully responsible for their choices — the age at which the sovereign will is mature enough to make genuine decisions about sin and righteousness.</p>
<p>Before the age of accountability, children exist under a special protection. Their wills are not yet fully formed. Their ability to genuinely consent — to genuinely exercise sovereign will in the spiritual sense — is not yet developed. And because demons cannot work against the sovereign will, they cannot engage most children. The door is not yet functional. The will is not yet sovereign in the full sense.</p>
<p>This means that the vast majority of demonic activity — over ninety percent — targets adults. People whose wills are fully formed. People who can make genuine choices. People whose doors can be genuinely opened.</p>
<h3 id="the-exception-the-fifth-tier">The Exception: The Fifth Tier</h3>
<p>But there is an exception. The mysterious fifth tier of demons — the unnamed, anonymous group whose power lies in remaining unknown — is the only group permitted to possess small children.</p>
<p>This exception is chilling. It means that some children, under specific circumstances, can be targeted by demonic forces. The fifth-tier demons can bypass the normal restriction — not by violating the child's will (which remains inviolable) but through some other mechanism that Pittman did not fully explain.</p>
<p>This may relate to generational patterns — the choices of parents and grandparents that create spiritual openings in their descendants. It may relate to extreme circumstances of abuse, ritual involvement, or occult activity in the child's environment. Whatever the mechanism, the fifth-tier demons exploit it to gain access to children who would normally be protected.</p>
<p>This is why these demons are the most dangerous. They target the most vulnerable. They operate in total anonymity. They mimic physical conditions like epilepsy — which recalls directly the Gospel account of the father who brought his "lunatick" son to Jesus (Matthew 17:14-18). The boy fell into the fire and into the water. The disciples could not help him. Only Jesus could cast the demon out.</p>
<p>The boy in that Gospel account may well have been targeted by a fifth-tier demon. Afflicted since childhood. Mimicking physical symptoms. Resistant to ordinary deliverance attempts. Requiring the direct authority of Christ Himself.</p>
<h2 id="the-authority-of-the-believer">The Authority of the Believer</h2>
<h3 id="all-gods-children-have-power">All God's Children Have Power</h3>
<p>Here is the counterbalance to the terrifying reality of the demonic hierarchy. Here is the weapon that the church has largely forgotten, ignored, or never learned:</p>
<p>ALL God's children have power over ALL demons.</p>
<p>Not some. Not the weak ones. Not the lower tiers. ALL. Every genuine, truly born-again, Christ-submitted believer has authority over every demon in existence — from the lowliest fifth-tier operative to the eight-foot warring giants to the territorial principalities.</p>
<p>This is not Pittman's opinion. This is scriptural reality confirmed by his experience in the spirit realm. Jesus said to His disciples: "Behold, I give unto you power to tread on serpents and scorpions, and over all the power of the enemy: and nothing shall by any means hurt you" (Luke 10:19). ALL the power of the enemy. Nothing shall by any means hurt you.</p>
<p>This authority is real. It is operational. It is available right now to every genuine believer. The demons know it. The angels know it. God knows it. The only ones who do not seem to know it are the Christians themselves.</p>
<h3 id="based-on-faith-and-knowledge">Based on Faith and Knowledge</h3>
<p>But — and this is the critical qualifier — the effectiveness of this authority depends on two things: faith and knowledge.</p>
<p>Faith means certainty. Not hope. Not wishful thinking. Not "I think maybe this might work." Certainty. The kind of unshakeable, bedrock, absolute conviction that does not waver when the demon pushes back. The kind of faith that Jesus described when He said, "If ye have faith as a grain of mustard seed, ye shall say unto this mountain, Remove hence to yonder place; and it shall remove; and nothing shall be impossible unto you" (Matthew 17:20).</p>
<p>A Christian who exercises authority over a demon with doubt in his heart will find the demon unmoved. Not because the authority is fake. Because the faith is insufficient. The authority is like a loaded weapon — it will work perfectly every time, but only if the person holding it knows how to pull the trigger and is confident it will fire.</p>
<p>Knowledge means understanding. Understanding who you are in Christ. Understanding the authority you possess. Understanding how the spirit realm operates. Understanding the demonic hierarchy — its structure, its tactics, its limitations. Understanding that YOUR authority is not your own — it is Christ's authority delegated to you, and Christ's authority is absolute.</p>
<p>This is why Pittman's testimony matters so much. It provides knowledge. It fills the gap that the placebo church has created. It tells the Christian: HERE is what you are fighting. HERE is how it is organized. HERE is how it operates. HERE are its limitations. And HERE is your authority.</p>
<p>Without this knowledge, the Christian is a soldier fighting blindfolded. With it, the Christian is a warrior — armed, informed, and dangerous.</p>
<h3 id="the-gift-of-deliverance">The Gift of Deliverance</h3>
<p>Pittman also learned about a special spiritual gift — the gift of Deliverance Ministry. This is a specific calling, a specific anointing, a specific equipping by the Holy Spirit for the work of casting out demons and setting captives free.</p>
<p>Not every Christian is called to this ministry. But every Christian should know it exists. And every Christian should know that within the body of Christ, there are men and women who have been specifically gifted, trained, and anointed to deal with demonic possession and oppression.</p>
<p>Alongside the gift of deliverance is the gift of discernment — the ability to perceive what spirit you are dealing with. A Christian with the gift of discernment can identify the specific demon at work, its tier, its specialty, and its strategy. This knowledge is tactical intelligence — the spiritual equivalent of knowing the enemy's name, rank, and position before engaging.</p>
<p>Without discernment, deliverance is like performing surgery blindfolded. You might succeed, but the chances of error are enormous. With discernment, the specific demon is identified, the specific authority is invoked, and the specific bondage is broken.</p>
<h2 id="the-battle-for-the-will">The Battle for the Will</h2>
<h3 id="the-daily-choice">The Daily Choice</h3>
<p>Every day, every human being makes a choice. Not once — thousands of times. The choice is always the same: who sits on the throne? Self or Christ? My will or His will? My desire or His command?</p>
<p>This is the battle. Not the dramatic, exorcism-movie version of spiritual warfare. The daily, mundane, moment-by-moment battle for the sovereign will. Every time you choose self over Christ — every time you pursue your desire instead of His direction, your comfort instead of His command, your plan instead of His purpose — you are opening the door. Not dramatically. Not suddenly. Incrementally. A crack. A little wider. A little wider.</p>
<p>And on the other side of that door, a specialist is waiting. A demon whose sole assignment is the specific sin you just chose. A demon who has been working that specialty for thousands of years. A demon who knows exactly how to make the next choice easier, the next crack wider, the next opening larger.</p>
<p>This is how the frog gets in. Not through force. Through invitation. Through the repeated exercise of the sovereign will in the wrong direction.</p>
<h3 id="the-defense">The Defense</h3>
<p>The defense is equally simple — and equally difficult. Close the door. Exercise the sovereign will in the opposite direction. Choose Christ. Not once. Not at an altar call. Every moment. Every decision. Every desire submitted to His lordship. Every thought brought into captivity (2 Corinthians 10:5). Every impulse filtered through obedience.</p>
<p>"Submit yourselves therefore to God. Resist the devil, and he will flee from you" (James 4:7). Two steps. Submit to God — put Christ on the throne. Resist the devil — exercise the sovereign will against the temptation. And the demon will flee. Not because you are powerful. Because the authority of Christ, exercised through a submitted will, is irresistible.</p>
<p>The sovereign will is the battlefield. Christ on the throne is the victory. Self on the throne is the defeat. And every human being, in every moment, is choosing.</p>
<h3 id="the-ultimate-freedom">The Ultimate Freedom</h3>
<p>The sovereign will is not a burden. It is the ultimate freedom. It is the gift that makes love possible — because love that is not freely chosen is not love. It is the gift that makes faith meaningful — because faith that is not freely offered is not faith. It is the gift that gives human existence its weight, its significance, its eternal consequence.</p>
<p>God did not create robots. He did not create puppets. He did not create beings that obey because they have no choice. He created beings with sovereign will — beings who can choose Him or reject Him, follow Him or rebel against Him, open the door to His Spirit or open the door to His enemy.</p>
<p>And He respects that freedom absolutely. Even when it breaks His heart. Even when it sends souls to destruction. Even when 97.5 percent of professing Christians exercise their sovereign will to keep self on the throne while mouthing the words of surrender.</p>
<p>The door is yours. The will is yours. The choice is yours.</p>
<p>Choose carefully. Eternity hangs on it.</p>
