15:T6beb,<h1 id="wake-up-church-the-narrow-gate-is-narrower-than-you-think">Wake Up Church: The Narrow Gate Is Narrower Than You Think</h1>
<h2 id="the-ancient-pattern">The Ancient Pattern</h2>
<blockquote>
<p><em>"Enter ye in at the strait gate: for wide is the gate, and broad is the way, that leadeth to destruction, and many there be which go in thereat: Because strait is the gate, and narrow is the way, which leadeth unto life, and few there be that find it."</em> — Matthew 7:13-14</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p><em>"I know thy works, that thou art neither cold nor hot: I would thou wert cold or hot. So then because thou art lukewarm, and neither cold nor hot, I will spue thee out of my mouth. Because thou sayest, I am rich, and increased with goods, and have need of nothing; and knowest not that thou art wretched, and miserable, and poor, and blind, and naked."</em> — Revelation 3:15-17</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p><em>"Beloved, believe not every spirit, but try the spirits whether they are of God: because many false prophets are gone out into the world."</em> — 1 John 4:1</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Jesus did not say "many." He said FEW. Few find the narrow gate. Few walk the narrow way. Few enter into life. The word "few" is not ambiguous. It does not mean "a significant minority." It does not mean "less than you might hope but still a respectable number." It means FEW. A remnant. A fraction. A number so small that Jesus chose the strongest possible contrast — MANY on the broad road, FEW on the narrow.</p>
<p>The Laodicean church thought it was rich. It thought it was increased with goods. It thought it had need of nothing. It was, by its own assessment, thriving. Successful. Blessed. And Jesus said it was wretched, miserable, poor, blind, and naked. The gap between what Laodicea believed about itself and what Christ knew about it was absolute.</p>
<p>This is the modern church. And the evidence — from three independent witnesses across three completely different vantage points — is now overwhelming.</p>

<h2 id="the-evidence-is-overwhelming">The Evidence Is Overwhelming</h2>
<h3 id="the-convergent-testimony">The Convergent Testimony</h3>
<p>Over the course of this series, we have examined three independent witnesses who deliver the same devastating verdict on the condition of the modern church. The evidence does not come from a single source. It does not rest on a single testimony. It converges from three completely different directions:</p>
<p><strong>97.5 percent of professing Christians are on Satan's placebo (Howard Pittman).</strong> A Baptist minister for thirty-five years who died and stood before God's throne. God showed him the rejection rate. Not of pagans. Not of atheists. Of professing Christians. People who believed they were saved. People who attended church, read their Bibles, and called on the name of Jesus. 97.5 percent rejected. "They never made Me Lord." They accepted the placebo — the sugarcoated, powerless counterfeit of genuine Christianity — and believed it was real.</p>
<p><strong>Five tiers of demons organized in military hierarchy against you (Pittman).</strong> The demonic realm is not chaotic. It is not random. It is structured in five tiers of increasing authority — from ordinary demons at the bottom to principalities at the top. Each tier has specific functions. Each level reports to the one above it. The organization mirrors a military command structure with perfect chain of command, clear assignments, and disciplined execution. This is what the church is up against. And most Christians do not even believe it exists.</p>
<p><strong>Jesuits have infiltrated every denomination from within (Catholic Confidential).</strong> The Society of Jesus has positioned its agents in every Christian denomination — Catholic and Protestant alike. "Disguised as painters, publishers, writers, schoolmasters." Operating with absolute obedience: "I will believe that white is black." Their objective: "universal spiritual and temporal monarchy." The theology taught in most seminaries, the practices followed in most churches, and the institutional structures that govern church life have all been shaped by an order whose allegiance is not to Christ but to their General — the Black Pope who "rules the world."</p>
<p><strong>Satan worshippers are praying against your church EVERY NIGHT while you cannot pray for one hour (John Ramirez).</strong> A devil worshipper for over twenty-five years who prayed to the devil all night, every night. He observed that most Christians could not sustain even one hour of prayer. No prayer life. No fasting. No genuine relationship with God. "Form of godliness but no power." He broke Christians through witchcraft. He chased everything that represented Jesus Christ. The only ones he could not touch were those with "a real relationship with Jesus Christ."</p>
<p><strong>The five worst principalities are assigned to "cut down the church" (Ramirez).</strong> Five principality demons — the highest-ranking entities in Satan's hierarchy — are specifically assigned to "operate the church, cut down the church, and cut down the opportunity for people" to say yes to Jesus. They do not merely oppose the church. They MANAGE it. They influence its direction, its doctrine, its decisions, and its effectiveness — ensuring that the maximum number of people remain safely on the placebo.</p>
<p>This is the battlefield. This is the situation report. And the church is losing.</p>
<h2 id="what-the-placebo-looks-like">What the Placebo Looks Like</h2>
<h3 id="the-comfortable-counterfeit">The Comfortable Counterfeit</h3>
<p>Satan's placebo does not look like deception. That is the genius of it. It looks like Christianity. It sounds like Christianity. It feels like Christianity. It uses all the right words, all the right rituals, all the right symbols. But it is spiritually inert. It has no power to save. It has no power to transform. It has no power to connect the soul to God.</p>
<p>Here is what the placebo looks like in practice:</p>
<p><strong>Going to church.</strong> Showing up every Sunday. Sitting in the pew. Standing when told to stand. Sitting when told to sit. Listening to the sermon. Putting money in the offering plate. Shaking hands with the people around you. Going home. Repeat next week.</p>
<p><strong>Singing songs.</strong> Worship music on Sunday morning. Worship music in the car. Worship playlists on your phone. The emotional experience of singing about God without the spiritual reality of knowing God. Music that makes you feel something without requiring you to BE something.</p>
<p><strong>Posting Bible verses.</strong> Scripture on social media. Inspirational quotes with mountain backgrounds. "God is good, all the time." Sharing verses without living them. Broadcasting faith without practicing it. Digital religion for a digital age.</p>
<p><strong>Saying "God is good."</strong> The reflexive Christian response to any situation — good or bad. God is good when you get the job. God is good when you lose the job. God is good when you feel good. God is good when you feel nothing. The phrase has become a verbal tic, a social signal, a tribal identifier — stripped of its theological depth and reduced to a bumper sticker.</p>
<p><strong>Tithing.</strong> Giving ten percent (or less) and believing that financial contribution equals spiritual commitment. The institutional church has elevated tithing to a primary mark of discipleship — because the institution needs money to fund its buildings, programs, and staff. But tithing without surrender is not worship. It is a transaction. And the devil is perfectly happy for you to pay your church dues as long as you never genuinely give God your life.</p>
<p><strong>Attending small groups.</strong> Weekly meetings where Christians discuss pre-packaged curriculum, share prayer requests about physical needs, and build social relationships that substitute for genuine spiritual community. The small group has become the church's answer to the need for authentic fellowship — but too often it produces social connection without spiritual transformation.</p>
<p><strong>Having a fish bumper sticker.</strong> The external identification of Christianity without the internal reality. The symbol without the substance. The name without the life.</p>
<p>ALL of this can be done without a genuine relationship with Jesus Christ.</p>
<p>Every single item on this list can be performed by a person who has never surrendered to Christ, never been born again, never received the Holy Spirit, and never entered a genuine covenant relationship with the living God. Satan ALLOWS all of it. He encourages all of it. He designed the placebo to include all of it — because all of it creates the perception of Christianity without the reality.</p>
<p>The one thing Satan will not allow: actually LIVING the surrendered life. Actually dying to self. Actually taking up the cross. Actually following Jesus — not into a comfortable pew on Sunday morning, but into the wilderness of obedience, the desert of fasting, the garden of agonizing prayer, and the daily crucifixion of the flesh.</p>
<h2 id="what-a-real-relationship-looks-like">What a Real Relationship Looks Like</h2>
<h3 id="the-marks-of-genuine-faith">The Marks of Genuine Faith</h3>
<p>If the placebo is a counterfeit, what does the genuine article look like? The three witnesses, taken together, paint a clear picture:</p>
<h3 id="prayer--not-five-minutes-but-hours">Prayer — Not Five Minutes, But Hours</h3>
<p>Ramirez prayed to the devil all night. The Christians he could not break prayed to God for hours. There is a direct correlation between prayer and spiritual authority. The believer who prays five minutes a day has almost no spiritual authority. The believer who prays for hours has authority that principality demons cannot overcome.</p>
<p>This is not about earning God's favor through duration. It is about the depth of relationship that sustained prayer produces. You do not spend hours with someone you barely know. You spend hours with someone you love deeply — someone whose presence transforms you, whose words reshape your thinking, whose companionship is the most valuable thing in your life.</p>
<p>Prayer is not a religious duty. It is the primary expression of a living relationship with God. When that relationship is genuine, prayer flows naturally — not as obligation, but as desire. The believer who cannot pray for more than five minutes does not have a time management problem. He has a relationship problem.</p>
<h3 id="fasting--denying-the-flesh-to-strengthen-the-spirit">Fasting — Denying the Flesh to Strengthen the Spirit</h3>
<p>Jesus said "WHEN you fast" — not "IF you fast" (Matthew 6:16-18). He assumed that His followers would fast. The early church fasted (Acts 13:2-3, 14:23). The Old Testament is saturated with fasting — Moses, David, Elijah, Esther, Daniel, Joel. The practice of denying the physical body in order to sharpen spiritual perception and increase spiritual authority is woven throughout scripture.</p>
<p>The modern church has almost entirely abandoned fasting. It is too uncomfortable. Too inconvenient. Too demanding. The flesh does not want to be denied. And a church that serves the flesh — a placebo church — will not practice the very discipline that weakens the flesh's grip and strengthens the spirit's authority.</p>
<p>Devil worshippers fast. Ramirez confirms this. The servants of Satan understand that spiritual power requires physical sacrifice. They deny their flesh to empower their spirits — for destruction. How much more should the servants of Christ deny their flesh to empower their spirits — for life?</p>
<h3 id="obedience--not-to-an-institution-but-to-the-holy-spirit">Obedience — Not to an Institution, But to the Holy Spirit</h3>
<p>The Jesuits demand absolute obedience to their human General. The institutional church demands obedience to its doctrines, traditions, and leadership structures. But biblical obedience is not to an institution. It is to the Holy Spirit.</p>
<p>"As many as are led by the Spirit of God, they are the sons of God" (Romans 8:14). Sonship is defined by Spirit-led obedience. Not institutional compliance. Not doctrinal agreement. Not cultural conformity. The Spirit of God leading, and the child of God following — moment by moment, decision by decision, step by step.</p>
<p>This kind of obedience is uncomfortable for institutions because it cannot be controlled. The Spirit leads where He will (John 3:8). He does not submit to denominational guidelines. He does not follow institutional protocols. He does not ask permission from the pastoral staff. A church full of Spirit-led believers is a church that no institution can manage — and no principality can control.</p>
<h3 id="discernment--knowing-the-spirits">Discernment — Knowing the Spirits</h3>
<p>"Beloved, believe not every spirit, but try the spirits whether they are of God: because many false prophets are gone out into the world" (1 John 4:1).</p>
<p>This command assumes that spirits are active. It assumes that false prophets are present. It assumes that deception is the norm rather than the exception. And it places the responsibility for spiritual discernment on EVERY believer — not on the pastor, not on the elders, not on the denomination, but on you.</p>
<p>Discernment is the ability to distinguish between the Spirit of God and the spirits of deception. It is the spiritual faculty that detects the placebo. It is the radar system that identifies enemy operations within the church. And it is almost entirely absent from the modern church — because the placebo does not produce it. The placebo produces comfort. Discernment produces discomfort. The placebo says "everything is fine." Discernment says "something is wrong." The placebo church does not want discernment. It wants peace. And the peace it has chosen is the peace of the anesthetized patient who does not feel the cancer growing inside him.</p>
<h3 id="authority--power-over-all-demons">Authority — Power Over All Demons</h3>
<p>"Behold, I give unto you power to tread on serpents and scorpions, and over all the power of the enemy: and nothing shall by any means hurt you" (Luke 10:19).</p>
<p>Jesus gave His followers authority over ALL the power of the enemy. Not some of it. ALL of it. Every demon. Every principality. Every spiritual weapon the enemy possesses. The believer who walks in genuine relationship with Christ has authority that surpasses any devil worshipper, any witch, any warlock, and any principality demon.</p>
<p>Ramirez encountered this authority in the Christians he could not break. The wall of fire around the youth group. The woman who survived twenty-one days of death ritual. The man who disappeared from the rooftop. These believers were exercising — consciously or unconsciously — the authority that Jesus gave to every genuine follower.</p>
<p>But authority that is not exercised is authority that is wasted. A soldier with a weapon he never fires is no threat to the enemy. A believer with authority he never uses is no threat to the kingdom of darkness. And the modern church, sitting on the greatest arsenal of spiritual weapons ever assembled, is no threat to the principalities that manage it — because it does not use what it has been given.</p>
<h3 id="the-blood-of-jesus-as-covering">The Blood of Jesus as Covering</h3>
<p>Every element of genuine spiritual life flows from one source: the blood of Jesus Christ. The blood is the covering. The blood is the authority. The blood is the covenant. The blood is the weapon that no demon can withstand, no principality can overcome, and no devil worshipper can penetrate.</p>
<p>Ramirez's twenty-one-day death ritual failed because the target was covered by the blood of Jesus. His attempted attack on the youth group failed because they were covered by the blood of Jesus. The blood is not a metaphor. It is not a theological concept. It is a spiritual reality with operational power in the invisible realm.</p>
<p>But the blood covers those who are in genuine covenant relationship with Christ. It does not cover those who merely claim the name. It does not cover those who attend church but have never surrendered. It does not cover those on the placebo. "Whatever is not covered with Jesus Christ is an easy target to bring down." This is the devil worshipper's assessment. And it should terrify every Christian who is relying on church membership rather than blood covenant for their spiritual protection.</p>
<h2 id="the-narrow-gate">The Narrow Gate</h2>
<h3 id="few-there-be-that-find-it">"Few There Be That Find It"</h3>
<p>Jesus said the gate is strait and the way is narrow. He said FEW find it. This is the direct, unambiguous statement of the Son of God about the proportion of people who will enter eternal life.</p>
<p>Not many. FEW.</p>
<p>The modern church has inverted this statement. It preaches a wide gate and a broad way. It tells people that salvation is easy — just say the prayer, walk the aisle, get baptized, join the church. It fills stadiums with people who raise their hands and are declared saved on the spot. It counts converts by the thousands and celebrates numerical growth as evidence of spiritual success.</p>
<p>But Jesus said FEW.</p>
<p>Pittman's 97.5 percent rejection rate confirms what Jesus said. If 97.5 percent of professing Christians are rejected, then only 2.5 percent pass through the narrow gate. Out of every hundred people sitting in church on Sunday morning, two or three have a genuine relationship with Jesus Christ. The rest are on the placebo.</p>
<p>This is not a popular message. It is not a message that fills stadiums or sells books or grows church attendance. But it is the message of Jesus Christ, confirmed by a man who stood before God's throne, confirmed by the documented infiltration of the institutional church, and confirmed by a devil worshipper who knew from operational experience which Christians were real and which were not.</p>
<h3 id="the-lukewarm-warning">The Lukewarm Warning</h3>
<p>The Laodicean church is the final of the seven churches addressed in Revelation — and many scholars believe it represents the final age of the church before Christ's return. Its condition is the most dangerous of all seven, because it is the only one that provokes not rebuke but revulsion:</p>
<p>"Because thou art lukewarm, and neither cold nor hot, I will spue thee out of my mouth" (Revelation 3:16).</p>
<p>Lukewarm. Neither hot nor cold. Not on fire for God and not openly opposed to Him. Just... comfortable. Settled. Moderate. Reasonable. The Laodicean church is the church of the comfortable middle — too religious to be worldly, too worldly to be spiritual. It has enough God to feel good and not enough God to be transformed.</p>
<p>And Jesus says He will VOMIT IT OUT OF HIS MOUTH.</p>
<p>This is not gentle correction. This is violent rejection. The lukewarm church produces a visceral reaction in Christ — not the sadness of seeing His people fail, but the revulsion of tasting something that claims to be one thing and is actually another. Lukewarm water that claims to be hot. A placebo that claims to be medicine. A church that claims to be alive and is dead.</p>
<p>The Laodicean church said: "I am rich, and increased with goods, and have need of nothing." It saw itself as blessed, prosperous, and self-sufficient. It had everything it needed — or so it believed.</p>
<p>And Jesus said it was "wretched, and miserable, and poor, and blind, and naked."</p>
<p>Wretched. Miserable. Poor. Blind. Naked. Five adjectives of total spiritual bankruptcy, applied to a church that believed itself to be thriving. The gap between self-perception and reality could not be wider. The placebo could not be more effective. The deception could not be more complete.</p>
<p>This is the modern church. Mega-buildings. Multi-million dollar budgets. Professional worship bands. Celebrity pastors. Bestselling books. Packed auditoriums. And Jesus says: wretched, miserable, poor, blind, naked.</p>
<h2 id="the-call-to-action">The Call to Action</h2>
<h3 id="what-must-change">What Must Change</h3>
<p>The evidence is in. The witnesses have testified. The verdict is unanimous. The question now is: what will you do with this information?</p>
<p>The answer is not complicated. It is not easy, but it is simple. Jesus gave it Himself, to the Laodicean church: "I counsel thee to buy of me gold tried in the fire, that thou mayest be rich; and white raiment, that thou mayest be clothed, and that the shame of thy nakedness do not appear; and anoint thine eyes with eyesalve, that thou mayest see" (Revelation 3:18).</p>
<p><strong>Gold tried in the fire</strong> — genuine faith, tested and refined through suffering, sacrifice, and surrender. Not the easy believism of the modern gospel. Not the comfortable Christianity of the placebo church. Faith that has been through the fire and emerged pure.</p>
<p><strong>White raiment</strong> — the righteousness of Christ, received not through church membership or good works but through genuine relationship and surrender. The covering of the blood. The garment that no principality can tear and no witchcraft can stain.</p>
<p><strong>Eyesalve</strong> — spiritual sight. Discernment. The ability to see what the church in its blindness cannot see: the principalities that operate it, the infiltrators that shape it, the placebo that sedates it, and the narrow gate that it is missing.</p>
<h3 id="behold-i-stand-at-the-door-and-knock">"Behold, I Stand at the Door and Knock"</h3>
<p>The most haunting verse in Revelation 3 is verse 20: "Behold, I stand at the door, and knock: if any man hear my voice, and open the door, I will come in to him, and will sup with him, and he with me."</p>
<p>Jesus is standing OUTSIDE the church, knocking. He is not inside leading it. He is not on the throne at the center of it. He is outside, knocking on the door, asking to be let in to His OWN church. The church that bears His name. The church that claims to worship Him. The church that thinks it is alive.</p>
<p>He is outside. Knocking. Waiting. And the church is too busy with its programs, its music, its social media, its attendance numbers, and its comfortable placebo to hear the knock.</p>
<p>But the promise still stands: "If any man hear my voice, and open the door, I will come in." Not if any church. Not if any denomination. Not if any institution. If any MAN — any individual, any single person willing to hear the knock, willing to open the door, willing to abandon the placebo and embrace the living Christ.</p>
<p>This is the narrow gate. It is not a church door. It is not a denominational affiliation. It is not a doctrinal statement. It is a personal encounter with the living Jesus Christ that produces a radical transformation of life — from self to surrender, from comfort to obedience, from religion to relationship, from the broad way to the narrow path.</p>
<h3 id="the-time-is-now">The Time Is Now</h3>
<p>The alarm has been sounded. Three witnesses. Three perspectives. One unanimous verdict: the church is asleep, the gate is narrow, and the time is short.</p>
<p>Pittman was sent back from God's throne to deliver this message. Ramirez was delivered from the kingdom of darkness to deliver this message. The Jesuit documentation has been preserved through centuries to deliver this message. God has gone to extraordinary lengths to warn His people.</p>
<p>He is not obligated to warn again.</p>
<p>"Remember therefore how thou hast received and heard, and hold fast, and repent" (Revelation 3:3).</p>
<p>Remember. Hold fast. Repent. Three actions. Not tomorrow. Not next Sunday. Not when you feel ready. NOW. Because the principalities are not waiting. The devil worshippers are not waiting. The Jesuits are not waiting. The broad road is full. The narrow gate is closing. And the One who stands outside the church, knocking, will not knock forever.</p>
