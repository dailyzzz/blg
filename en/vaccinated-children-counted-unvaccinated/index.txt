15:T360b,<h1 id="vaccinated-children-being-counted-as-unvaccinated-in-texas-measles-outbreak">Vaccinated Children Being Counted as Unvaccinated in Texas Measles Outbreak</h1>
<h2 id="the-ancient-pattern">The Ancient Pattern</h2>
<blockquote>
<p>"A false witness shall not be unpunished, and he that speaketh lies shall not escape." — Proverbs 19:5</p>
</blockquote>
<p>When the Pharisees brought the woman caught in adultery before Jesus, they presented what appeared to be an open-and-shut case. The evidence was clear. The law was clear. But Jesus asked a question that revealed the corruption of the witnesses: "He that is without sin among you, let him first cast a stone at her" (John 8:7). The accusers departed. The case collapsed — not because the evidence was fabricated, but because the witnesses were corrupt.</p>
<p>As the Confliction track <a href="https://confliction.zzzdaily.com/tracks/en/59-The_Deceivers_Lies.mp4">The Deceivers' Lies</a> reveals, the most effective deceptions weaponize data — counting the righteous as wicked, the obedient as disobedient, the vaccinated as unvaccinated. When the data itself is corrupted, every conclusion drawn from it serves the deceiver's purpose.</p>
<p>Vaccinated children are being counted as unvaccinated in the Texas measles outbreak. The false witness has been discovered. But the damage to truth — and to the children caught between corrupt data and corrupted institutions — continues.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="the-texas-measles-outbreak">The Texas Measles Outbreak</h2>
<p>The Texas measles outbreak triggered national media coverage, CDC alerts, and renewed calls for mandatory vaccination. The narrative was consistent and emphatic: unvaccinated children were driving the outbreak, vaccine hesitancy was to blame, and the solution was increased vaccination rates — including the elimination of religious and philosophical exemptions.</p>
<p>This narrative depended on a critical assumption: that the distinction between "vaccinated" and "unvaccinated" cases was accurately maintained in the outbreak data. That assumption has been undermined by evidence that children who had received one or more doses of the MMR (measles, mumps, rubella) vaccine were being classified as "unvaccinated" in the case count.</p>
<h3 id="how-vaccinated-children-become-unvaccinated">How Vaccinated Children Become "Unvaccinated"</h3>
<p>The misclassification occurs through several mechanisms:</p>
<p><strong>Incomplete series definition:</strong> The CDC considers a child "fully vaccinated" against measles only after receiving two doses of the MMR vaccine — the first at 12-15 months and the second at 4-6 years. A child who has received one dose but not the second is classified as "unvaccinated" or "not fully vaccinated" — despite having received a vaccine dose that the CDC itself states provides approximately 93% protection.</p>
<p><strong>Timing windows:</strong> Children who receive the vaccine within 14 days before symptom onset are classified as "unvaccinated" — because the vaccine is considered to require 14 days to produce immunity. This means a child who was vaccinated, contracted measles during the immunity development window, and became symptomatic is counted as an unvaccinated measles case.</p>
<p><strong>Record gaps:</strong> Children whose vaccination records are incomplete, unavailable, or not provided to public health investigators are defaulted to "unvaccinated" status — regardless of their actual vaccination history.</p>
<p><strong>Vaccine failure classification:</strong> Children who received both doses of the MMR vaccine and still contracted measles (vaccine failure cases) are sometimes classified separately from the "vaccinated" count — appearing in the data as neither vaccinated nor unvaccinated, or being absorbed into the "unvaccinated" count through inconsistent reporting practices.</p>
<p>Karen Kingston has analyzed the data classification issue in The Kingston Report (karenkingston.substack.com): "The classification system is designed to inflate the unvaccinated case count and deflate the vaccinated case count. Every gray area — incomplete series, timing windows, record gaps, vaccine failures — is resolved in the direction that makes unvaccinated populations appear more dangerous and vaccinated populations appear more protected. This is not neutral data collection. This is advocacy disguised as epidemiology."</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="the-political-weaponization-of-outbreak-data">The Political Weaponization of Outbreak Data</h2>
<p>The misclassification of vaccinated children as unvaccinated serves a specific political purpose: it inflates the apparent risk of being unvaccinated, strengthens the case for mandatory vaccination, and undermines the arguments for exemptions — religious, philosophical, or medical.</p>
<p>When media outlets report that "X% of measles cases occurred in unvaccinated children," the public reasonably concludes that vaccination provides robust protection and that unvaccinated children pose a disproportionate risk. But if a significant portion of the "unvaccinated" cases actually received one or more vaccine doses, the conclusion is different: the vaccine's effectiveness may be lower than advertised, and the risk profile of truly unvaccinated individuals may be overstated.</p>
<p>This matters because:</p>
<p><strong>Exemption legislation</strong> is debated on the basis of outbreak data. States considering the elimination of religious or philosophical exemptions cite outbreak case counts — and the proportion of cases in "unvaccinated" populations — as justification.</p>
<p><strong>Mandate enforcement</strong> depends on the public perception that unvaccinated populations pose a danger. Misclassified data inflates this perception.</p>
<p><strong>Vaccine confidence</strong> depends on accurate efficacy data. If vaccinated children are contracting measles at higher rates than reported — because their cases are being reclassified away from the "vaccinated" count — the public is being given a false picture of vaccine effectiveness.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="the-broader-data-integrity-crisis">The Broader Data Integrity Crisis</h2>
<p>The Texas misclassification is not an isolated incident. It reflects a broader crisis in public health data integrity that extends across the vaccination program:</p>
<p><strong>COVID-19 death classification:</strong> During the pandemic, deaths were classified as "COVID-19 deaths" if the deceased tested positive within a specified period before death — regardless of whether COVID-19 was the primary cause. This inflated the COVID-19 death toll by including deaths from other causes in patients who happened to have a positive PCR test.</p>
<p><strong>COVID-19 vaccine adverse event underreporting:</strong> VAERS captures fewer than 1% of adverse events (according to the Harvard Pilgrim Healthcare study), meaning that the vaccine's harm profile is dramatically underreported.</p>
<p><strong>COVID-19 hospitalization classification:</strong> Patients hospitalized "with" COVID-19 (incidental positives) were counted identically to patients hospitalized "for" COVID-19 (primary diagnosis), inflating hospitalization numbers attributed to the disease.</p>

The classification system is not neutral — it is designed to produce results that support the institutional narrative.</p>
