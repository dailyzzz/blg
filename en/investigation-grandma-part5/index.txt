<h2 id="my-grandmothers-story">My Grandmother's Story</h2>
<h3 id="the-voices-began">The Voices Began</h3>
<p>My grandmother began reporting voices in her middle years. She described hearing conversations, music, and sometimes commands or comments directed at her. The experiences were vivid, persistent, and deeply distressing.</p>
<p>She was a woman of faith — a churchgoer, a prayer warrior, a person who took spiritual matters seriously. When the voices began, she initially interpreted them through a spiritual lens. She prayed about them. She sought counsel from her pastor. She tried to discern whether the voices were from God, from demons, or from somewhere else entirely.</p>
<h3 id="the-medical-response">The Medical Response</h3>
<p>Eventually, the family insisted she see a doctor. The doctor listened to her symptoms, noted the absence of other psychotic features (she was oriented, coherent, and functional), and nevertheless diagnosed her with a psychiatric condition. She was prescribed antipsychotic medication.</p>
<p>The medication changed her. The woman who had been sharp, engaged, and spiritually active became dulled, withdrawn, and passive. The voices may have diminished — or she may have simply stopped reporting them, having learned that reporting them led to more medication and more pity.</p>
<p>She spent years on psychiatric medication. The family believed the doctors. The doctors followed their training. And no one — not the family, not the doctors, not the pastors — considered the possibility that the voices had a technological source.</p>
