<h2 id="the-demolition-of-the-white-city">The Demolition of the White City</h2>
<h3 id="the-fires-of-1894">The Fires of 1894</h3>
<p>On January 8, 1894 — just three months after the exposition closed — a fire broke out in the Casino building on the exposition grounds. On February 14, a second fire swept through the Peristyle, the Music Hall, and the Casino. On July 5, a third and final fire destroyed most of the remaining buildings, including the Manufactures and Liberal Arts Building, the Administration Building, and the majority of the Court of Honor.</p>
<p>Three "accidental" fires in seven months, each conveniently destroying a major section of the exposition grounds. The official explanation: arson by vagrants who had taken up residence in the abandoned buildings. This is the same caliber of explanation as Mrs. O'Leary's cow — a convenient story that precludes further investigation.</p>
<p>Consider the logistics. The Manufactures Building covered 44 acres. Its steel frame weighed millions of pounds. Its glass and iron roof was one of the engineering marvels of the age. A fire started by homeless squatters could not have destroyed this building. The temperatures required to collapse the steel frame exceed what any ordinary fire can produce. This was a controlled demolition, executed by professionals, and disguised as an accident.</p>
