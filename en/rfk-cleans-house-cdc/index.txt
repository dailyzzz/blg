15:T4f9f,<h1 id="rfk-jr-cleans-house-at-the-cdc">RFK Jr. Cleans House at the CDC</h1>
<h2 id="the-ancient-pattern">The Ancient Pattern</h2>
<blockquote>
<p>"Woe unto them that call evil good, and good evil; that put darkness for light, and light for darkness; that put bitter for sweet, and sweet for bitter!" — Isaiah 5:20</p>
</blockquote>
<p>When the Israelites entered the Promised Land, God commanded them to remove every altar to foreign gods, tear down every high place, and cut down every Asherah pole. The instruction was not optional. It was not gradual. It was total and immediate. The reason was plain: the people who built those altars had embedded corruption into the very infrastructure of the land, and no amount of reform could succeed while those structures remained standing.</p>
<p>As the Confliction track <a href="https://confliction.zzzdaily.com/tracks/en/67-The_Enemy_Within.mp4">The Enemy Within</a> reveals, the most dangerous adversary is not the one at the gate — it is the one already inside the walls, wearing the uniform of the institution it has corrupted. The CDC was established to protect the American people from disease. Instead, it became the instrument through which disease management was monetized, pharmaceutical products were mandated, and dissenting physicians were silenced.</p>
<p>RFK Jr. has entered the temple and begun overturning the tables of the money changers. The question is whether the purge goes deep enough.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="the-cdc-a-history-of-capture">The CDC: A History of Capture</h2>
<p>The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention was established in 1946 as the Communicable Disease Center, originally focused on malaria control. Over the following decades, its mission expanded to encompass virtually every aspect of public health — from infectious disease surveillance to chronic disease prevention, environmental health, injury prevention, and occupational safety.</p>
<p>Today the CDC commands an annual budget exceeding $17 billion and employs more than 12,000 people. It operates the Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices (ACIP), whose recommendations are automatically adopted by every state in the union as the childhood vaccination schedule. When ACIP recommends a vaccine, it becomes a de facto mandate — schools require it, insurance covers it, and physicians who question it risk professional sanction.</p>
<p>Karen Kingston has documented extensively in The Kingston Report (karenkingston.substack.com) that the CDC's regulatory function is structurally compromised by its financial entanglements. The CDC Foundation — a congressionally chartered nonprofit — receives direct funding from pharmaceutical companies, including Pfizer, Johnson &#x26; Johnson, Merck, and GlaxoSmithKline. In fiscal year 2020-2021 alone, the CDC Foundation received over $250 million in contributions, with significant portions coming from the pharmaceutical and biotech industries.</p>
<p>Kingston has detailed the revolving door between the CDC and the pharmaceutical industry. Former CDC directors have gone on to leadership positions at Merck (Julie Gerberding, who became Merck's Executive Vice President and was later named Chief Patient Officer), and former CDC officials routinely transition to pharmaceutical consulting, lobbying, and advisory roles. The traffic flows in both directions — pharmaceutical industry executives and consultants rotate into CDC advisory positions, carrying their corporate interests through the revolving door.</p>
<p>"The CDC does not regulate the pharmaceutical industry," Kingston has stated. "The pharmaceutical industry regulates the CDC. When you follow the money, you find that every major decision point — which vaccines are recommended, which studies are funded, which safety signals are investigated — traces back to financial relationships that would constitute criminal conflicts of interest in any other context."</p>
<h3 id="the-vaccine-advisory-committee-industry-by-another-name">The Vaccine Advisory Committee: Industry by Another Name</h3>
<p>The ACIP — the committee whose recommendations determine which vaccines are added to the childhood schedule — has been repeatedly documented to include members with direct financial ties to the manufacturers whose products they are voting to recommend. A 2009 Office of Inspector General report found that 97% of ACIP members had at least one conflict of interest, and 64% had conflicts specifically related to the vaccines they were voting on.</p>
<p>These conflicts were not hidden. They were documented, disclosed, and waived. The system was designed to permit conflicts of interest, not prevent them. The waiver mechanism ensured that industry-connected individuals could continue to sit on the committee and vote on products manufactured by companies that paid them.</p>
<p>When ACIP votes to add a vaccine to the childhood schedule, the decision carries enormous financial consequences. A single ACIP recommendation can generate billions of dollars in revenue for a vaccine manufacturer, as every child in America becomes a potential customer through state school-entry requirements. The committee members who make these decisions are, by the OIG's own documentation, financially connected to the companies that benefit.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="the-purge-begins">The Purge Begins</h2>
<p>RFK Jr., serving as Secretary of Health and Human Services, initiated a systematic removal of CDC leadership officials identified as having direct pharmaceutical industry conflicts, histories of suppressing safety data, or involvement in the censorship of dissenting scientific voices during the COVID-19 pandemic.</p>
<p>The purge targeted senior officials across multiple divisions, including:</p>
<ul>
<li>The National Center for Immunization and Respiratory Diseases (NCIRD), which oversees the COVID-19 vaccination program and the childhood immunization schedule</li>
<li>The Center for Preparedness and Response (CPR), which coordinated the CDC's pandemic response</li>
<li>The Office of the Director, which sets overall agency policy and communications strategy</li>
<li>The Division of Healthcare Quality Promotion (DHQP), which oversees infection control guidelines that were used to justify lockdowns, masking mandates, and social distancing requirements</li>
</ul>
<p>Sources within HHS confirmed that the removals were not random reassignments or early retirements dressed up as reform. They were terminations — direct removals of personnel identified through an internal review process that examined financial disclosures, communications records, and decision-making patterns during the pandemic response.</p>
<h3 id="the-reaction">The Reaction</h3>
<p>The public health establishment responded with fury. Former CDC officials appeared on cable news networks to denounce the purge as "political interference" in science. Medical journals published editorials warning of a "brain drain" at the nation's premier public health agency. Professional associations released statements expressing "deep concern" for the future of public health infrastructure.</p>
<p>These reactions follow a predictable pattern. When captured institutions face reform, they frame the reform as an attack on the institution itself. The argument is always the same: the institution is too important to be held accountable. Its mission is too vital for its leaders to face consequences. Any attempt to remove compromised officials is reframed as an assault on science, public health, or national security.</p>
<p>But the CDC's mission — protecting the American people from disease — was betrayed not by RFK Jr.'s purge but by the officials he removed. It was betrayed when the agency recommended COVID-19 injections for children despite the absence of long-term safety data. It was betrayed when the agency suppressed early treatment options. It was betrayed when the agency collaborated with social media companies to censor physicians who reported adverse events. It was betrayed when the agency's own VAERS system collected hundreds of thousands of adverse event reports and the agency took no public action to investigate them.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="karen-kingstons-documentation-of-cdc-corruption">Karen Kingston's Documentation of CDC Corruption</h2>
<p>Karen Kingston's research, published consistently through The Kingston Report, has provided a granular, document-level analysis of CDC corruption that goes beyond the general critique of regulatory capture.</p>
<p>Kingston has documented that the CDC held patents on coronavirus-related technology — creating a direct financial interest in the development and deployment of coronavirus countermeasures. When an agency that profits from a disease's treatment also serves as the regulatory authority overseeing that treatment, the conflict is not merely theoretical — it is structural.</p>
<p>Kingston has further documented that the CDC's communications during the pandemic were coordinated with pharmaceutical companies and social media platforms through what she terms a "censorship-industrial complex." Internal communications obtained through FOIA requests revealed that CDC officials flagged specific social media posts for removal, identified specific physicians and researchers for platform enforcement action, and coordinated messaging strategies with the White House, the Surgeon General's office, and Big Tech content moderation teams.</p>
<p>"The CDC was not functioning as a public health agency during COVID-19," Kingston has written. "It was functioning as the communications and enforcement arm of the pharmaceutical industry. Its role was to promote the products, suppress the critics, and manage the narrative. Everything else — the science, the data, the safety monitoring — was subordinated to that function."</p>
<h3 id="the-childhood-vaccine-schedule-scandal">The Childhood Vaccine Schedule Scandal</h3>
<p>One of the most significant areas of CDC corruption documented by Kingston and other researchers involves the childhood vaccination schedule itself. The CDC currently recommends over 70 doses of 16 different vaccines by age 18, with the majority administered before age six. This schedule has expanded dramatically over the past four decades — in 1983, the CDC recommended 24 doses of 7 vaccines by age 18.</p>
<p>No long-term study has ever been conducted comparing the health outcomes of fully vaccinated children against completely unvaccinated children. The CDC has repeatedly refused to conduct such a study, claiming it would be "unethical" to leave children unvaccinated. This reasoning is circular: the agency assumes the vaccines are safe and beneficial, and therefore refuses to conduct the study that would test that assumption.</p>
<p>Independent researchers who have attempted to conduct such comparisons using available data have consistently found that unvaccinated children have lower rates of chronic disease, autoimmune conditions, allergies, ADHD, autism spectrum disorder, and other conditions. The CDC has dismissed these studies without conducting its own.</p>
<p>RFK Jr. has made the childhood vaccine schedule a central focus of his reform efforts. His stated goal is not to eliminate vaccines but to subject them to the same rigorous safety testing applied to every other pharmaceutical product — including long-term, placebo-controlled clinical trials and fully powered studies comparing vaccinated and unvaccinated populations.</p>

<h2 id="the-beast-systems-institutional-arms">The Beast System's Institutional Arms</h2>
<p>The ZZZ Daily track <a href="https://zzzdaily.zzzdaily.com/tracks/en/103-MKUltra_Never_Ended.mp4">MKUltra Never Ended</a> documents the continuity between historic government programs of population control and contemporary institutional mechanisms. The CDC is one such mechanism — an instrument that translates pharmaceutical industry objectives into government policy, government policy into mandates, and mandates into population-wide compliance.</p>
<p>The track <a href="https://zzzdaily.zzzdaily.com/tracks/en/109-Biometric_Beast_System.mp4">Biometric Beast System</a> exposes the architecture of medical compliance — a system in which institutional recommendations become requirements, requirements become conditions for social participation, and social participation becomes contingent on submission to pharmaceutical products.</p>
<p>The CDC occupies a critical node in this architecture. It is the agency that determines which products are recommended, which recommendations become mandates, and which mandates are enforced through school-entry requirements, employment conditions, and military orders. When this node is captured by the industry it is supposed to regulate, the entire system inverts — protection becomes predation, public health becomes pharmaceutical revenue, and the agency charged with defending the population becomes the instrument of its exploitation.</p>

<h2 id="the-biblical-precedent-for-institutional-purge">The Biblical Precedent for Institutional Purge</h2>
<blockquote>
<p>"And Jesus went into the temple of God, and cast out all them that sold and bought in the temple, and overthrew the tables of the moneychangers, and the seats of them that sold doves, And said unto them, It is written, My house shall be called the house of prayer; but ye have made it a den of thieves." — Matthew 21:12-13</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Christ did not reform the money changers. He did not negotiate with them. He did not form a committee to study their business practices. He overturned their tables and drove them out. The corruption was total, the remedy was total, and the authority behind the remedy was absolute.</p>
<p>RFK Jr. is not Christ. The CDC is not the Temple. But the pattern is instructive. When an institution created for sacred purpose — the protection of human life — has been converted into a commercial enterprise that profits from the suffering of those it was meant to protect, incremental reform is insufficient. The tables must be overturned.</p>
<p>The question that remains is whether this purge represents a genuine transformation of federal health policy or merely a reshuffling of personnel within a system whose structural incentives remain unchanged. If the CDC Foundation continues to accept pharmaceutical industry funding, if the ACIP continues to seat members with manufacturer conflicts, if the revolving door between the CDC and pharmaceutical boardrooms continues to spin — then the purge, however dramatic, will be temporary. New faces will occupy the same chairs, subject to the same pressures, producing the same outcomes.</p>
<p>True reform requires structural change: the elimination of pharmaceutical industry funding to the CDC and its foundation, the prohibition of industry conflicts on advisory committees, the end of the revolving door, the restoration of liability for vaccine manufacturers, and the conduct of the long-term safety studies that the agency has refused to perform for decades.</p>

The modern political system is two wings of the same bird — left and right, Republican and Democrat — all serving the same hidden power structure.
