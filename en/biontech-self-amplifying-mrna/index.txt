<h2 id="the-presentations-what-biontech-has-shown">The Presentations: What BioNTech Has Shown</h2>
<h3 id="corporate-and-investor-presentations">Corporate and Investor Presentations</h3>
<p>BioNTech's corporate presentations — delivered to investors, analysts, and industry conferences — provide an unfiltered view of the company's strategic vision. These are not marketing materials for the public. They are documents intended for shareholders and industry partners, and they describe BioNTech's technology with a candor that is absent from public-facing communications.</p>
<p>Key revelations from these presentations include:</p>
<p><strong>sa-mRNA as Core Platform:</strong> BioNTech describes self-amplifying mRNA as one of its core technology platforms — not an experimental side project, but a central pillar of its commercial and research strategy. The platform is being developed for:</p>
<ul>
<li>Infectious disease vaccines (influenza, RSV, and others)</li>
<li>Cancer immunotherapy (individualized neoantigen vaccines)</li>
<li>Autoimmune disease treatments</li>
<li>Rare disease therapies</li>
<li>Combination products (multiple antigens in single constructs)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Lower Doses, Higher Effect:</strong> Presentations emphasize that sa-mRNA requires dramatically lower doses than conventional mRNA because the construct replicates inside the body. Where conventional mRNA requires 30-100 micrograms per dose, sa-mRNA constructs require as little as 0.1-1 microgram. The body does the manufacturing. The injection is merely the seed.</p>
<p><strong>Extended Duration of Protein Production:</strong> BioNTech presentations show data indicating that sa-mRNA constructs produce target proteins for significantly longer periods than conventional mRNA — weeks to months rather than days to weeks. This extended production is presented as a benefit (longer immune stimulation) without acknowledging the extended duration of harm (longer spike protein production, longer p53 suppression, longer immune dysregulation).</p>
<p><strong>Alphavirus Backbone Technology:</strong> The presentations confirm that BioNTech's sa-mRNA constructs are built on alphavirus replication machinery — the same viral replication engine used by ARCT-154 (the first approved sa-mRNA product in Japan). The nonstructural proteins (nsP1-nsP4) from alphaviruses are retained to form the self-replicating engine, while the structural proteins are replaced with the target antigen.</p>
<h3 id="scientific-publications">Scientific Publications</h3>
<p>BioNTech's scientific publications in peer-reviewed journals provide the technical details behind the corporate vision:</p>
<p><strong>Replicase Complex Optimization:</strong> Published research describes how BioNTech has optimized the alphavirus replicase complex for maximum efficiency in human cells. The replicase — the enzyme that copies the mRNA — has been engineered for:</p>
<ul>
<li>Higher copy number (more copies per cell)</li>
<li>Longer activity duration (replication continues for extended periods)</li>
<li>Broader tissue tropism (the construct can replicate in more cell types)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>LNP Delivery System Advances:</strong> Publications describe next-generation lipid nanoparticle formulations designed specifically for sa-mRNA delivery. These LNPs are optimized for:</p>
<ul>
<li>Enhanced cellular uptake</li>
<li>Broader tissue distribution</li>
<li>Longer circulation time in the bloodstream</li>
<li>Immune evasion (avoiding premature clearance)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Combination Constructs:</strong> BioNTech has published research on sa-mRNA constructs encoding multiple antigens — a single self-replicating genetic program that produces multiple foreign proteins simultaneously. This represents the convergence of self-amplifying technology with multi-disease coverage.</p>

<p>Kingston has translated BioNTech's technical language into plain implications:</p>
<p><strong>"Lower dose required"</strong> means: The initial injection is small, but the amount of genetic material in your body grows exponentially as the construct replicates. The dose you received is not the dose your body experiences. Your body manufactures a dose many times larger than what was injected.</p>
<p><strong>"Extended duration of protein production"</strong> means: Your cells produce a foreign protein for weeks or months. Every day of production is a day of potential harm — spike protein toxicity, p53 suppression, immune dysregulation, cardiac damage, placental damage, and all other documented harms of spike protein persistence.</p>
<p><strong>"Alphavirus backbone"</strong> means: The replication engine inside your cells comes from a family of dangerous viruses. The structural proteins were removed (so you theoretically cannot form infectious virus particles), but the replication machinery is fully functional. Your cells are running viral replication software.</p>
<p><strong>"Broader tissue tropism"</strong> means: The construct can replicate in more types of cells throughout the body — including cells in the heart, brain, liver, ovaries, and other organs. More cell types producing spike protein means more tissue damage across more organs.</p>
<p><strong>"Immune evasion"</strong> means: The construct is designed to avoid being destroyed by the immune system. This ensures maximum replication and maximum protein production — but it also ensures that the body cannot shut down the process even if it is causing harm.</p>

<p>Kingston has documented that informed consent for self-amplifying mRNA is functionally impossible:</p>
<ol>
<li>
<p><strong>The dose is unpredictable.</strong> Because the construct replicates, the actual amount of genetic material and protein produced in any individual depends on factors that cannot be controlled or predicted — including the person's immune status, age, genetics, and the number of cells transfected.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>The duration is unknown.</strong> There is no established method to determine how long the sa-mRNA construct will continue replicating in any individual. It could be weeks, months, or — if integration into the genome occurs — permanent.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>The off switch does not exist.</strong> Once injected, there is no known pharmaceutical intervention to stop the construct from replicating. The recipient has no way to halt the process.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Shedding is unstudied.</strong> Whether the self-amplifying construct — not just the protein product but the replicating RNA itself — can be shed from vaccinated to unvaccinated individuals is not adequately characterized. </li>
</ol>
<p>How can anyone give informed consent to a product when the dose is unpredictable, the duration is unknown, there is no off switch, and the exposure of non-consenting third parties is unstudied?</p>
