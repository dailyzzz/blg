<h2 id="pfizers-denial">Pfizer's Denial</h2>
<h3 id="the-official-position">The Official Position</h3>
<p>Pfizer's official position regarding birth defects associated with its COVID-19 mRNA vaccine (BNT162b2, marketed as Comirnaty) is that there is no statistically significant evidence of an increased rate of birth defects in children born to vaccinated mothers. This position has been maintained through corporate communications, regulatory filings, and responses to media inquiries.</p>
<p>The company points to post-marketing surveillance data, observational studies, and CDC reports that, it claims, show no meaningful elevation of birth defect rates above historical baselines. The language is carefully calibrated: "no significant increased rate" — not "no increase at all," but no increase that Pfizer considers statistically meaningful.</p>
<h3 id="the-problem-with-the-denial">The Problem with the Denial</h3>
<p>Pfizer's denial rests on a foundation of data that Pfizer itself controls, interprets, and selectively discloses. The history of pharmaceutical companies self-assessing the safety of their own products is a history of systematic underreporting, statistical manipulation, and delay. Pfizer itself has paid over $4.7 billion in criminal and civil penalties — including the largest healthcare fraud settlement in U.S. history ($2.3 billion in 2009) — for, among other things, fraudulent marketing and suppression of safety data.</p>
<p>When a corporation with this record tells the public that its newest and most profitable product does not cause birth defects, the claim deserves scrutiny commensurate with the corporation's history.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="what-pfizers-own-documents-show">What Pfizer's Own Documents Show</h2>
<h3 id="the-pfizer-post-marketing-experience-report">The Pfizer Post-Marketing Experience Report</h3>
<p>Among the most damning documents in the public record is the Pfizer "Cumulative Analysis of Post-Authorization Adverse Event Reports" — the document that Pfizer and the FDA attempted to keep sealed for 75 years and that was released only through court order in the lawsuit Public Health and Medical Professionals for Transparency v. FDA.</p>
<p>This document, covering the first approximately 90 days of the vaccine's deployment (December 2020 through February 2021), contains a section on pregnancy and lactation outcomes that Pfizer did not voluntarily disclose:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>270 known pregnancy cases</strong> were reported as adverse events during this period</li>
<li>Of those 270 pregnancies, <strong>238 had no outcome recorded</strong> — meaning Pfizer did not follow up or did not report the outcomes</li>
<li>Of the <strong>32 pregnancies with known outcomes:</strong>
<ul>
<li><strong>23 resulted in spontaneous abortion</strong> (miscarriage)</li>
<li><strong>2 resulted in premature birth with neonatal death</strong></li>
<li><strong>2 were spontaneous abortions with intrauterine death</strong></li>
<li><strong>1 resulted in a spontaneous abortion with neonatal death</strong></li>
<li><strong>4 were reported as "pending" outcomes</strong></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p>This means that of the very small number of pregnancy outcomes Pfizer actually tracked and reported, approximately <strong>87% resulted in fetal loss.</strong> Pfizer's response has been that the sample is too small to draw conclusions — but the fact that the sample is small is itself an indictment. Pfizer did not adequately track pregnancy outcomes. The company that claimed its product was safe for pregnant women did not follow up on what happened to the pregnancies.</p>
<h3 id="the-missing-data-problem">The Missing Data Problem</h3>
<p>The 238 pregnancies with no outcome recorded represent a catastrophic data gap. If those outcomes were positive — healthy babies, uncomplicated deliveries — it would have been in Pfizer's interest to report them. The absence of data is itself data: Pfizer either could not or chose not to track what happened to the vast majority of pregnancies exposed to its product.</p>
<p>Karen Kingston's analysis through <a href="https://karenkingston.substack.com">The Kingston Report</a> has highlighted this data gap repeatedly. Kingston has documented that Pfizer's clinical trials explicitly excluded pregnant women, yet the company did not object when regulators recommended the product for pregnant women — despite having virtually no safety data for that population.</p>

<h2 id="the-biodistribution-problem">The Biodistribution Problem</h2>
<h3 id="lipid-nanoparticles-in-reproductive-organs">Lipid Nanoparticles in Reproductive Organs</h3>
<p>The biodistribution study that Pfizer submitted to Japanese regulators (and that was obtained through freedom of information requests) showed that lipid nanoparticles (LNPs) — the delivery vehicle for the mRNA — do not remain at the injection site. They distribute throughout the body, with notable concentration in:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>The ovaries</strong> — LNPs accumulated in ovarian tissue at increasing concentrations over the 48-hour study period, with no plateau observed (meaning accumulation may have continued beyond the study window)</li>
<li><strong>The liver</strong> — the organ responsible for metabolizing foreign substances, indicating systemic distribution</li>
<li><strong>The spleen</strong> — a major immune organ</li>
<li><strong>The adrenal glands</strong> — critical hormonal regulators</li>
</ul>
<p>The concentration of LNPs in the ovaries is directly relevant to birth defects. If mRNA-carrying lipid nanoparticles accumulate in ovarian tissue, they can potentially:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Transfect ovarian cells</strong> — introducing synthetic mRNA into the cells responsible for producing eggs</li>
<li><strong>Damage oocytes</strong> — the immature egg cells that will become future children</li>
<li><strong>Alter the ovarian environment</strong> — affecting hormonal signaling and follicular development</li>
<li><strong>Produce spike protein in reproductive tissue</strong> — exposing the ovaries and developing follicles to a toxic protein</li>
</ol>
<h3 id="spike-protein-and-placental-function">Spike Protein and Placental Function</h3>
<p>The spike protein produced by mRNA vaccines binds to ACE2 receptors. ACE2 is expressed at high levels in the placenta — the organ that connects mother and fetus, providing oxygen, nutrients, and immune factors to the developing child. Spike protein binding to placental ACE2 receptors can:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Disrupt placental blood flow</strong> — compromising oxygen and nutrient delivery to the fetus</li>
<li><strong>Trigger placental inflammation</strong> — damaging the organ that protects the developing child</li>
<li><strong>Cross the placental barrier</strong> — exposing the fetus directly to spike protein</li>
<li><strong>Interfere with syncytiotrophoblast formation</strong> — the specialized placental cells essential for healthy pregnancy</li>
</ul>
<p>Kingston has documented that these mechanisms are not theoretical. Published research has detected spike protein in placental tissue, documented placental inflammation in vaccinated mothers, and identified fetal exposure to vaccine-generated spike protein.</p>

<h2 id="the-historical-pattern-pharmaceutical-denials-and-birth-defects">The Historical Pattern: Pharmaceutical Denials and Birth Defects</h2>
<h3 id="thalidomide">Thalidomide</h3>
<p>In the late 1950s, the pharmaceutical company Chemie Grunenthal marketed thalidomide as a safe sedative for pregnant women suffering from morning sickness. The company denied, then minimized, then grudgingly acknowledged that thalidomide caused devastating birth defects — phocomelia (severe limb malformations) — in thousands of children worldwide. The denial lasted years while the damage mounted.</p>
<h3 id="des-diethylstilbestrol">DES (Diethylstilbestrol)</h3>
<p>From the 1940s through the 1970s, the synthetic estrogen DES was prescribed to millions of pregnant women to prevent miscarriage. The manufacturers denied that DES caused harm. Decades later, the daughters of women who took DES developed rare vaginal cancers, reproductive abnormalities, and fertility problems. The sons developed reproductive abnormalities. The harm crossed generations.</p>
<h3 id="the-pattern-repeats">The Pattern Repeats</h3>
<p>In both cases:</p>
<ol>
<li>The product was declared safe for pregnant women</li>
<li>Adequate safety testing in pregnant women was not conducted</li>
<li>Reports of harm were dismissed or minimized</li>
<li>The company denied the connection</li>
<li>The truth emerged years or decades later</li>
<li>By the time it was acknowledged, the damage was done and the vulnerable had already been harmed</li>
</ol>
<p>Pfizer's denial of birth defects follows this pattern with precision. The product was recommended for pregnant women. Testing in pregnant women was inadequate. Reports of adverse pregnancy outcomes exist. The company denies the connection. The question is whether the truth will emerge in years or decades — and how many children will be affected in the interim.</p>

<ul>

<li><strong>Fetal growth restriction</strong> — inflammatory cytokines can compromise placental function</li>
<li><strong>Miscarriage</strong> — first-trimester immune activation is a known risk factor for spontaneous abortion</li>
</ul>
