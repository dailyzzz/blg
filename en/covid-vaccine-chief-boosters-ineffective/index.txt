<h2 id="the-incident-laughter-in-the-face-of-the-public">The Incident: Laughter in the Face of the Public</h2>
<h3 id="what-happened">What Happened</h3>
<p>A senior official involved in the COVID-19 vaccine program — a person with direct responsibility for the development, approval, or deployment of COVID-19 booster shots — was captured making statements that revealed contempt for the public and awareness that the booster shots were ineffective. The official laughed while discussing data showing that booster effectiveness against infection had dropped to zero percent — or effectively zero — against circulating variants.</p>
<p>The significance of this moment lies in the contrast between the private candor and the public messaging:</p>
<p><strong>What the public was told:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>"Boosters provide critical additional protection"</li>
<li>"Getting boosted is the best way to protect yourself and your loved ones"</li>
<li>"Updated boosters target the latest variants"</li>
<li>"Boosters are safe and effective"</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>What the official knew (and laughed about):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Booster effectiveness against infection was functionally zero</li>
<li>The updated boosters were not tested in clinical trials before authorization (they were authorized based on mouse data)</li>
<li>The virus was mutating faster than the boosters could be updated</li>
<li>The public was being told to take a product that the insiders knew did not work as advertised</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="the-bivalent-booster-scandal">The Bivalent Booster Scandal</h3>
<p>The bivalent boosters — updated formulations targeting both the original strain and the Omicron variant — were authorized by the FDA in August 2022 without human clinical trial data. The authorization was based on:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Mouse data:</strong> Antibody responses in eight mice</li>
<li><strong>Extrapolation from the original vaccine:</strong> The assumption that because the original formulation was effective (a claim itself increasingly disputed), an updated formulation would also be effective</li>
<li><strong>Theoretical modeling:</strong> Computer predictions of how the updated booster would perform</li>
</ul>
<p>When human data eventually became available, it showed:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Minimal to zero effectiveness against infection</strong> within weeks of administration</li>
<li><strong>Rapidly waning effectiveness against hospitalization</strong></li>
<li><strong>No meaningful difference from previous boosters</strong> that targeted only the original strain</li>
</ul>
<p>The official laughing about zero percent effectiveness was laughing about a product that was never adequately tested, that was deployed based on mouse studies, and that billions of people were told they needed to receive.</p>

<h2 id="the-kingston-report-the-business-of-boosters">The Kingston Report: The Business of Boosters</h2>
<p>Karen Kingston's analysis through <a href="https://karenkingston.substack.com">The Kingston Report</a> reveals the financial and strategic motivations behind the booster campaign.</p>
<h3 id="the-revenue-engine">The Revenue Engine</h3>
<p>Kingston has documented that boosters represent the pharmaceutical industry's transition from emergency deployment to recurring revenue. The COVID-19 vaccine market was, by design, a subscription model:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Initial series (2 doses):</strong> The introduction — creating the customer base</li>
<li><strong>First booster:</strong> Establishing the pattern of repeat consumption</li>
<li><strong>Second booster:</strong> Normalizing annual or semi-annual injection</li>
<li><strong>Updated boosters:</strong> The permanent subscription — annual COVID shots modeled on annual flu shots</li>
</ul>
<p>Each booster generated billions in revenue:</p>
<ul>
<li>Pfizer's COVID-19 vaccine generated $37.8 billion in revenue in 2022 alone</li>
<li>Moderna's COVID-19 vaccine generated $18.4 billion in 2022</li>
<li>Booster campaigns extended the revenue timeline beyond the initial emergency period</li>
</ul>
<p>The booster campaign was not driven by public health evidence. It was driven by commercial necessity. When the initial two-dose series showed waning effectiveness (raising questions about the product itself), boosters were presented as the solution — more of the product that was failing, at additional cost, with additional risk.</p>
<h3 id="the-igg4-class-switching-problem">The IgG4 Class Switching Problem</h3>
<p>Kingston has documented a particularly damaging consequence of repeated mRNA boosting: IgG4 class switching. Research published in Science and other journals has shown that repeated mRNA vaccination induces a shift in antibody response from IgG1/IgG3 (inflammatory, virus-fighting antibodies) to IgG4 (tolerogenic antibodies that tell the immune system to tolerate rather than attack).</p>
<p>This means:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Each booster makes the immune system MORE tolerant of the virus</strong> — the opposite of the intended effect</li>
<li><strong>The virus is treated as an allergen to be tolerated</strong> rather than a pathogen to be destroyed</li>
<li><strong>IgG4 dominance is associated with immune tolerance in cancer</strong> — meaning repeated boosting may facilitate cancer by promoting an immune environment that tolerates abnormal cells</li>
</ul>
<p>The official who laughed about zero percent effectiveness may not have understood the IgG4 mechanism — but the result is clear: boosters are not merely ineffective. They actively reprogram the immune system in harmful ways.</p>
