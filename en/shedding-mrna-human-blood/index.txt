<h2 id="the-evidence-for-shedding">The Evidence for Shedding</h2>
<p>The term "shedding" refers to the transmission of vaccine components — including spike protein, lipid nanoparticles, and synthetic mRNA — from vaccinated individuals to unvaccinated individuals through close contact. <p>Pfizer's Phase 1/2/3 clinical trial protocol (document C4591001) included specific provisions for reporting what it termed "Environmental Exposure During Pregnancy or Breastfeeding." These provisions described scenarios in which an unvaccinated individual could be exposed to the vaccine through:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Inhalation</strong> — breathing air in proximity to a vaccinated individual</li>
<li><strong>Skin contact</strong> — touching a vaccinated individual</li>
</ul>
<p>The protocol required that these exposure events be reported to Pfizer's safety monitoring system. The very existence of this reporting requirement in a clinical trial for a product described as "non-transmissible" constitutes an admission that transmission was anticipated and considered a safety concern.</p>
<p>If the product could not shed, why did the manufacturer include a protocol for monitoring shedding events? The answer is self-evident.</p>
