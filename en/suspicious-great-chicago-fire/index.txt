<p>The Chicago Fire did not occur in isolation. On the same night — October 8, 1871 — massive fires broke out across the Upper Midwest:</p>

<li><strong>The Great Michigan Fire</strong> burned across the entire state, destroying the cities of Holland and Manistee and burning over 2.5 million acres.</li>
<li><strong>Port Huron, Michigan</strong> was largely destroyed by fire on the same night.</li>
</ul>
<p>The simultaneous eruption of massive fires across hundreds of miles of territory cannot be explained by a cow, by drought, or by any natural phenomenon.
