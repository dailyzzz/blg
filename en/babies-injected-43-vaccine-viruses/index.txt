<p>This aluminum is not ingested (where approximately 0.3% is absorbed). It is injected directly into muscle tissue and enters the bloodstream and lymphatic system. <ul>
<li>Cross the blood-brain barrier</li>
<li>Accumulate in brain tissue</li>
<li>Trigger neuroinflammation</li>
<li>Persist in the body for years</li>
</ul>
<p>The FDA's maximum recommended aluminum exposure for intravenous feeding in premature infants is 4-5 micrograms per kilogram per day. A 12-pound (5.4 kg) infant at a 2-month well-baby visit receiving multiple aluminum-containing vaccines can receive over 1,000 micrograms of aluminum in a single visit — far exceeding the FDA's own safety threshold for injected aluminum.</p>

<h2 id="the-developing-immune-system">The Developing Immune System</h2>
<h3 id="gods-design-gradual-training">God's Design: Gradual Training</h3>
<p>The infant immune system develops through a divinely designed sequence:</p>
<ol>
<li>
<p><strong>Maternal antibodies (passive immunity):</strong> At birth, the infant carries maternal IgG antibodies transferred across the placenta. These antibodies provide protection for the first months of life while the infant's own immune system develops.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Breast milk immunity:</strong> Breast milk contains IgA antibodies, lactoferrin, lysozyme, and immune cells that provide ongoing passive protection and actively train the infant gut microbiome — a critical component of immune development.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Natural exposure:</strong> As the infant encounters environmental microbes through normal life — skin contact, breathing, oral exploration — the adaptive immune system gradually learns to recognize and respond to pathogens. This training is graded and sequential, matching the immune system's developmental capacity.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Microbiome development:</strong> The gut microbiome — home to 70% of the immune system — develops over the first 2-3 years of life. Its development is influenced by birth method, breastfeeding, diet, and environmental exposure. Disruption of microbiome development through pharmaceutical interventions (including vaccines and antibiotics) can permanently alter immune function.</p>
</li>
</ol>
