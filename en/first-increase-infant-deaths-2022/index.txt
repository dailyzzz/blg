15:T3986,<h1 id="2022-was-the-first-increase-in-infant-deaths-in-decades">2022 Was The First Increase in Infant Deaths in Decades</h1>
<h2 id="the-ancient-pattern">The Ancient Pattern</h2>
<blockquote>
<p>"And Pharaoh charged all his people, saying, Every son that is born ye shall cast into the river, and every daughter ye shall save alive." — Exodus 1:22</p>
</blockquote>
<p>The state-ordered killing of infants is an ancient pattern. Pharaoh targeted Hebrew baby boys to break the bloodline from which the Deliverer would come. Herod slaughtered the infants of Bethlehem to destroy the Messiah. The killing of the innocents is always couched in institutional language — public safety, national security, disease prevention — but the result is always the same: children die, and the state bears no consequence.</p>
<p>In 2022, for the first time in decades, the infant mortality rate in the United States increased. This reversal of a long-standing downward trend occurred in the same year that COVID-19 mRNA vaccines were authorized for children as young as six months and in the period following mass vaccination of pregnant women. The coincidence demands investigation. The silence demands explanation.</p>

<h2 id="the-data-what-the-numbers-show">The Data: What the Numbers Show</h2>
<h3 id="the-historical-trend">The Historical Trend</h3>
<p>The U.S. infant mortality rate has been declining for decades:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>1980:</strong> 12.6 deaths per 1,000 live births</li>
<li><strong>1990:</strong> 9.2 per 1,000</li>
<li><strong>2000:</strong> 6.9 per 1,000</li>
<li><strong>2010:</strong> 6.1 per 1,000</li>
<li><strong>2020:</strong> 5.4 per 1,000</li>
<li><strong>2021:</strong> 5.4 per 1,000</li>
</ul>
<p>This steady decline reflected genuine improvements in neonatal care, prenatal medicine, nutrition, and public health infrastructure. The decline was so consistent that it was considered one of the most reliable positive health trends in American statistics.</p>
<h3 id="the-2022-reversal">The 2022 Reversal</h3>
<p>In 2022, the infant mortality rate increased — breaking a decades-long downward trend. The increase was statistically significant and was noted by the CDC's National Center for Health Statistics (NCHS) in its annual mortality report.</p>
<p>Key observations:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>The increase was across multiple categories</strong> — not limited to a single cause of death, race, or region</li>
<li><strong>Neonatal deaths (0-27 days)</strong> and <strong>postneonatal deaths (28-364 days)</strong> both showed concerning trends</li>
<li><strong>Certain cause-of-death categories</strong> showed particular increases, including congenital anomalies, sudden infant death, and complications of pregnancy</li>
<li><strong>The increase coincided temporally</strong> with the widespread vaccination of pregnant women (throughout 2021-2022) and the authorization of COVID-19 vaccines for infants and young children (June 2022)</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="what-changed-in-2022">What Changed in 2022?</h3>
<p>The factors that historically drive infant mortality improvements — neonatal intensive care, prenatal care access, nutrition — did not deteriorate in 2022. Medical technology continued to advance. Prenatal care remained available. Nutrition programs continued.</p>
<p>What changed was the introduction of novel medical products into the reproductive and pediatric population:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Mass vaccination of pregnant women</strong> with mRNA COVID-19 vaccines throughout 2021 and 2022</li>
<li><strong>Authorization of mRNA COVID-19 vaccines for children 6 months and older</strong> in June 2022</li>
<li><strong>Continued exposure of pregnant women and infants to spike protein</strong> through direct vaccination and shedding</li>
<li><strong>The addition of COVID-19 vaccines to the childhood immunization schedule</strong> in October 2022</li>
</ol>

<h2 id="the-kingston-report-connecting-vaccination-to-infant-mortality">The Kingston Report: Connecting Vaccination to Infant Mortality</h2>
<p>Karen Kingston's analysis through <a href="https://karenkingston.substack.com">The Kingston Report</a> connects the 2022 infant mortality increase to the biological mechanisms documented across her body of work.</p>
<h3 id="maternal-vaccination-and-fetal-harm">Maternal Vaccination and Fetal Harm</h3>
<p>Kingston has documented multiple pathways by which maternal vaccination with mRNA products can harm the developing fetus:</p>
<p><strong>Spike protein crosses the placenta.</strong> Published research has detected spike protein in placental tissue and in the fetal circulation of vaccinated mothers. The fetus is directly exposed to a toxic protein that suppresses p53, damages ACE2-expressing tissues, and triggers inflammatory responses.</p>
<p><strong>Anti-spike antibodies cross-react with placental proteins.</strong> As documented in <a href="/spike-antibodies-fetal-deaths/?lang=en">Spike Protein ANTIBODIES Increase Fetal Deaths</a>, antibodies generated by vaccination cross-react with syncytin-1 and other essential placental proteins, damaging the organ that sustains fetal life.</p>
<p><strong>LNPs accumulate in the ovaries.</strong> Lipid nanoparticles concentrate in ovarian tissue, potentially affecting oocyte development and early embryonic survival.</p>
<p><strong>Immune activation during pregnancy increases miscarriage risk.</strong> The inflammatory immune response triggered by mRNA vaccination can shift the maternal immune profile from tolerogenic (required for healthy pregnancy) to inflammatory (associated with pregnancy complications).</p>
<h3 id="congenital-anomalies">Congenital Anomalies</h3>
<p>The 2022 data showed increases in deaths from congenital anomalies — birth defects. Kingston has connected this to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Spike protein exposure during critical periods of organogenesis (the first trimester, when major organs are forming)</li>
<li>Lipid nanoparticle distribution to embryonic tissue</li>
<li>DNA contamination (including SV40 promoter sequences) in mRNA vaccine vials, which could theoretically integrate into the embryonic genome during the period of rapid cell division</li>
<li>Immune-mediated damage to developing organ systems</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="sids-signal">SIDS Signal</h3>
<p>The data also showed concerns regarding sudden infant death — the unexplained death of otherwise healthy infants. Kingston has documented the temporal clustering of SIDS around vaccination dates and the plausibility of vaccine-induced neurological events (particularly brainstem inflammation affecting respiratory control) as a mechanism for sudden infant death.</p>

<h2 id="the-institutional-silence">The Institutional Silence</h2>
<h3 id="what-the-cdc-did-not-say">What the CDC Did Not Say</h3>
<p>The CDC's reporting on the 2022 infant mortality increase was notable for what it did not discuss:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>No mention of COVID-19 vaccination as a potential contributing factor</strong> — despite the temporal coincidence and the known biological mechanisms</li>
<li><strong>No investigation into maternal vaccination status</strong> of mothers whose infants died — the basic question of whether infant death rates differed between vaccinated and unvaccinated mothers was not addressed</li>
<li><strong>No stratification by infant vaccination status</strong> — whether infants who received COVID-19 vaccines had different mortality rates than those who did not was not reported</li>
<li><strong>No investigation of the SIDS clustering</strong> around vaccination dates</li>
</ul>
<p>The absence of these analyses is itself evidence. The CDC — the agency charged with monitoring the safety of vaccines — did not investigate whether vaccines contributed to the first increase in infant mortality in decades. The only possible explanation is that the agency did not want the answer.</p>
<h3 id="the-pharaoh-pattern">The Pharaoh Pattern</h3>
<p>The institutional silence mirrors the Pharaoh pattern: the state acts in ways that kill children, and the state does not investigate its own actions. Pharaoh did not commission a study on the effectiveness of drowning Hebrew infants. The institution that causes the harm does not investigate the cause — because the investigation would implicate the institution.</p>
