15:T5fcf,<h1 id="the-three-heavens-what-howard-pittman-saw-when-he-died">The Three Heavens: What Howard Pittman Saw When He Died</h1>
<h2 id="the-ancient-pattern">The Ancient Pattern</h2>
<blockquote>
<p><em>"I knew a man in Christ above fourteen years ago, (whether in the body, I cannot tell; or whether out of the body, I cannot tell: God knoweth;) such an one caught up to the third heaven. And I knew such a man, (whether in the body, or out of the body, I cannot tell: God knoweth;) How that he was caught up into paradise, and heard unspeakable words, which it is not lawful for a man to utter."</em> — 2 Corinthians 12:2-4</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p><em>"For we wrestle not against flesh and blood, but against principalities, against powers, against the rulers of the darkness of this world, against spiritual wickedness in high places."</em> — Ephesians 6:12</p>
</blockquote>
<p>The Apostle Paul spoke of a "third heaven." If there is a third, there must be a first and a second. Paul wrote these words nearly two thousand years ago. He described being caught up — physically or spiritually, he himself did not know — to a place where he heard things that could not be spoken. He experienced dimensions of reality that human language could not contain.</p>
<p>Nearly twenty centuries later, on August 3, 1979, a Baptist minister named Howard Pittman experienced the same journey. But unlike Paul, Pittman was given permission to speak. He was given permission — and a command — to tell the world what he saw.</p>
<p>Three heavens. Three dimensions of reality stacked one upon another, separated by walls that flesh cannot penetrate. And in the second heaven, a realm of such absolute darkness that it redefined Pittman's understanding of evil forever.</p>

<h2 id="the-man-on-the-table">The Man on the Table</h2>
<h3 id="a-life-of-service">A Life of Service</h3>
<p>Before examining what Pittman saw, it is essential to understand who he was. Credibility matters. Context matters. And the context of Howard Pittman's life makes his testimony impossible to dismiss as the hallucination of a weak mind.</p>
<p>Howard Pittman was a Baptist minister for thirty-five years. Not a televangelist. Not a prosperity preacher. A Baptist minister — rooted in conservative, Bible-believing, traditional Protestant Christianity. He had spent decades in the pulpit, in the hospital room, at the graveside, in the counseling office. He knew the Bible inside and out. He knew church politics. He knew the language, the rituals, the expectations, and the culture of institutional Christianity.</p>
<p>Beyond the church, he was a retired officer of the New Orleans Police Department. NOPD. One of the toughest police forces in one of the toughest cities in America. This was not a man given to flights of fancy or emotional excess. This was a man trained to observe, to analyze, to report facts, and to survive.</p>
<p>He lived on a sixty-one-acre farm in Mississippi. He was married. He was established. He was, by every measure, an unremarkable man living an unremarkable life — unremarkable, that is, until his main artery ruptured and his body began to die.</p>
<h3 id="the-night-of-august-3-1979">The Night of August 3, 1979</h3>
<p>The medical facts are straightforward. Howard Pittman's main artery ruptured, causing devastating blood loss. Internal hemorrhage — the kind that kills quickly and efficiently. He was transported to the hospital and admitted to the intensive care unit around midnight.</p>
<p>For six hours, the medical staff worked to stabilize him. Monitors beeped. IV lines ran. Nurses checked vitals. Doctors assessed and reassessed. And at six o'clock in the morning, his vital signs failed. His body was dying. The machines confirmed what the medical team feared.</p>
<p>A seven-hour surgery followed — a desperate attempt to repair the damage and restore blood flow. Seven hours on the operating table. Seven hours during which Howard Pittman's physical body hovered between life and death.</p>
<p>But Pittman was not unconscious. He was not "under." He was somewhere else entirely.</p>
<h3 id="the-strange-prayer">The Strange Prayer</h3>
<p>As his body failed, Pittman prayed. But it was not the prayer of a dying man begging for more time. It was not "God, please heal me" or "God, please let me live." It was what Pittman himself described as "a strange prayer."</p>
<p>He prayed to appear before God's throne.</p>
<p>Consider the audacity of this request. Consider the boldness. A dying man, a Baptist minister who had served for thirty-five years, who had every reason to believe he was saved, who had every reason to expect heaven — this man prayed not simply to go to heaven, but to stand before the throne of God Himself.</p>
<p>And God said yes.</p>
<p>An angel came. Not a metaphorical angel. Not a symbol. Not a feeling. A being — an actual, personal, conscious being — came and lifted Pittman's spirit from his physical body. And the journey began.</p>
<h2 id="the-first-heaven">The First Heaven</h2>
<h3 id="the-physical-realm">The Physical Realm</h3>
<p>The first heaven, as Pittman described it, is the physical world. The material universe. Everything that human senses can perceive — the earth, the sky, the stars, the planets, the air we breathe, the ground we walk on. This is the first heaven.</p>
<p>But Pittman immediately noticed something that changed his understanding of reality. When his spirit separated from his body, the first heaven did not become less real. The physical world did not become more real. The opposite happened. The spirit realm was MORE real than the physical realm. The material world, which we treat as the ultimate reality, was revealed to be a shadow — a thin, temporary layer stretched over a deeper, more permanent, more substantive reality.</p>
<p>This aligns perfectly with Paul's statement in 2 Corinthians 4:18: "While we look not at the things which are seen, but at the things which are not seen: for the things which are seen are temporal; but the things which are not seen are eternal."</p>
<p>The first heaven is temporal. It is the temporary realm. The seen realm. The realm that most humans treat as the only reality. But Pittman, now freed from his physical body, could see clearly: the first heaven is the least real of the three. It is the surface. Beneath it — or above it, or around it, or through it — lies something far more substantial.</p>
<h3 id="the-nature-of-spirit-travel">The Nature of Spirit Travel</h3>
<p>Freed from his body, Pittman discovered the fundamental differences between physical and spiritual existence. In the physical world, travel requires time. You move through space at a rate determined by the limitations of your body or your vehicle. The fastest you can go is bounded by physical law.</p>
<p>In the spirit realm, travel happens at the speed of thought. You think of a location, and you are there. There is no journey in the physical sense — no distance to cover, no time to elapse. Thought is transportation. Will is velocity. The limitations of physics do not apply.</p>
<p>Communication was similarly transformed. In the physical world, we communicate through vibration — vocal cords vibrating air molecules that travel to eardrums that translate the vibration into meaning. It is slow, imprecise, and prone to misunderstanding. In the spirit realm, communication happens through thought waves — direct, instantaneous, precise, and complete. Not mouth to ear, but mind to mind. Thought to thought. Meaning transferred without the clumsiness of language.</p>
<p>Pittman learned that human beings are not physical beings having a spiritual experience. They are spiritual beings having a physical experience. The spirit is the true self. The body is the vehicle. And when the vehicle fails, the self continues — traveling at the speed of thought, communicating through thought waves, existing in a reality far more vivid and substantial than the physical world it left behind.</p>
<h2 id="the-second-heaven">The Second Heaven</h2>
<h3 id="the-dimensional-wall">The Dimensional Wall</h3>
<p>Between the first heaven and the second heaven, Pittman encountered what he described as a dimensional wall — a boundary separating the physical realm from the spiritual realm. This wall is not made of physical material. It cannot be seen with physical eyes. It cannot be measured with physical instruments. But it is real — as real as a brick wall, and far more impenetrable.</p>
<p>Flesh cannot pass through this wall. No physical body, no physical object, no physical force can penetrate the dimensional barrier between the first and second heavens. This is why the spirit realm remains invisible to physical senses. This is why scientists cannot detect it with instruments. This is why materialists deny its existence. The wall is absolute. The separation is complete.</p>
<p>But spirit can pass through. Pittman's spirit, freed from his body and accompanied by the angel, passed through the dimensional wall as easily as thought. On the other side was the second heaven.</p>
<h3 id="satans-domain">Satan's Domain</h3>
<p>The second heaven is Satan's domain. His territory. His throne room. The headquarters from which he commands his forces and administers his war against humanity and against God.</p>
<p>Pittman was taken through this realm, and what he witnessed defied everything he had imagined about the spiritual world in thirty-five years of ministry. He had preached about Satan. He had taught about demons. He had counseled people who claimed to be under spiritual attack. But nothing — absolutely nothing — had prepared him for the reality of the second heaven.</p>
<p>The second heaven is not the popular Christian image of hell — fire and brimstone and red-suited devils with pitchforks. It is far worse than that. It is a realm of organized, disciplined, militaristic evil. Demons by the millions, organized into hierarchies, carrying out specific assignments with terrifying efficiency. Not chaos. Not anarchy. Order. Brutal, purposeful, relentless order.</p>
<p>Every demon has a specific assignment. Every demon has a specific area of authority. Every demon operates within a chain of command as rigid and disciplined as any military structure on earth — more disciplined, in fact, than any human military that has ever existed.</p>
<h3 id="the-total-absence-of-love">The Total Absence of Love</h3>
<p>But the most devastating revelation of the second heaven was not its organization. It was not its size. It was not the number or the power of the demons who inhabited it. The most devastating revelation was what was missing.</p>
<p>Love.</p>
<p>In the second heaven, love is completely absent. Not diminished. Not distorted. Not perverted. Absent. Zero. None. Not one particle, not one molecule, not one atom of love exists anywhere in Satan's domain.</p>
<p>This is almost impossible for a human being to comprehend. We live in a world where love exists — imperfect, broken, often twisted, but present. Even the most evil human being on earth lives in an environment where love exists somewhere. A mother loves a child. A friend loves a friend. A dog loves its owner. Love permeates the first heaven in some form, even in its most degraded state.</p>
<p>But in the second heaven, love is completely gone. Every interaction, every communication, every relationship, every transaction in the second heaven occurs in the total absence of love. Demons do not love each other. They do not love Satan. Satan does not love them. There is no affection, no warmth, no compassion, no empathy, no tenderness. Nothing.</p>
<p>And yet — and this is the paradox that stunned Pittman — they work together with incredible loyalty and zeal. Despite the total absence of love, Satan's forces are more organized, more disciplined, more committed, and more effective than the church. They carry out their assignments with a dedication that puts most Christians to shame. Not out of love. Not out of devotion. Out of pure, loveless, calculated obedience to their hierarchy.</p>
<p>This should terrify every Christian who thinks that love alone is sufficient to win the spiritual war. The enemy operates without love — and he is winning. He is winning because he is organized, disciplined, and relentless. The church, which has love, is losing because it is disorganized, undisciplined, and casual.</p>
<h2 id="the-third-heaven">The Third Heaven</h2>
<h3 id="gods-throne">God's Throne</h3>
<p>From the second heaven, Pittman was taken into the third heaven — the dwelling place of God Himself. The realm where the Creator of all things sits on His throne. The place Paul described as "paradise," where he heard "unspeakable words, which it is not lawful for a man to utter."</p>
<p>Pittman's account of the third heaven is, by its nature, limited. The human mind cannot fully comprehend what exists there. Human language cannot adequately describe it. But what Pittman experienced was a place of absolute power, absolute authority, absolute holiness, and — in stark contrast to the second heaven — absolute love.</p>
<p>If the second heaven is defined by the total absence of love, the third heaven is defined by its total presence. Love is not merely present in the third heaven. Love IS the third heaven. It is the atmosphere. The substance. The foundation. Everything that exists there exists within love and because of love.</p>
<p>The contrast between the second and third heavens is the contrast between Satan and God, between rebellion and obedience, between death and life, between the absence of love and its overwhelming, consuming, indescribable presence.</p>
<h3 id="before-the-throne">Before the Throne</h3>
<p>It was here, before God's throne, that Pittman received the message he was sent back to deliver. The devastating statistic: 97.5 percent of professing Christians rejected. The root cause: serving self. The diagnosis: the church is taking a placebo. The prescription: wake up.</p>
<p>But it was also here that Pittman understood why the message was so urgent. Standing in the presence of absolute holiness, he could see clearly what self-deception looks like from God's perspective. From the first heaven — from the pew, from the pulpit, from the church parking lot — the placebo looks convincing. The form of godliness is impressive. The words sound right. The activities seem productive.</p>
<p>But from the throne, none of it matters. From the throne, only one thing matters: is Christ on the throne of your life, or is self? And the answer, for 97.5 percent, is self.</p>
<h2 id="the-laws-of-the-spirit-realm">The Laws of the Spirit Realm</h2>
<h3 id="spirit-supersedes-flesh">Spirit Supersedes Flesh</h3>
<p>Pittman's journey through the three heavens revealed fundamental laws that govern the spirit realm — laws as absolute and inviolable as the laws of physics that govern the material world.</p>
<p>The first law: spirit is superior to flesh. The spirit realm is more real, more permanent, more substantial, and more powerful than the physical realm. When there is a conflict between spirit and flesh, spirit prevails. Always. Without exception.</p>
<p>This has enormous implications. It means that physical solutions cannot solve spiritual problems. Medicine cannot cure a spiritually-rooted illness. Therapy cannot resolve a spiritually-rooted conflict. Education cannot overcome a spiritually-rooted deception. The material world is subordinate to the spiritual world, and addressing the material while ignoring the spiritual is addressing the symptom while ignoring the disease.</p>
<h3 id="communication-through-thought">Communication Through Thought</h3>
<p>The second law: communication in the spirit realm is through thought, not through physical sound. This means that every thought is heard. Every intention is visible. Every motive is exposed. In the physical world, we can hide our thoughts behind our words. We can say one thing and think another. We can present a facade. In the spirit realm, the facade is stripped away. Thought is transparent.</p>
<p>This is why scripture says that God "searcheth the hearts" (Romans 8:27). Not because He is suspicious, but because the spirit realm operates on thought. In His realm, thought IS communication. There is no distinction between what you think and what you say. Which means that the mouth-professing Christian's fundamental strategy — saying the right things while thinking and living differently — is exposed as utterly futile in the spirit realm.</p>
<h3 id="dimensional-separation">Dimensional Separation</h3>
<p>The third law: the heavens are separated by dimensional walls that flesh cannot penetrate. This means that the spirit realm is not far away. It is not in a distant galaxy or a separate universe. It is here — right here, occupying the same space, coexisting with the physical world. But the dimensional wall makes it invisible and inaccessible to physical senses.</p>
<p>This is why you cannot see demons with your eyes or hear angels with your ears (under normal circumstances). Not because they are far away. Because they are on the other side of a dimensional wall that your flesh cannot penetrate. They are here. Right here. In this room. Around your chair. Around your bed. Around your workplace. The second heaven is not somewhere else. It is everywhere — separated from your perception by a wall that only spirit can cross.</p>
<h3 id="the-soul-belongs-to-the-spirit">The Soul Belongs to the Spirit</h3>
<p>The fourth law: the human soul belongs to the spirit, not the body. Unlike animals, whose consciousness is tied to their physical form, human beings are spirit beings inhabiting flesh temporarily. The body is a vehicle. The spirit is the driver. When the body dies, the spirit continues. The soul — the personality, the consciousness, the "you" — goes with the spirit, not with the body.</p>
<p>This is what happened to Pittman. When his body failed on the operating table, his spirit — his true self — continued. He was still Howard Pittman. He still had his memories, his personality, his awareness. He had merely left the vehicle. And in leaving the vehicle, he discovered that the road — the spirit realm — was far more vast, far more real, and far more consequential than anything the vehicle had allowed him to see.</p>
<h2 id="implications-for-the-living">Implications for the Living</h2>
<h3 id="we-are-not-alone">We Are Not Alone</h3>
<p>The most immediate implication of Pittman's testimony is that we are not alone. Ever. The spirit realm is present — always present, everywhere present. The second heaven, with its demonic hierarchy, is operating right now, in this room, around you. Demons are carrying out assignments. Angels are standing guard. The war is happening. It is not a metaphor. It is not a theological abstraction. It is a present, active, ongoing reality.</p>
<p>This changes everything. It changes how you pray. It changes how you think. It changes how you live. If the spirit realm is as real as Pittman says — more real than the physical — then ignoring it is the most dangerous thing a human being can do. And yet that is exactly what most Christians do. They live as practical materialists, acknowledging the spirit realm in theory on Sunday morning and ignoring it in practice the rest of the week.</p>
<h3 id="the-war-is-real">The War Is Real</h3>
<p>Ephesians 6:12 is not a metaphor. "We wrestle not against flesh and blood, but against principalities, against powers, against the rulers of the darkness of this world, against spiritual wickedness in high places." Paul wrote this because it is literally true. And Pittman saw it with his own eyes.</p>
<p>The war between God and Satan, between the forces of the third heaven and the forces of the second heaven, is being fought right now. And the primary battlefield is the human heart. The throne room. Who sits on the throne? Christ or self? This is the question that every demon assignment is designed to influence, every angelic intervention is sent to protect, and every moment of every life revolves around.</p>
<p>The placebo church does not teach warfare. It teaches comfort. It teaches blessing. It teaches feeling good about feeling good. And meanwhile, the forces of the second heaven are organized, disciplined, assigned, and relentless. They never rest. They never take a Sunday off. They never declare a ceasefire.</p>
<h3 id="love-is-the-weapon">Love Is the Weapon</h3>
<p>The total absence of love in the second heaven is not merely a descriptive detail. It is a strategic revelation. Love is the one thing Satan does not have and cannot replicate. It is the one weapon in the arsenal that the enemy possesses no defense against. Not emotion. Not sentimentality. Genuine, self-sacrificing, cross-bearing, Christ-reflecting love.</p>
<p>This is why the greatest commandment is love. Not because God is sentimental. Because love is the fundamental force of the third heaven — the force that holds everything together, the force that defeats everything it touches, the force that the second heaven has no answer for.</p>
<p>And this is why the placebo is so dangerous. It replaces genuine love with emotion. It substitutes feelings for sacrifice. It offers the experience of love without the cost of love. And in doing so, it disarms the church of its only weapon.</p>
