<h2 id="the-rockefeller-eugenics-empire">The Rockefeller Eugenics Empire</h2>
<h3 id="the-cold-spring-harbor-laboratory">The Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory</h3>
<p>In 1904, Charles Davenport — a biologist with close ties to the Rockefeller family — established the Station for Experimental Evolution at Cold Spring Harbor, Long Island. This facility, funded by Carnegie and Rockefeller money, became the headquarters of the American eugenics movement.</p>
<p>Cold Spring Harbor was not merely a research facility. It was the command center of a nationwide campaign to classify human beings by genetic "fitness" and to eliminate those deemed "unfit" through sterilization, institutionalization, and immigration restriction.</p>
<p>The Eugenics Record Office, also at Cold Spring Harbor, collected family histories and genetic data on millions of Americans. Field workers were dispatched across the country to document family trees, identifying individuals and families deemed genetically inferior. This data was used to support forced sterilization laws in 32 states and to justify immigration restrictions targeting Southern and Eastern Europeans, Asians, and Africans.</p>

<ul>
<li>The Kaiser Wilhelm Institute for Anthropology, Human Heredity, and Eugenics — directed by Eugen Fischer, who later became a Nazi racial scientist</li>

<li>Josef Mengele's own research institute received Rockefeller funding before Mengele became the "Angel of Death" at Auschwitz</li>
</ul>
<p>The connection between American eugenics and Nazi Germany is direct, documented, and undeniable. The Nazis explicitly cited American eugenics laws as inspiration for their own racial purity programs. The Nuremberg Laws of 1935 were modeled, in part, on American forced sterilization statutes.</p>

<h2 id="human-dissection-the-hidden-history">Human Dissection: The Hidden History</h2>
<h3 id="the-body-snatching-trade">The Body Snatching Trade</h3>
<p>The history of human dissection in America is far darker than most people realize. In the 19th century, the demand for cadavers for medical schools far exceeded the legal supply. This created a thriving black market in human bodies, operated by "resurrection men" who dug up fresh graves and sold the bodies to medical schools.</p>
<p>The Rockefeller-funded medical schools were among the primary consumers of this trade. As the Flexner Report of 1910 consolidated medical education into a handful of elite institutions, the demand for cadavers increased. The body snatching trade evolved from grave robbery into something far worse: the murder of vulnerable people — the poor, the homeless, the mentally ill, minorities — for their bodies.</p>
<h3 id="the-anatomical-gift-acts">The Anatomical Gift Acts</h3>
<p>By the mid-20th century, the body trade was legalized through a series of Anatomical Gift Acts that allowed institutions to claim the bodies of the unclaimed dead — people who died in hospitals, prisons, and mental institutions without family to claim them. In practice, this meant that the bodies of the poorest and most marginalized members of society were funneled to medical schools and research institutions.</p>
<p>The ethical implications are staggering. People who were already disadvantaged in life — denied adequate healthcare, nutrition, and social services — had their bodies claimed by the very institutions that had failed them. Their flesh became the raw material of medical education, their organs the subjects of research, their bones the specimens in anatomy museums.</p>
