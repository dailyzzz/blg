<h2 id="what-happened-to-me">What Happened to Me</h2>
<h3 id="the-first-experiences">The First Experiences</h3>
<p>The music came first. It was always the same kind — structured, rhythmic, sometimes recognizable melodies and sometimes unfamiliar compositions. It played most clearly in quiet environments, especially at night. The volume varied — sometimes barely perceptible, sometimes loud enough to prevent sleep.</p>
<p>I am not a person with a history of mental illness. I have no family history of schizophrenia or psychotic disorders. I was in good physical health, eating well, sleeping normally (until the music started), and functioning well in daily life. The music came from nowhere and changed everything.</p>
<p>The first reaction was denial. I told myself it was tinnitus — a ringing in the ears that can sometimes manifest as musical tones. But tinnitus doesn't play recognizable melodies. It doesn't change songs. It doesn't respond to your attention.</p>
<p>The second reaction was fear. Am I losing my mind? Is this the beginning of schizophrenia? Am I having a breakdown? The fear was paralyzing, and it isolated me — because who could I tell? The moment you say "I hear music that isn't there," you are one step from a psychiatric diagnosis, medication, and the permanent label of "mentally ill."</p>
