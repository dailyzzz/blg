15:T4e9e,<h1 id="ye-shall-be-as-gods-the-ancient-lie-behind-transhumanism">Ye Shall Be as Gods: The Ancient Lie Behind Transhumanism</h1>
<h2 id="the-ancient-pattern">The Ancient Pattern</h2>
<blockquote>
<p>"For God doth know that in the day ye eat thereof, then your eyes shall be opened, and ye shall be as gods, knowing good and evil." — Genesis 3:5</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Five words changed the course of human history: "ye shall be as gods." The serpent's promise was not merely a temptation to eat fruit. It was a philosophical proposition — a complete alternative to the Creator's design. God made you limited. The serpent offers you unlimited. God made you dependent. The serpent offers you independent. God made you human. The serpent offers you divine.</p>
<p>This is the original transhumanist manifesto.</p>
<p>Every subsequent rebellion against God — every empire, every philosophy, every technology wielded in defiance of divine boundaries — traces back to this moment. The promise changes form but never changes substance. In Eden, it was fruit. In Babel, it was a tower. In Egypt, it was Pharaoh's divinity. In Greece, it was philosophy. In Rome, it was emperor worship. In the 21st century, it is transhumanism — the explicit, funded, institutional project to transcend the human condition through technology.</p>
<p>The Confliction track <a href="https://confliction.zzzdaily.com/tracks/en/07-The_Serpents_Curse.mp4">The Serpent's Curse</a> reminds us that the serpent who made this promise was cursed for making it. The promise was a lie. Adam and Eve did not become gods. They became fallen — ashamed, afraid, separated from the God whose image they bore. The lie produced death, not divinity. And the same lie, repackaged in silicon and genetics, will produce the same result.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="the-original-temptation-a-three-part-lie">The Original Temptation: A Three-Part Lie</h2>
<p>The serpent's temptation in Genesis 3:1-5 follows a three-part structure that maps precisely to the transhumanist agenda:</p>
<h3 id="part-1-hath-god-said--question-the-creators-authority">Part 1: "Hath God said?" — Question the Creator's Authority</h3>
<p>The serpent's first move was to question God's word: "Yea, hath God really said, Ye shall not eat of every tree of the garden?" This is the deconstruction of authority — the suggestion that God's commands are not absolute, not reasonable, not in your best interest.</p>
<p>Transhumanism begins with the same question: Did God really design the human body as the final product? Is the human condition really the Creator's intended outcome? Are the limitations of mortality, aging, disease, and cognitive boundary really the design specification — or are they flaws to be corrected by human engineering?</p>
<p>The transhumanist answer is that the human body is a "first draft" — a starting point for enhancement, not a finished work. The limitations are bugs, not features. The boundaries are problems, not designs. This directly contradicts Genesis 1:31: "And God saw every thing that he had made, and, behold, it was very good." God declared the creation — including the human body — "very good." The serpent says: "Not good enough."</p>
<h3 id="part-2-ye-shall-not-surely-die--deny-the-consequences">Part 2: "Ye shall not surely die" — Deny the Consequences</h3>
<p>God's warning was clear: "in the day that thou eatest thereof thou shalt surely die" (Genesis 2:17). The serpent directly contradicts God: "Ye shall not surely die." This is the denial of consequences — the promise that transgression carries no penalty, that crossing God's boundaries has no cost.</p>
<p>Transhumanism makes the same promise. Death is not a divine appointment. It is a technical problem to be solved. Aging is not the natural progression of a mortal body. It is a disease to be cured. The limitations of the human condition are not consequences of the Fall. They are engineering challenges to be overcome.</p>
<p>Ray Kurzweil, Google's director of engineering and the godfather of modern transhumanism, predicts that humans will achieve digital immortality by 2045 — uploading consciousness into digital substrates that do not age, do not decay, and do not die. Jeff Bezos invested over $3 billion in Altos Labs, a biotechnology company focused on cellular rejuvenation. Google's Calico (California Life Company) has the explicit mission of solving the problem of death.</p>
<p>"Ye shall not surely die." The serpent promised it in Eden. Silicon Valley promises it with venture capital. The lie is the same. The lie is always the same.</p>
<h3 id="part-3-ye-shall-be-as-gods--replace-the-creator">Part 3: "Ye shall be as gods" — Replace the Creator</h3>
<p>The climax of the temptation: you will be divine. Your eyes will be opened. You will know good and evil. You will transcend the human condition and become something more — something equal to God.</p>
<p>Transhumanism's explicit goal is the creation of "posthumans" — beings who have transcended the biological limitations of <em>Homo sapiens</em> through genetic modification, cybernetic enhancement, cognitive augmentation, and digital integration. The stated goal is not merely to improve human health. It is to create a new species — <em>Homo deus</em>, in the title of Yuval Noah Harari's 2016 book. Man becoming god. The serpent's promise made literal.</p>
<p>Harari, a senior advisor to the World Economic Forum, writes openly about humanity's imminent transcendence: "Homo sapiens as we know them will disappear in a century or so... We are probably one of the last generations of Homo sapiens. Within a century or two, Earth will be dominated by entities that are more different from us than we are different from Neanderthals or from chimpanzees."</p>
<p>This is not a neutral observation. It is the serpent's manifesto updated for the TED Talk circuit.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="the-five-pillars-of-transhumanist-ascension">The Five Pillars of Transhumanist Ascension</h2>
<p>The transhumanist project rests on five pillars, each of which represents a specific rejection of God's design:</p>
<h3 id="pillar-1-genetic-enhancement">Pillar 1: Genetic Enhancement</h3>
<p>CRISPR gene editing enables direct modification of the human genome. Scientists can add, remove, or alter genes with increasing precision. The stated medical applications — eliminating genetic diseases — are the entry point. The trajectory is enhancement: intelligence augmentation, physical optimization, disease resistance beyond natural parameters, and ultimately the creation of designer humans whose genetic code is written by human engineers rather than by the Creator.</p>
<p>As explored in <a href="/fallen-angels-book-of-enoch/?lang=en">Fallen Angels and the Book of Enoch: The Forbidden History</a>, genetic manipulation is not new. The Watchers taught humanity forbidden knowledge that included the corruption of natural genetic boundaries. The pre-Flood world was destroyed because "all flesh had corrupted his way upon the earth" (Genesis 6:12). CRISPR is the digital version of the Watchers' teaching — the technology to rewrite the code that God wrote.</p>
<h3 id="pillar-2-cybernetic-enhancement">Pillar 2: Cybernetic Enhancement</h3>
<p>Neural interfaces, prosthetic limbs, cochlear implants, retinal implants, exoskeletons — the merger of the biological body with mechanical and digital systems. The medical applications are the gateway. The trajectory is augmentation: enhanced vision beyond human range, enhanced hearing beyond human frequency, enhanced strength beyond human muscle, and enhanced cognition through direct brain-computer connection.</p>
<p>As documented in research on brain-computer interfaces, Neuralink has implanted its device in human patients. The stated purpose is restoring function to paralyzed individuals. The actual trajectory — stated openly by Elon Musk — is human-AI symbiosis: the merger of human consciousness with artificial intelligence to create a "third layer" of cognition beyond the biological brain.</p>
<h3 id="pillar-3-cognitive-enhancement">Pillar 3: Cognitive Enhancement</h3>
<p>Nootropics (cognitive-enhancing drugs), brain stimulation technologies, AI-assisted thinking, and eventually direct neural augmentation aim to expand human cognitive capacity beyond its natural limits. The goal is not merely to treat cognitive impairment but to create superintelligent humans — beings whose cognitive capabilities exceed the natural human range.</p>
<p>The implicit claim is that the human mind, as designed by God, is insufficient. The serpent whispered: "your eyes will be opened." The transhumanist promises: "your mind will be expanded." The original temptation offered knowledge beyond human design. The modern iteration offers intelligence beyond human parameters. The promise is the same: transcendence through acquisition of what was not given.</p>
<h3 id="pillar-4-life-extension">Pillar 4: Life Extension</h3>
<p>Senolytics (drugs that clear aging cells), telomere therapy, organ regeneration, cryonics, and digital consciousness uploading all aim at the same target: defeating death. The medical applications — extending healthy lifespan, reducing age-related disease — are again the entry point. The trajectory is immortality.</p>
<p>"Ye shall not surely die." The serpent promised immortality through disobedience. Transhumanism promises immortality through technology. Neither delivers what it promises. The serpent's fruit brought death. Technology will bring a different kind of death — the death of the soul, trapped in a synthetic body that God did not design and the Spirit will not indwell.</p>
<h3 id="pillar-5-digital-transcendence">Pillar 5: Digital Transcendence</h3>
<p>The ultimate transhumanist vision is "mind uploading" — the transfer of human consciousness from the biological brain to a digital substrate. If achieved, this would represent the complete separation of the self from the body — the disembodied existence that God never intended. God created humanity as embodied beings — soul and body united. The resurrection is the restoration of this unity, not its dissolution.</p>
<p>Digital transcendence is the serpent's ultimate counterfeit: eternal existence without the body, without the Creator, without redemption. It is the inverse of resurrection. Resurrection restores the body to eternal glory. Digital transcendence abandons the body for eternal simulation. One is God's plan. The other is the serpent's imitation.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="the-tower-rebuilt">The Tower Rebuilt</h2>
<p>As explored in <a href="/tower-of-babel-two-rebuilding/?lang=en">Tower of Babel 2.0: Rebuilding the Gateway to Heaven</a>, transhumanism is the ascension project of the new Babel. The original tower was a gateway to heaven — an attempt to reach the divine realm through human engineering. Transhumanism is the same project with better tools:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>CRISPR</strong> is the brick — the building material of genetic reconstruction</li>
<li><strong>Neural interfaces</strong> are the stairway — the connection between the human and the digital/divine</li>
<li><strong>AI</strong> is the architect — the intelligence that designs what human minds cannot conceive</li>
<li><strong>Life extension</strong> is the mortar — the binding agent that holds the project together by eliminating the deadline of death</li>
<li><strong>Digital transcendence</strong> is the pinnacle — the final reaching of "heaven" through technological means</li>
</ul>
<p>God judged Babel by confusing the language and scattering the builders. The transhumanist project will face the same Judge — and the judgment will be proportional to the ambition.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="the-image-of-god-vs-the-image-of-the-beast">The Image of God vs. The Image of the Beast</h2>
<p>The fundamental question of transhumanism is theological: What is a human being?</p>
<p>Scripture answers: a human being is a creature made in the image of God (Genesis 1:27), designed with specific parameters, endowed with a soul, intended for relationship with the Creator, and destined either for eternal life through redemption or eternal separation through rejection.</p>
<p>Transhumanism answers: a human being is a biological machine — an evolutionary accident that can be improved, upgraded, modified, and eventually transcended. There is no soul. There is no Creator. There is no design. There is only material that can be engineered into something better.</p>
<p>The transhumanist vision is the image of the beast — a being that bears no resemblance to God's original design, whose identity is defined by technology rather than by the Creator, whose "life" is sustained by machines rather than by the breath of God, and whose allegiance belongs to the system that enhanced it rather than to the God who created it.</p>
<p>When a human being is genetically modified beyond recognition, cybernetically augmented beyond natural parameters, cognitively enhanced beyond human capacity, and digitally integrated into a machine network — is that being still human? Is it still made in the image of God? Can the Holy Spirit dwell in a temple that has been fundamentally redesigned by human engineers?</p>
<p>These are not academic questions. They are the most urgent questions of our generation. And the answer determines whether the beings that emerge from the transhumanist project are still redeemable — or whether they have crossed a boundary from which there is no return.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="the-line-that-cannot-be-crossed">The Line That Cannot Be Crossed</h2>
<p>Scripture establishes boundaries that the Creator takes seriously:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Genetic boundaries</strong> — "after its kind" appears ten times in Genesis 1. Each creature reproduces according to its kind. Crossing these boundaries was the sin of the Watchers (Genesis 6, Jude 6).</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Mortality boundaries</strong> — "It is appointed unto men once to die, and after this the judgment" (Hebrews 9:27). Death is not a bug. It is an appointment. Attempting to defeat death through technology is attempting to evade divine appointment.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Identity boundaries</strong> — "Male and female created he them" (Genesis 1:27). Human identity is defined by the Creator, not by the creature. Redefining identity through technology is usurping the Creator's authority.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Worship boundaries</strong> — "Thou shalt have no other gods before me" (Exodus 20:3). When technology becomes the means of transcendence — replacing God as the source of immortality, knowledge, and power — it has become a god. And worshipping it is idolatry.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Transhumanism crosses every one of these boundaries. It crosses genetic boundaries through CRISPR. It crosses mortality boundaries through life extension. It crosses identity boundaries through cognitive and physical modification. It crosses worship boundaries by placing technology in the role of savior.</p>
<p>The serpent's promise — "ye shall be as gods" — is the promise to cross every boundary God established. The consequence in Eden was death. The consequence in the end times will be the same — but on a civilizational scale.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="the-true-transcendence">The True Transcendence</h2>
<blockquote>
<p>"Behold, I shew you a mystery; We shall not all sleep, but we shall all be changed, In a moment, in the twinkling of an eye, at the last trump: for the trumpet shall sound, and the dead shall be raised incorruptible, and we shall be changed." — 1 Corinthians 15:51-52</p>
</blockquote>
<p>God offers true transcendence — not through technology but through resurrection. Not through CRISPR but through Christ. Not through uploading consciousness to the cloud but through being raised in a glorified body that will never age, never decay, never die.</p>
<p>The resurrection body described in 1 Corinthians 15 is everything transhumanism promises but cannot deliver:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Incorruptible</strong> — no aging, no disease, no decay</li>
<li><strong>Glorious</strong> — radiant, beautiful, perfected</li>
<li><strong>Powerful</strong> — no weakness, no limitation</li>
<li><strong>Spiritual</strong> — animated by the Spirit of God, not by biological processes</li>
</ul>
<p>This is the true transcendence. It is given, not engineered. It is received, not achieved. It is a gift of grace, not a product of technology. And it preserves the image of God rather than replacing it with the image of the beast.</p>
<p>The serpent offers counterfeit transcendence — technology that promises godhood but delivers bondage. The Creator offers true transcendence — resurrection that transforms mortality into immortality, corruption into incorruption, and weakness into power. The choice between transhumanism and resurrection is the choice between the serpent's promise and God's promise. And only one of them has a track record of delivery.</p>
