<h2 id="robert-koch-and-the-tuberculosis-fraud">Robert Koch and the Tuberculosis Fraud</h2>
<h3 id="the-1882-announcement">The 1882 Announcement</h3>
<p>On March 24, 1882, Robert Koch presented his findings to the Berlin Physiological Society, claiming to have identified the Mycobacterium tuberculosis as the cause of tuberculosis. The announcement made Koch an instant celebrity and is still celebrated as a landmark in medical history. The WHO even designates March 24 as "World Tuberculosis Day."</p>
<p>But Koch's evidence was far weaker than the mythology suggests.</p>
<h3 id="kochs-postulates--his-own-rules-he-couldnt-follow">Koch's Postulates — His Own Rules He Couldn't Follow</h3>
<p>Koch himself established four criteria (known as Koch's Postulates) that must be met to prove a microorganism causes a disease:</p>
<ol>
<li>The microorganism must be found in all cases of the disease</li>
<li>It must be isolated from the host and grown in pure culture</li>
<li>The cultured microorganism must cause disease when introduced into a healthy host</li>
<li>The microorganism must be re-isolated from the new host and shown to be identical</li>
</ol>
<p>Koch <strong>failed to satisfy his own postulates</strong> for tuberculosis:</p>
<p><strong>Postulate 1</strong>: The tubercle bacillus was NOT found in all cases of tuberculosis. Many patients diagnosed with tuberculosis showed no evidence of the bacillus. Koch explained this away by claiming the bacillus was present but undetectable — a circular argument that would be laughed out of any honest scientific forum.</p>
<p><strong>Postulate 3</strong>: When Koch injected the cultured bacillus into animals, the results were inconsistent. Some animals developed lesions; others did not. The animals that did develop lesions were often already in poor health — malnourished, stressed, kept in terrible conditions. Koch's experiments proved that weakened animals can become sick when injected with foreign material — not that a specific bacillus causes a specific disease.</p>
<p><strong>Postulate 4</strong>: Re-isolation was inconsistent and plagued by contamination issues that Koch himself acknowledged but dismissed.</p>
<h3 id="the-tuberculin-fraud">The Tuberculin Fraud</h3>
<p>Koch's credibility should have been permanently destroyed by the tuberculin scandal. In 1890, Koch announced that he had developed "tuberculin" — a substance that could cure tuberculosis. The announcement caused worldwide excitement. Patients flooded into Berlin seeking the miracle cure.</p>
<p>Tuberculin was a disaster. Not only did it fail to cure tuberculosis, it actively harmed patients. Many patients given tuberculin experienced severe reactions and accelerated disease progression. Some died directly as a result of treatment. Koch was widely condemned, and an investigation revealed that he had financial interests in the tuberculin product — he was profiting from the sale of the very substance he was promoting as a cure.</p>
<p>Despite this catastrophic failure — a failure that would destroy any modern researcher's career — Koch's original claim about the tubercle bacillus was never seriously re-examined. The scientific establishment had already built too much on the foundation of Koch's 1882 announcement. Retracting it would have meant demolishing germ theory itself.</p>
