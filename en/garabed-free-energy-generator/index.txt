<h2 id="the-garabed-story">The Garabed Story</h2>
<h3 id="garabed-tk-giragossian">Garabed T.K. Giragossian</h3>
<p>Garabed T.K. Giragossian was an Armenian-American inventor who, in the early 1900s, developed what he called the "Garabed" — an energy device that he claimed could extract usable energy from the natural forces of the universe. Giragossian spent years developing and refining the device, eventually seeking government support for a full-scale demonstration.</p>
<h3 id="the-congressional-resolution">The Congressional Resolution</h3>
<p>Giragossian's persistence and the apparent merit of his claims led to an extraordinary event: on December 15, 1918, Congressman Lathrop Brown of New York introduced House Resolution 642, directing the Secretary of the Interior to investigate the Garabed invention.</p>
<p>The resolution stated that Giragossian had "invented a method of securing available energy from an inexhaustible natural source" and that the invention warranted government investigation. The resolution passed, and a committee was appointed to examine the device.</p>
<p>This is remarkable. A member of the United States Congress formally requested government investigation of a free energy device. The resolution passed through the political process, meaning that enough representatives considered the claim credible to warrant official action. This was not a fringe event — it was an act of government.</p>
<h3 id="the-navy-examination">The Navy Examination</h3>
<p>The US Navy was tasked with examining the Garabed device. Navy engineers tested the device and, according to available records, acknowledged that it operated as described — producing energy output that exceeded the apparent input.</p>
<p>However, the Navy's report included a caveat that proved fatal to the investigation: the engineers could not explain how the device worked within the framework of accepted physics. Because they could not identify the energy source, they concluded that they must be missing something — rather than concluding that the device worked through principles not yet understood by conventional physics.</p>
<p>This is the institutional catch-22 that kills every free energy device: if the device cannot be explained by current physics, it is dismissed — even if it demonstrably works. The evidence of operation is trumped by the absence of a theoretical explanation. This is not science — it is ideology masquerading as science.</p>
<h3 id="the-disappearance">The Disappearance</h3>
<p>After the Navy examination, the Garabed investigation stalled. The device did not receive government support. Giragossian continued to advocate for his invention but was unable to overcome institutional resistance. The Garabed generator — like so many free energy devices before and after — faded from the public record.</p>
<p>What happened to the device itself is unclear. Whether it was confiscated, destroyed, or simply abandoned without support, the result was the same: a working free energy device, examined by the US government, was suppressed.</p>
