15:T549c,<h1 id="the-placebo-church-how-satan-administers-false-religion-to-billions">The Placebo Church: How Satan Administers False Religion to Billions</h1>
<h2 id="the-ancient-pattern">The Ancient Pattern</h2>
<blockquote>
<p><em>"Having a form of godliness, but denying the power thereof: from such turn away."</em> — 2 Timothy 3:5</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p><em>"Not every one that saith unto me, Lord, Lord, shall enter into the kingdom of heaven; but he that doeth the will of my Father which is in heaven. Many will say to me in that day, Lord, Lord, have we not prophesied in thy name? and in thy name have cast out devils? and in thy name done many wonderful works? And then will I profess unto them, I never knew you: depart from me, ye that work iniquity."</em> — Matthew 7:21-23</p>
</blockquote>
<p>These are the most terrifying words in the entire Bible. Not because they are spoken to atheists. Not because they are addressed to pagans or witches or open enemies of God. They are spoken to people who called Jesus "Lord." People who prophesied. People who cast out demons. People who did wonderful works in His name. And Jesus says to them: "I never knew you."</p>
<p>Howard Pittman — a Baptist minister for thirty-five years — was shown exactly how this deception operates. He was shown the mechanism. The prescription. The doctor. The patient. The pill. And the name of the pill is "Placebo."</p>
<p>Satan does not fear the church. He administers it.</p>

<h2 id="the-definition-of-a-placebo">The Definition of a Placebo</h2>
<p>Webster's Dictionary defines a placebo as "a medication prescribed more for the mental relief of the patient than for its actual effect on a disorder." The word itself comes from the Latin — "I shall please." A placebo is designed not to heal, but to make the patient believe he has been healed. The medication is fake. The relief is real — but only in the mind. The disease remains. The patient smiles. And the doctor knows the truth.</p>
<p>Howard Pittman understood this definition. And when God showed him what was happening to the church — to billions of professing Christians across the world — the word "placebo" was the only word that fit. Not metaphorically. Precisely.</p>
<h3 id="dr-satans-prescription">Dr. Satan's Prescription</h3>
<p>Picture a doctor's office. The patient comes in. He is sick — spiritually sick, sin-sick, dying inside. He knows something is wrong. He feels the weight of guilt, the emptiness, the fear of death, the distance from God. He wants a cure. He is desperate for a cure.</p>
<p>Enter the doctor.</p>
<p>But this doctor is not interested in healing the patient. This doctor has one goal: to keep the patient sick while making him believe he is well. And the doctor's name is Satan.</p>
<p>Satan — "Dr. Satan," as Pittman describes him — examines the patient. He takes note of the symptoms. He listens to the complaints. And then he writes a prescription. The prescription looks legitimate. It comes on official paper. It references real ingredients. It sounds medical. It sounds authoritative.</p>
<p>The prescription reads: "Church attendance. Sunday mornings. Sing the hymns. Say the prayers. Repeat the creeds. Accept Jesus as your Savior. Get baptized. Join a Bible study. Teach Sunday school. Tithe. Volunteer. Tell others you are born again."</p>
<p>The patient fills the prescription. He takes the pill. And the pill works — not on the disease, but on the mind. The patient feels better. The guilt subsides. The fear diminishes. The emptiness is masked. He walks out of the office with a smile on his face, declaring to everyone he meets: "I have been healed. I am born again. I am saved."</p>
<p>But the disease remains. The sin remains. The self remains on the throne. And the doctor knows it.</p>
<h3 id="the-sugarcoated-religion">The Sugarcoated Religion</h3>
<p>This is what Pittman was shown. This is the placebo. It is not atheism. It is not paganism. It is not open rebellion against God. Those are crude weapons. Satan is far more sophisticated than that.</p>
<p>The placebo is sugarcoated religion. It is Christianity without the cross. It is faith without surrender. It is confession without transformation. It is the form of godliness — exactly as Paul warned Timothy — without the power thereof.</p>
<p>The sugar coating makes the pill go down easy. The patient does not gag. He does not resist. He swallows it willingly, eagerly, gratefully. And why wouldn't he? It tastes good. It feels good. It costs nothing — no real sacrifice, no real death to self, no real surrender of the throne.</p>
<p>Satan's genius is not in creating a religion that looks obviously false. His genius is in creating a religion that looks almost entirely true. The placebo contains real ingredients — real scripture, real worship, real community, real emotion. But the active ingredient — the one thing that would actually cure the disease — is missing. And that missing ingredient is total lordship. Complete surrender. The death of self.</p>
<h2 id="the-mouth-professing-christian">The Mouth-Professing Christian</h2>
<h3 id="saying-the-right-things">Saying the Right Things</h3>
<p>Howard Pittman identified a specific term for the billions trapped in this deception: "mouth-professing Christians." These are not hypocrites in the traditional sense. They are not secretly living in obvious sin while pretending to be righteous. Many of them are sincere. Many of them genuinely believe they are saved. That is what makes the placebo so effective.</p>
<p>A mouth-professing Christian says all the right things. He confesses Jesus as Lord. He declares himself born again. He quotes scripture. He prays before meals. He tells others about his faith. His mouth professes everything that a genuine Christian would profess.</p>
<p>But there is one exception. One critical, fatal, eternal exception.</p>
<p>He will never be allowed to LIVE the life he confesses with his mouth.</p>
<p>This is the placebo's design. The patient is given permission to SAY anything. He can say "Jesus is Lord" a thousand times a day. He can say "I am born again" to every person he meets. He can say "I surrender all" while singing in church on Sunday morning. Satan does not care what the patient says. Satan cares what the patient does — or more precisely, what the patient does NOT do.</p>
<p>The mouth-professing Christian will never truly surrender the throne of his life to Christ. He will never truly die to self. He will never truly make Jesus Lord — not Savior only, but Lord. He will never truly allow God to direct his steps, control his desires, reshape his will, dismantle his comfort, and rebuild him from the foundation up.</p>
<p>He will say all of this. He will never do it.</p>
<h3 id="the-one-exception">The One Exception</h3>
<p>This is the prescription's hidden clause. The fine print. The clause that the patient never reads because the sugarcoating is too sweet, the relief too immediate, the comfort too great.</p>
<p>Satan's placebo allows everything except the one thing that matters. You can attend church — every Sunday, every Wednesday, every revival, every conference. You can sing — loudly, passionately, with tears streaming down your face. You can pray — long prayers, eloquent prayers, prayers that move entire congregations. You can teach — Sunday school, Bible study, seminary courses. You can preach — from pulpits, from street corners, from television studios.</p>
<p>You can do all of this. You must do all of this. The placebo requires it. The form of godliness demands activity. The busier the patient, the more convinced he becomes that the medication is working. Activity becomes evidence. Busyness becomes proof. The patient points to his schedule and says, "Look at all I do for God. Surely I am saved."</p>
<p>But the one thing — the ONLY thing — that would transform the placebo into actual medicine is the one thing the prescription forbids: genuine, total, unconditional surrender of self.</p>
<h2 id="the-doctor-must-convince-the-patient">The Doctor Must Convince the Patient</h2>
<h3 id="the-art-of-deception">The Art of Deception</h3>
<p>Pittman makes a critical observation about how the placebo works. He says: "The doctor must convince the patient that it is real."</p>
<p>This is essential. A placebo only works if the patient believes it is genuine medicine. If the patient knows the pill is sugar, he will not experience relief. The deception must be complete. The patient must be fully convinced — in his mind, in his heart, in his emotions — that what he has received is the real thing.</p>
<p>Satan understands this perfectly. He does not hand out placebos with a wink and a nod. He does not say, "Here, take this fake religion." He presents it with absolute sincerity. He wraps it in scripture. He delivers it through genuine-seeming pastors, through emotional worship services, through life-changing altar calls, through powerful testimonies, through miraculous-seeming experiences.</p>
<p>The patient must walk away convinced. And he does. He goes on his merry way — Pittman's exact words — declaring that he has been born again. And he genuinely believes it. He is not lying. He is not pretending. He has been deceived so thoroughly that his conviction is absolute.</p>
<p>This is what makes the placebo infinitely more dangerous than open atheism or blatant false religion. The atheist knows he does not believe in God. The pagan knows he worships other gods. But the mouth-professing Christian believes — truly, sincerely, deeply believes — that he belongs to Christ. And he will believe this all the way to the throne of God, where he will hear the words: "I never knew you."</p>
<h3 id="the-emotional-counterfeit">The Emotional Counterfeit</h3>
<p>One of the placebo's most powerful ingredients is emotion. Satan knows that humans equate strong feelings with genuine spiritual experience. If a worship service makes you cry, it must be the Holy Spirit. If a sermon gives you chills, God must be speaking. If an altar call fills you with warmth, you must be saved.</p>
<p>But emotion is not evidence of salvation. Emotion is evidence of emotion. A skilled musician can make an entire auditorium weep without a single word of scripture. A gifted speaker can produce goosebumps with nothing more than vocal inflection and timing. A well-designed environment — the lighting, the music, the crowd, the expectation — can generate feelings that are indistinguishable from genuine spiritual encounter.</p>
<p>The placebo exploits this. It creates environments saturated with emotional triggers. The patient takes the pill, feels something powerful, and concludes: "This is real. I have met God. I am saved." The emotion becomes the evidence. The feeling becomes the foundation.</p>
<p>And when the feeling fades — as it always does — the patient returns for another dose. Another service. Another conference. Another revival. Another emotional experience to reassure him that the medication is working. This is why so many Christians live on an emotional roller coaster — mountain-top experiences followed by valleys of doubt, followed by another mountain-top experience. They are addicted to the placebo. They need the next dose to maintain the illusion.</p>
<h2 id="the-exact-treatment-most-christians-use-today">The Exact Treatment Most Christians Use Today</h2>
<h3 id="the-scale-of-deception">The Scale of Deception</h3>
<p>Pittman does not mince words. He says plainly: "This is the exact treatment most mouth-professing Christians use today."</p>
<p>Not some. Not a few. Not a fringe group. MOST.</p>
<p>This aligns perfectly with what God showed Pittman at the throne — that 97.5 percent of professing Christians are not truly saved. The placebo is not a minor problem. It is not a theological nuance. It is the dominant form of Christianity practiced on earth today. The vast majority of people who call themselves Christians are taking a spiritual placebo prescribed by the enemy of their souls.</p>
<p>Consider the implications. Billions of people across the globe identify as Christian. They attend services. They celebrate holidays. They mark life events — births, marriages, deaths — with religious ceremony. They pray. They give. They serve. And according to what God revealed to Howard Pittman, nearly all of them are deceived.</p>
<p>The church buildings are full. The pews are packed. The offerings are generous. The programs are impressive. And almost none of it is real.</p>
<h3 id="the-half-truth-strategy">The Half-Truth Strategy</h3>
<p>Satan's placebo does not rely on outright lies. It relies on half-truths — which are far more dangerous.</p>
<p>A lie can be identified and rejected. "There is no God" is a lie that many people recognize immediately. "The Bible is fiction" is a claim that crumbles under honest investigation. These are crude weapons, and while they work on some, they fail on many.</p>
<p>But a half-truth? A half-truth is almost impossible to detect because it contains real truth. It is mixed in. The real truth provides cover for the false part.</p>
<p>"Accept Jesus as your Savior and you will be saved." This is a half-truth. The true part: Jesus is the Savior. Faith in Him is essential. The false part: the omission. Jesus must also be LORD. Not just Savior — Lord. Savior means He rescues you. Lord means He rules you. The placebo offers the rescue without the rule. It offers salvation without surrender. It offers heaven without the cross.</p>
<p>"Believe in your heart and confess with your mouth." This is scripture — Romans 10:9. But the placebo version strips it of context. What does it mean to believe? What does it mean to confess? The placebo reduces belief to intellectual agreement and confession to verbal repetition. But biblical belief is trust — full, complete, life-altering trust. And biblical confession is not a one-time verbal formula. It is a continuous declaration of allegiance that governs every aspect of life.</p>
<p>The half-truth gets the patient through the door. It gets him to swallow the pill. And once swallowed, the patient defends the pill with his life — because it contains just enough real truth to seem legitimate.</p>
<h2 id="the-power-of-words">The Power of Words</h2>
<h3 id="every-idle-word-judged">Every Idle Word Judged</h3>
<p>Jesus said something in Matthew 12:36-37 that most Christians glide past without fully grasping its weight: "But I say unto you, that every idle word that men shall speak, they shall give account thereof in the day of judgment. For by thy words thou shalt be justified, and by thy words thou shalt be condemned."</p>
<p>Every idle word. Not every sermon. Not every prayer. Every word. Including the idle ones — the careless ones, the throwaway lines, the casual declarations, the unconsidered confessions.</p>
<p>Words have spiritual power. This is not metaphor. This is spiritual law. When a mouth-professing Christian says "Jesus is Lord" but lives as though self is lord, he is not merely being inconsistent. He is speaking words that carry weight in the spiritual realm — words that he will account for before God's throne.</p>
<p>The placebo teaches people to speak without living. It teaches them to confess without obeying. It trains an entire generation of Christians to use spiritual language as decoration — verbal wallpaper for lives that are fundamentally unchanged.</p>
<h3 id="the-difference-between-confession-and-surrender">The Difference Between Confession and Surrender</h3>
<p>Confession says: "Jesus is Lord."
Surrender says: "Jesus is Lord — of my money, my time, my career, my relationships, my desires, my fears, my future, my comfort, my reputation, my very life."</p>
<p>Confession costs nothing.
Surrender costs everything.</p>
<p>The placebo offers confession. God requires surrender.</p>
<p>This is not legalism. This is not works-based salvation. This is the fundamental nature of genuine faith. As James wrote: "Faith without works is dead" (James 2:26). A faith that does not transform is not faith at all. It is agreement — intellectual assent to a set of propositions. And intellectual assent has never saved anyone.</p>
<p>The mouth-professing Christian agrees that Jesus is Lord. The surrendered Christian submits to His lordship. The difference is infinite. The gap between them is the gap between heaven and hell. And the placebo is designed to make that gap invisible.</p>
<h2 id="breaking-free-from-the-placebo">Breaking Free From the Placebo</h2>
<h3 id="the-diagnosis">The Diagnosis</h3>
<p>The first step in breaking free from any deception is recognizing that you have been deceived. And this is the hardest step of all. The placebo is designed to prevent exactly this recognition. The patient is comfortable. The symptoms have been masked. The emotional experiences reinforce the belief that everything is fine.</p>
<p>But the truth remains: if self is still on the throne — if YOUR desires, YOUR comfort, YOUR plans, YOUR preferences still govern your life — then the placebo is still working. You are still taking the pill. The prescription has not been filled with genuine medicine.</p>
<p>Ask yourself: Has my faith cost me anything? Has following Christ required genuine sacrifice — not the sacrifice of attending church on Sunday (which costs nothing but an hour), but the sacrifice of my will? Have I truly surrendered the right to run my own life?</p>
<p>If the answer is no — if the honest, painful, undecorated answer is no — then the placebo has been working exactly as designed.</p>
<h3 id="the-cure">The Cure</h3>
<p>The cure for the placebo is not more religion. It is not a better church, a better pastor, a better worship team, a better Bible study. The cure is the very thing the placebo was designed to prevent: total, absolute, unconditional surrender to the lordship of Jesus Christ.</p>
<p>This means stepping off the throne. It means acknowledging that YOU are not the lord of your life — that you have no right to your own plans, your own timeline, your own comfort, your own preferences. It means submitting every area of your life — not just the areas you are willing to give up, but ALL of them — to the authority of Christ.</p>
<p>This is what it means to be born again. Not the placebo version — the real version. Death to self. Resurrection in Christ. A new creature. Old things passed away. All things become new.</p>
<p>The narrow gate. The road less traveled. The path that few find.</p>
<p>And 97.5 percent of professing Christians have never walked through it.</p>
