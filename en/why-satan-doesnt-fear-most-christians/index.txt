15:T5a96,<h1 id="why-satan-doesnt-fear-most-christians-a-devil-worshippers-confession">Why Satan Doesn't Fear Most Christians: A Devil Worshipper's Confession</h1>
<h2 id="the-ancient-pattern">The Ancient Pattern</h2>
<blockquote>
<p><em>"Having a form of godliness, but denying the power thereof: from such turn away."</em> — 2 Timothy 3:5</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p><em>"Thou believest that there is one God; thou doest well: the devils also believe, and tremble."</em> — James 2:19</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Paul warned Timothy about people in the last days who would have "a form of godliness but deny the power thereof." James delivered an even more cutting rebuke: you believe in God? Congratulations. The demons believe too — and at least they tremble. The demons know exactly who God is and what He can do. They respond with fear. Most Christians do not even manage that much.</p>
<p>John Ramirez spent over twenty-five years as a devil worshipper. He operated within the demonic hierarchy. He carried out assignments against the church, against Christians, against families. He had five principality demons assigned to him. He could astral project, place spirits on neighborhoods, and execute death rituals. And from that position — from inside the enemy's camp — he observed the church.</p>
<p>What he saw was devastating. Most Christians could not pray for even one hour. They had no fasting life. They had no genuine relationship with God. They had a form of godliness — the right clothes, the right Bible, the right vocabulary — but no power. They were, as Paul described, a shell without substance, a container without content, a name without reality.</p>
<p>Howard Pittman saw the same thing from God's perspective: 97.5 percent of professing Christians rejected. "Never made Me Lord." Ramirez confirms this from the enemy's perspective: most Christians were easy targets. No covering. No authority. No connection to the power of Christ. The two testimonies converge on the same horrifying truth: the church is full of people who think they are saved and are not.</p>

<h2 id="the-prayer-gap-one-hour-versus-all-night">The Prayer Gap: One Hour Versus All Night</h2>
<h3 id="the-devil-worshippers-prayer-life">The Devil Worshipper's Prayer Life</h3>
<p>When Ramirez was serving the devil, he prayed ALL NIGHT LONG. Not for thirty minutes. Not for an hour. All night. Hours upon hours of concentrated, focused communion with the demonic entities that empowered him. This was not a burden. This was not an obligation he resented. It was the fuel that powered his assignments. It was the connection that maintained his authority. It was the pipeline through which demonic intelligence, instruction, and power flowed into his life.</p>
<p>Devil worshippers understand something that most Christians do not: the spiritual realm is accessed through sustained communication. Power flows through the channel of prayer. Authority is maintained through continuous contact with the source of that authority. Ramirez knew that if he stopped praying, his connection would weaken. His assignments would fail. His authority would diminish. So he prayed — all night, every night, with intensity and focus and dedication.</p>
<h3 id="the-christians-prayer-life">The Christian's Prayer Life</h3>
<p>And then Ramirez looked at the church.</p>
<p>Most Christians could not pray for even ONE HOUR. Jesus Himself had asked this question in the Garden of Gethsemane: "Could ye not watch with me one hour?" (Matthew 26:40). Two thousand years later, the answer is still no. Most Christians cannot sustain one hour of focused prayer. Many cannot sustain fifteen minutes. Some cannot sustain five.</p>
<p>The implications are staggering. A devil worshipper is praying against you all night. You cannot pray for one hour. A Satanist is maintaining continuous contact with the principalities assigned to destroy you. You check in with God for five minutes before breakfast — if you remember. A witch is fasting, sacrificing, bleeding, and dedicating every ounce of spiritual energy to cursing your family. You skipped your quiet time because you were running late for work.</p>
<p>This is not a fair fight. It is not a fight at all. It is a massacre. The enemy's soldiers are fully trained, fully committed, and fully engaged. The church's soldiers are untrained, uncommitted, and almost entirely disengaged from the spiritual reality of the war they are in.</p>
<p>Ramirez saw this with absolute clarity from inside the enemy's camp. He knew — with operational certainty — that most Christians posed no threat whatsoever to the kingdom of darkness. Their prayer life was nonexistent. Their spiritual authority was theoretical. Their connection to Christ was nominal. They were, in every practical sense, defenseless.</p>
<h2 id="the-spiritual-realm-is-more-real-than-the-natural">"The Spiritual Realm Is More Real Than the Natural"</h2>
<h3 id="a-reality-most-christians-deny">A Reality Most Christians Deny</h3>
<p>Ramirez makes a statement that should be written on every church wall in America: "The spiritual realm is more real than the natural." This is not mysticism. This is not superstition. This is the testimony of a man who operated in both realms for over two decades and who knows, from direct experience, which one has primacy.</p>
<p>The natural world — the world of jobs, bills, politics, sports, entertainment, and social media — is the world that most Christians live in. They give it most of their attention, most of their energy, most of their time, and most of their concern. The spiritual realm, if they acknowledge it at all, is an abstraction — something they believe in theoretically but do not experience practically.</p>
<p>Ramirez experienced the spiritual realm practically. He left his body and traveled in it. He spoke to entities who inhabited it. He received assignments from the principalities who governed it. He placed spirits on neighborhoods and individuals within it. For him, the spiritual realm was not theology. It was geography. It had locations, inhabitants, hierarchies, and rules of engagement.</p>
<p>The Bible confirms this perspective. Paul says we wrestle NOT against flesh and blood but against principalities and powers (Ephesians 6:12). The Book of Daniel describes a spiritual battle over Persia that delayed an angel for twenty-one days (Daniel 10:13). Elisha's servant saw the hills full of horses and chariots of fire (2 Kings 6:17). The spiritual realm is not a metaphor. It is the primary reality. The natural world is the derivative, the shadow, the temporary expression of eternal spiritual forces.</p>
<p>Most Christians live as materialists who happen to believe in God. They make decisions based on natural logic. They assess threats based on physical circumstances. They measure success by material outcomes. And they are utterly blind to the spiritual forces that actually determine the course of their lives.</p>
<h3 id="no-prayer-life-no-fasting-no-relationship">No Prayer Life, No Fasting, No Relationship</h3>
<p>Ramirez catalogued the specific deficiencies he observed in the Christians he targeted:</p>
<p><strong>No prayer life.</strong> Not merely insufficient prayer, but effectively no prayer at all. The kind of prayer most Christians practice — a brief request list before meals or before bed — does not constitute a prayer life. It constitutes a prayer habit, and barely that. A prayer life involves sustained, focused, intimate communion with God — the kind of communion that builds spiritual authority and maintains spiritual covering.</p>
<p><strong>No fasting.</strong> Fasting is the practice of denying the flesh to strengthen the spirit. It is commanded by Jesus (Matthew 6:16-18 — "WHEN you fast," not "IF you fast"). It is practiced throughout scripture by those who needed breakthrough — Moses, Elijah, Daniel, Esther, Jesus Himself. And it is almost entirely absent from the modern church. Most Christians have never fasted a single day in their lives. Meanwhile, practitioners of the occult fast regularly as part of their spiritual discipline.</p>
<p><strong>No relationship with God.</strong> This is the core deficiency from which all others flow. The Christians Ramirez observed had no genuine, living, dynamic relationship with Jesus Christ. They had religion. They had routine. They had ritual. But they did not have the intimate, moment-by-moment connection with the living God that produces spiritual power, spiritual discernment, and spiritual authority.</p>
<p>They had "form of godliness but no power." Right clothes and a Bible, but "no connection with Jesus Christ because he was out of His will, out of His promises, out of His divine purpose."</p>
<h2 id="the-devils-assignment-against-the-church">The Devil's Assignment Against the Church</h2>
<h3 id="five-principalities-on-assignment">Five Principalities on Assignment</h3>
<p>Five principalities were assigned specifically to "operate the church, cut down the church, and cut down the opportunity for people" to say yes to Jesus Christ. This is not a general spiritual opposition. This is a targeted, specific, military-style assignment. Five of the highest-ranking demons in Satan's hierarchy were dedicated to the single purpose of neutralizing the church.</p>
<p>Consider what this means. The devil does not ignore the church. He does not dismiss it. He assigns his best and most powerful agents against it. Five principalities — entities that Howard Pittman described as the generals of the demonic army — devoted to the destruction of the church's effectiveness.</p>
<p>Their assignment had three components:</p>
<p><strong>Operate the church.</strong> This is the most frightening of the three. Not merely oppose the church, but OPERATE it. The principalities were assigned to influence the direction, decisions, and doctrine of churches from within. To steer them toward ineffectiveness. To ensure that what happens in church buildings on Sunday morning produces no actual spiritual fruit. To make the church a place of activity without authority, worship without power, and teaching without transformation.</p>
<p><strong>Cut down the church.</strong> To diminish the church's impact. To reduce its effectiveness. To weaken it through division, scandal, compromise, and complacency. Every church split, every pastoral scandal, every doctrinal compromise, every drift toward worldliness serves this assignment. The principalities are not passive observers of the church's decline. They are actively engineering it.</p>
<p><strong>Cut down the opportunity for people to say yes to Jesus.</strong> This is the ultimate goal. Every assignment, every strategy, every deployment of demonic resources has this single objective: to prevent human beings from entering a genuine relationship with Jesus Christ. The devil does not care if you go to church, sing songs, post Bible verses, or call yourself a Christian — as long as you never actually surrender your life to Jesus Christ.</p>
<h3 id="breaking-christians-through-witchcraft">Breaking Christians Through Witchcraft</h3>
<p>Ramirez did not merely observe Christian weakness. He exploited it. He broke Christians through witchcraft. He put witchcraft directly on individual believers — targeted, personalized spiritual attacks designed to destroy their faith, their families, their health, and their lives.</p>
<p>He chased "everything down that represents Jesus Christ" — Christians, families, churches. No target was too small. No believer was too insignificant. If you represented Jesus Christ in any way, you were on the enemy's target list. The question was not whether you would be attacked, but whether you had the spiritual covering to survive the attack.</p>
<h2 id="i-was-going-to-kill-you">"I Was Going to Kill You"</h2>
<h3 id="the-key-statement">The Key Statement</h3>
<p>Ramirez's most chilling confession cuts through every theological debate about spiritual warfare with the precision of a machete:</p>
<p>"If you don't care who you work with, if you don't care what you know, if you don't care who you work for — if you're Catholic, Christian, or believer — I was going to kill you UNLESS you had a real relationship with Jesus Christ."</p>
<p>Parse this carefully. The threat was universal. Catholic, Christian, believer — denomination did not matter. Church membership did not matter. Doctrinal knowledge did not matter. The ONLY thing that protected a person was "a real relationship with Jesus Christ."</p>
<p>Not a theoretical relationship. Not a nominal relationship. Not a Sunday-morning relationship. A REAL relationship. The kind that produces prayer life, fasting, obedience, spiritual authority, and the covering of the blood of Jesus.</p>
<p>Everyone else was vulnerable. Everyone else was a target. Everyone else was, in Ramirez's operational assessment, killable.</p>
<h3 id="the-easiest-targets">The Easiest Targets</h3>
<p>Those without real spiritual covering were "easy to destroy because they will not seek any spiritual help." They did not believe they were in a war. They did not believe they needed protection. They did not seek deliverance, intercession, or spiritual counsel. They lived in a bubble of comfortable religion, unaware that they were being targeted by principality demons and attacked by devil worshippers.</p>
<p>But the absolute EASIEST targets were those who did not believe in the devil at all. Christians who dismissed spiritual warfare as superstition. Believers who thought demons were metaphors. Church members who considered the devil a medieval fantasy. These were the people Ramirez could destroy with the least effort — because they did not even know they had an enemy.</p>
<p>This is the genius of the devil's strategy. Convince the world that you do not exist, and you can operate with impunity. If a soldier on a battlefield convinces himself that there is no enemy, he removes his armor, puts down his weapon, and stands in the open. He does not become safer. He becomes the easiest kill on the field.</p>
<h2 id="the-confirmation-of-pittmans-placebo">The Confirmation of Pittman's Placebo</h2>
<h3 id="two-witnesses-one-truth">Two Witnesses, One Truth</h3>
<p>Howard Pittman stood before God's throne and was told that 97.5 percent of professing Christians would be rejected. God described a "placebo" religion — sugarcoated, shallow, half-truths that produce a feeling of salvation without the reality of it. The church was full of people who could say it, teach it, preach it — but could not LIVE it.</p>
<p>John Ramirez, from the opposite side of the spiritual battlefield, confirms every element of Pittman's revelation:</p>
<p><strong>Pittman said the church lacks power.</strong> Ramirez confirms: "form of godliness but no power."</p>
<p><strong>Pittman said most Christians serve self.</strong> Ramirez confirms: they had no genuine relationship with Christ — "out of His will, out of His promises, out of His divine purpose."</p>
<p><strong>Pittman said Satan administers a placebo.</strong> Ramirez confirms: five principalities assigned to "operate the church" — ensuring it functions as the placebo Satan designed.</p>
<p><strong>Pittman said 97.5 percent would be rejected.</strong> Ramirez confirms: only those with "a real relationship with Jesus Christ" could survive his attacks — and these were the rare exception, not the rule.</p>
<p>Two witnesses. One from God's throne room. One from the devil's inner circle. Both telling the same story. Both delivering the same warning. Both confirming the same devastating truth: the church, as it currently operates, is failing almost everyone who walks through its doors.</p>
<h3 id="the-convergence-of-evidence">The Convergence of Evidence</h3>
<p>When independent witnesses from completely different vantage points produce the same testimony, the evidence becomes virtually impossible to dismiss. Pittman did not know Ramirez. Ramirez did not know Pittman. Their experiences occurred in different decades, different locations, different spiritual contexts. Yet their descriptions of the church's condition are identical.</p>
<p>This convergence is the pattern of biblical witness. Deuteronomy 19:15 requires two or three witnesses to establish a matter. Jesus confirmed this principle in Matthew 18:16. The testimony of two or three witnesses was sufficient to condemn or acquit in ancient Israel.</p>
<p>We now have three witnesses — Pittman (from God's perspective), the Jesuit infiltration documented in <em>Catholic Confidential</em> (from the infiltrators' perspective), and Ramirez (from the enemy's perspective) — all delivering the same verdict: the church is compromised, powerless, and dangerously deceived about its own spiritual condition.</p>
<h2 id="what-real-spiritual-authority-looks-like">What Real Spiritual Authority Looks Like</h2>
<h3 id="the-christians-ramirez-could-not-touch">The Christians Ramirez Could Not Touch</h3>
<p>Not everyone was an easy target. Ramirez encountered Christians he could not break. The youth group with the wall of fire. The woman who survived twenty-one days of death ritual. The man who disappeared from the rooftop before Ramirez could kill him. These were the exceptions — the remnant within the church that had genuine spiritual authority.</p>
<p>What set them apart was not denomination. Not theology. Not church size or worship style or preaching quality. What set them apart was the reality of their relationship with Jesus Christ. They prayed — not for five minutes, but for hours. They fasted — regularly, sacrificially, as a spiritual discipline. They walked in obedience — not to an institution, but to the Holy Spirit. They were covered by the blood of Jesus — not as a theological concept, but as a spiritual reality that created an impenetrable barrier around their lives.</p>
<p>These are the Christians that the devil fears. Not the ones with the biggest churches. Not the ones with the most followers. Not the ones with the best theology. The ones who PRAY. The ones who FAST. The ones who OBEY. The ones who are genuinely, intimately, unshakably connected to Jesus Christ.</p>
<p>The devil does not fear your denomination. He does not fear your seminary degree. He does not fear your worship band or your building fund or your small group curriculum. He fears the blood of Jesus applied through genuine faith. He fears the authority of believers who have been on their knees for hours. He fears the power of fasting that weakens the flesh and strengthens the spirit.</p>
<p>And he knows — with operational certainty — that most Christians have none of these things.</p>
<h3 id="the-call-to-real-warfare">The Call to Real Warfare</h3>
<p>This is not a call to fear. It is a call to reality. The spiritual war is real. The enemy is organized. The church is largely unprepared. But the weapons of our warfare are "not carnal, but mighty through God to the pulling down of strong holds" (2 Corinthians 10:4).</p>
<p>Every believer has access to these weapons. Prayer. Fasting. The blood of Jesus. The Word of God. The name of Jesus. The full armor of God described in Ephesians 6. These weapons are sufficient to defeat every principality, every power, every ruler of darkness, and every spiritual wickedness in high places. But they must be USED. They must be practiced. They must become the daily reality of the believer's life — not optional accessories to a comfortable religion, but essential equipment for survival in a war zone.</p>
<p>John Ramirez knows what defeats the devil. He served the devil for twenty-five years and saw firsthand what the kingdom of darkness cannot overcome. His testimony is not a scare tactic. It is a strategic briefing. The intelligence is clear. The enemy's weaknesses are exposed. The question is whether the church will receive this intelligence and act on it — or continue sleeping while the principalities operate freely.</p>
