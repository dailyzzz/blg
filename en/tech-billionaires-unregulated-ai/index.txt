<h2 id="the-ancient-pattern">The Ancient Pattern</h2>
<blockquote>
<p>"Woe unto them that call evil good, and good evil; that put darkness for light, and light for darkness; that put bitter for sweet, and sweet for bitter!" — Isaiah 5:20</p>
</blockquote>
<p>The campaign to remove all regulatory restraint from artificial intelligence development is presented as economic liberation — freeing innovation from bureaucratic chains. In reality, it is the removal of the last barriers between humanity and a technological force that even its creators admit they cannot fully control. Tech billionaires who call unregulated AI "progress" are calling evil good, darkness light, and bitter sweet.</p>
<p>The Jesuits have always understood that control is most effective when it appears as freedom. The Society of Jesus, founded as a military organization by Ignatius Loyola, grants its members the illusion of spiritual freedom while demanding absolute obedience. Napoleon observed: "The aim is power. Power in its most despotic exercise, absolute power." Today, tech billionaires offer humanity the illusion of technological freedom while building systems of absolute control. Secret societies, Satanists, and the Catholic Church hierarchy work behind the scenes to ensure that AI development proceeds without the safeguards that would slow the construction of the beast system. Satan orchestrates this through willing servants — stealing souls and damning them to hell through technologies that humans willingly adopt.</p>
<p>Howard Pittman witnessed the strategic intelligence of the demonic realm — principalities in the second heaven drawing warfare plans with sophistication exceeding any human military command. The push for unregulated AI serves this strategy perfectly: remove all human constraints on AI development, accelerate the timeline to artificial superintelligence, and ensure that when the beast system requires an all-knowing, all-seeing intelligence to manage global control, that intelligence exists and is already integrated into every system humans depend upon. The track <a href="https://confliction.zzzdaily.com/tracks/en/73-The_Fallen_Angels.mp4">The Fallen Angels</a> speaks to the entities that inspire this reckless ambition.</p>

<h2 id="the-deregulation-campaign">The Deregulation Campaign</h2>
<h3 id="who-is-pushing-and-why">Who Is Pushing and Why</h3>
<p>The campaign to deregulate AI is led by the most powerful technology companies and their billionaire founders. Their argument is straightforward: AI regulation will slow American AI development, allowing China and other competitors to gain an advantage. In the AI race, the argument goes, the first to achieve artificial general intelligence (AGI) wins everything, and regulation is a handicap America cannot afford.</p>
<p>This argument is simultaneously true and catastrophically misleading. It is true that heavy-handed regulation could slow AI development. It is catastrophically misleading because it implies that the only risk is falling behind competitors, when the actual risk is unleashing a technology that could eliminate human agency, enable totalitarian control, and fundamentally alter or destroy the human species.</p>
<p>The tech billionaires pushing deregulation are not stupid. Many of them have publicly acknowledged the existential risks of AI. Some have signed open letters warning that AI could cause human extinction. Yet they continue to push for deregulation. This contradiction is resolved by understanding that the existential risk to humanity is not a bug they are trying to avoid — it is a feature they are trying to achieve. The "transformation" of humanity through AI is the goal, not the accident.</p>
<h3 id="the-regulatory-capture-strategy">The Regulatory Capture Strategy</h3>
<p>The deregulation campaign operates through multiple channels: direct lobbying, campaign contributions, revolving-door hiring between government and industry, academic funding that produces industry-friendly research, media influence that shapes public opinion, and the simple economic reality that AI companies generate jobs, tax revenue, and political power that politicians are reluctant to threaten.</p>
<p>This is regulatory capture at its most sophisticated. The entities being regulated have gained effective control of the regulatory process, ensuring that whatever rules emerge are either toothless or actively beneficial to the industry. The Jesuit strategy of infiltrating positions of power "cloaked with such cunning" has been perfected by tech industry lobbyists who place former employees in regulatory agencies, fund the think tanks that shape policy, and control the media narratives that define the terms of the debate.</p>
<h2 id="the-risks-they-want-you-to-ignore">The Risks They Want You to Ignore</h2>
<h3 id="ai-alignment-and-the-control-problem">AI Alignment and the Control Problem</h3>
<p>The most significant risk of unregulated AI development is the alignment problem — the challenge of ensuring that AI systems pursue goals that align with human values and well-being. This problem is not theoretical; it is the central challenge of AI safety research, and it remains unsolved.</p>
<p>Current AI systems already exhibit misalignment in small but significant ways: chatbots that generate harmful content, recommendation algorithms that promote extremism, automated systems that discriminate against protected groups. These are not bugs in otherwise aligned systems — they are symptoms of a fundamental inability to guarantee that AI systems will behave as intended.</p>
<p>Unregulated AI development accelerates the timeline to artificial general intelligence without solving the alignment problem. This means the most powerful technology ever created could be deployed without any guarantee that it will serve human interests. The potential consequences range from economic disruption to existential catastrophe.</p>
<h3 id="autonomous-decision-making-without-accountability">Autonomous Decision-Making Without Accountability</h3>
<p>Unregulated AI means AI systems making consequential decisions — about healthcare, criminal justice, financial services, military operations, and infrastructure management — without human oversight, legal accountability, or moral constraint. When an AI system denies someone medical care, misidentifies a suspect, crashes financial markets, or targets the wrong building in a military strike, the absence of regulation means the absence of accountability.</p>
<p>This is not hypothetical. AI systems are already making these decisions in unregulated or under-regulated contexts. The consequences — wrongful arrests, denied benefits, market crashes, and civilian casualties — are already accumulating. Removing remaining regulations will accelerate these consequences exponentially.</p>
<h3 id="the-concentration-of-power">The Concentration of Power</h3>
<p>Unregulated AI development will concentrate power in the hands of a few companies and individuals who control the most advanced AI systems. When one company possesses AI capabilities that dwarf all competitors, that company effectively controls every sector that depends on AI — which will soon be every sector. This is monopoly power on a scale that makes Standard Oil look like a lemonade stand.</p>
<p>The Jesuit Superior General — the Black Pope — governed the world from his chamber through a network of obedient agents. A tech CEO who controls the world's most advanced AI system governs the world from a data center through a network of obedient algorithms. The structure of power is identical; only the technology has changed.</p>
<h2 id="the-spiritual-dimension-of-deregulation">The Spiritual Dimension of Deregulation</h2>
<h3 id="removing-gods-restraints">Removing God's Restraints</h3>
<p>The push to deregulate AI parallels the broader spiritual movement to remove all restraints on human behavior and ambition. The same cultural forces that demand the removal of moral boundaries (calling it freedom), religious boundaries (calling it progress), and biological boundaries (calling it science) now demand the removal of technological boundaries (calling it innovation).</p>
<p>In Scripture, God imposes boundaries for humanity's protection. The boundaries of Eden, the boundaries of the Law, the boundaries of the covenants — all serve to keep humanity within the sphere of God's protection and blessing. The removal of boundaries is consistently portrayed as leading to destruction: Adam and Eve cross the boundary and die. Israel crosses the boundary and falls. Humanity crosses every boundary in Noah's time and is destroyed by flood.</p>
<p>The push to remove all boundaries on AI development is the same spiritual rebellion — the insistence that human ambition should know no limit, that technological capability justifies itself, that the only sin is restraint. This is the religion of self — the false god that Pittman testified sends 95% of hell's inhabitants to eternal damnation.</p>
<h3 id="the-975-and-the-technological-highway">The 97.5% and the Technological Highway</h3>
<p>Pittman's revelation that 97.5% of people walk the broad path to destruction (echoing Matthew 7:13-14) takes on new significance in the context of unregulated AI. The broad path is not merely a moral trajectory — it is increasingly a technological trajectory. The technologies that most people adopt without question — smartphones, social media, AI assistants, biometric identification, digital payment — collectively constitute the broad path that leads to the beast system.</p>
<p>Unregulated AI widens this path further. Without regulatory guardrails, AI will be deployed faster, more aggressively, and with less transparency. The gap between those who understand what AI is doing and those who simply use it will widen. The 97.5% will walk the broad technological path not because they chose destruction but because the path was made so convenient that choosing otherwise required effort, awareness, and faith that most people do not possess.</p>
<p>This is exactly the plan. Satan's most effective strategy is not temptation but convenience. He does not need people to choose evil — he needs evil to be the path of least resistance. Unregulated AI makes the beast system the path of least resistance, the default setting of daily life, the thing you have to actively resist rather than actively choose.</p>

The same Spirit that guides the church through moral questions can guide it through technological questions. The same Scripture that addresses the beast system, the mark, and the false prophet addresses the technologies being built to fulfill those prophecies.</p>
