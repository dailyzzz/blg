<h2 id="what-is-rouleaux-formation">What Is Rouleaux Formation?</h2>
<p>Rouleaux formation is a condition in which red blood cells aggregate into stacks — forming structures that look like rolls of coins when viewed under a microscope. The term comes from the French word for "rolls."</p>
<h3 id="normal-red-blood-cell-behavior">Normal Red Blood Cell Behavior</h3>
<p>In healthy blood, red blood cells (erythrocytes) flow as individual, free-moving cells. Each cell is a biconcave disc approximately 6-8 micrometers in diameter, with a negative electrical surface charge (zeta potential) that causes cells to repel each other — maintaining separation and ensuring that each cell moves independently through the bloodstream.</p>
<p>This individual flow is essential for oxygen delivery. Each red blood cell carries hemoglobin molecules that bind oxygen in the lungs and release it in tissues. The biconcave shape maximizes surface area for gas exchange, and the individual flow ensures that cells can pass through the smallest capillaries — vessels so narrow that red blood cells must deform to squeeze through single-file.</p>
<h3 id="what-happens-during-rouleaux-formation">What Happens During Rouleaux Formation</h3>
<p>When the negative surface charge (zeta potential) of red blood cells is disrupted, the cells lose their mutual repulsion and begin to stick together, forming stacks. This aggregation:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Reduces total surface area</strong> available for oxygen exchange</li>
<li><strong>Impairs microcirculation</strong> — stacked cells cannot pass through capillaries individually</li>
<li><strong>Creates relative tissue hypoxia</strong> — reduced oxygen delivery to tissues</li>
<li><strong>Increases blood viscosity</strong> — the blood becomes thicker and flows more slowly</li>
<li><strong>Promotes microclot formation</strong> — aggregated cells create conditions favorable for anomalous clotting</li>
</ul>
<p>Rouleaux formation is associated with inflammatory conditions, certain cancers, diabetes, and — as research now demonstrates — electromagnetic radiation exposure.</p>
