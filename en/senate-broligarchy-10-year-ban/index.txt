<h2 id="the-rise-of-the-broligarchy">The Rise of the Broligarchy</h2>
<h3 id="who-are-the-tech-oligarchs">Who Are the Tech Oligarchs</h3>
<p>The broligarchy refers to the concentrated power of tech billionaires who have accumulated influence that dwarfs traditional political and economic institutions. These individuals — controlling social media platforms, AI development, space programs, electric vehicle markets, payment systems, cloud computing infrastructure, and increasingly, healthcare and military technology — possess the ability to shape human experience at a scale previously reserved for governments and empires.</p>
<p>The concentration of this power in so few hands is historically unprecedented. A single tech billionaire can control global communication (social media), transportation (electric vehicles and ride-sharing), space access (rocket companies), neural interfaces (brain-computer companies), and artificial intelligence — simultaneously. This is not merely wealth; it is the infrastructure of civilization held in private hands.</p>
<p>The term "broligarchy" also captures the cultural dimension of this power concentration. The tech billionaires share a common worldview: techno-utopianism, transhumanism, libertarianism regarding their own operations, and a messianic self-conception that positions them as humanity's saviors. This shared ideology makes them effectively a ruling class, united not by birth or nation but by wealth, technology, and a belief that they know better than the rest of humanity how the future should unfold.</p>
<h3 id="the-10-year-ban-context">The 10-Year Ban Context</h3>
<p>The specific regulatory ban that the Senate voted to address relates to restrictions that have limited certain aspects of tech industry behavior for a decade. The details of the ban matter less than the pattern it reveals: a decade of regulatory protection for citizens, now being removed to accommodate the demands of tech billionaires whose political influence has reached a tipping point.</p>
<p>The broligarchy did not gain its power through innovation alone. It gained power through regulatory capture — the process by which the entities being regulated gain control of the regulatory bodies meant to oversee them. Tech lobbyists have spent billions shaping legislation, funding campaigns, and placing allies in regulatory positions. The removal of a 10-year ban is not the Senate exercising independent judgment — it is the Senate responding to the broligarchy's demands.</p>
<h2 id="political-theater-and-genuine-control">Political Theater and Genuine Control</h2>
<h3 id="the-illusion-of-democratic-accountability">The Illusion of Democratic Accountability</h3>
<p>The Senate vote creates the appearance that democratic institutions are functioning — that elected representatives are holding powerful interests accountable. This appearance is essential for maintaining public trust in a system that has been captured by the interests it claims to regulate.</p>
<p>In reality, the tech broligarchy operates above democratic accountability. When a tech billionaire can influence elections through platform manipulation, shape public opinion through algorithm adjustment, fund candidates through both direct contributions and dark money networks, and threaten economic disruption if regulations displease them, the democratic process becomes a performance staged for public consumption.</p>
<p>The Jesuit model of governance through invisible influence — Tambulini governing "not only to Paris but to China but to all the world" from his chamber — is replicated by tech oligarchs governing from their headquarters. The difference is that the Jesuits required centuries to build their network of influence. Tech billionaires built theirs in decades, accelerated by the exponential power of digital technology.</p>
<h3 id="who-benefits-from-the-vote">Who Benefits From the Vote</h3>
<p>The question to ask about any political action is: who benefits? If the 10-year ban is removed, the tech broligarchy gains expanded freedom to operate without oversight. If the ban is maintained, the broligarchy adapts — finding new methods of achieving the same objectives through different channels. Either way, the underlying power dynamic remains unchanged.</p>
<p>This is the strategic genius of the Jesuit-influenced power structure: outcomes are pre-determined because both sides of the debate serve the same master. John Adams warned: "If ever there were a body of men who merited eternal damnation on earth and in hell, it is this society of Loyolas." The modern broligarchy, educated at elite institutions (many of them Jesuit), connected through the same networks, and pursuing the same transhumanist agenda, merits the same warning.</p>

God scattered the builders because He recognized that unchecked human power, concentrated and unified, leads to rebellion against the Creator.</p>
<p>The broligarchy represents a new Babel — a concentration of power so extreme that a handful of individuals can reshape the human experience according to their vision. They are building a digital tower to heaven, seeking to transcend human limitations through technology rather than through God. And like the original Babel builders, they share a common language — the language of code — and a common ambition: to be as gods.</p>
<p>God's response to Babel was confusion and scattering. His response to the modern Babel will be no different. But between the building and the scattering, immense damage can be done — and is being done — to millions of souls who are being captured by the technological systems these builders create.</p>

<p>Howard Pittman testified that 95% of hell's inhabitants are there for serving "self" — the false god. The broligarchy represents the apotheosis of self-worship: individuals who genuinely believe they are saving humanity through their personal vision, their personal technology, their personal genius. <p>When a tech billionaire says "I'm going to save humanity by colonizing Mars" or "by merging humans with AI" or "by creating digital consciousness," they are making a religious claim — they are positioning themselves as saviors, their technology as salvation, and their vision as gospel. This is the religion of self, and it leads where all self-worship leads: to the broad path of destruction.</p>

The broligarchy operates under a similar inversion: they claim to serve humanity while serving themselves, claim to advance progress while advancing control, claim to liberate while enslaving.</p>
