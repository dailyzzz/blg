<h2 id="why-pharmaceutical-disagreement-is-validation">Why Pharmaceutical Disagreement Is Validation</h2>
<h3 id="when-the-accused-disagrees-with-the-charge">When the Accused Disagrees with the Charge</h3>
<p>RFK Jr.'s central argument is that the pharmaceutical industry corrupts the regulatory process, suppresses evidence of harm, and prioritizes profit over patient safety. Pfizer — which has paid the largest criminal fine in US history ($2.3 billion in 2009 for fraudulent marketing), which withheld adverse event data, which changed clinical trial endpoints, and which lobbied for liability protection — is precisely the entity Kennedy is critiquing.</p>
<p>When Pfizer says Kennedy is wrong, Pfizer is saying "the entity I accuse of corruption says I am wrong about corruption." This is not a scientific refutation — it is a conflict of interest so obvious that its effectiveness depends entirely on the public's inability to recognize it. The industry being accused of wrongdoing denies the wrongdoing. This is not surprising; it is expected. What would be surprising is if Pfizer agreed.</p>
<h3 id="the-mainstream-science-fallacy">The "Mainstream Science" Fallacy</h3>
<p>The appeal to "mainstream science" is itself a fallacy when the mainstream has been captured. When pharmaceutical companies fund the research, own the journals, populate the review boards, control the regulatory agencies, and determine which science gets published and which gets suppressed, "mainstream science" is not an independent authority — it is a controlled narrative.</p>
<p>Karen Kingston's work demonstrates that the evidence RFK Jr. cites is not fringe — it is drawn from the pharmaceutical companies' own filings, patent applications, regulatory submissions, and internal communications. The "mainstream" that disagrees is the same mainstream that these documents expose as compromised.</p>
<h2 id="the-spiritual-significance-of-kennedys-position">The Spiritual Significance of Kennedy's Position</h2>
<h3 id="the-prophet-and-the-establishment">The Prophet and the Establishment</h3>
<p>Throughout biblical history, prophets stood against established religious and political authority — and established authority uniformly rejected them. Jeremiah was imprisoned. Isaiah was (traditionally) sawn in two. John the Baptist was beheaded. Jesus was crucified. In every case, the establishment's rejection was cited as evidence of the prophet's error. In every case, history vindicated the prophet.</p>
<p>Kennedy occupies a prophetic position — not as a religious figure but as one who speaks uncomfortable truth to entrenched power. The pharmaceutical establishment's rejection of his message follows the biblical pattern precisely: the authorities he challenges use their institutional power to discredit him, and the public is expected to side with authority over evidence.</p>
<p>Pittman's warning that 97.5% walk the broad path to destruction is directly relevant. The broad path is always the path endorsed by institutional authority. Kennedy's critics may not realize it, but their opposition is evidence of his proximity to truth — because truth, in a corrupted system, is always opposed by those who benefit from corruption.</p>
